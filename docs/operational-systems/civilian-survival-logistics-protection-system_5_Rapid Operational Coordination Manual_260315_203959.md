Rapid Operational Coordination Manual
Operational Manual

Date: March 15, 2026 (KST)

Article 1. Purpose
① The purpose of this manual is as follows.
• enabling rapid registration and approval of neutral-certified vessels
• supporting safe passage of civilian vessels through high-risk maritime areas
• facilitating real-time navigation information sharing
• establishing incident response and reporting procedures

Article 2. Operational Principles
① Operations shall follow the following principles.
• speed
• transparency
• safety
• international cooperation
② Vessel certification and transit procedures should be processed in real time or within the shortest possible operational timeframe whenever feasible.

Article 3. Vessel Registration Procedure
Vessel registration shall consist of the following stages.
① Stage 1: Online Registration
• Vessel operators shall submit the following information.
• vessel IMO number
• vessel name
• flag state
• shipping company
• cargo type
• port of departure
• destination port
• intended route
② Stage 2: Automated Verification
• The operational system may verify the following information.
• IMO registry data
• vessel tracking systems
• submitted cargo information
Manifest–Draft Verification Algorithm
To reduce the possibility of concealed cargo, the following automated verification may be conducted.
• cargo manifest analysis
• vessel draft data verification
• estimated cargo weight calculation
• comparison with actual draft data
If inconsistencies are detected, additional verification procedures may be initiated.
③ Stage 3: Certification Issuance
• After verification is completed, the vessel may receive the following certifications.
• Neutral Vessel ID
• neutral transit certification ID
• digital certification document
• QR code certification
• Certification shall remain valid for a limited period.
Cargo manifest information and vessel draft data may be compared through automated verification systems.

Article 4. Transit Authorization
① Certified vessels may transit through designated maritime routes within high-risk areas.
② Operational authorities may take the following measures when necessary.
• adjustment of transit timing
• route guidance
• issuance of navigation alerts

Article 5. Real-Time Navigation Information
① Certified vessels shall maintain the following operational information.
• active AIS navigation system
• position information sharing
• navigation status updates
② Such information may be used by operational authorities for transit coordination and safety management.

Article 6. Communication Channels
Communication between vessels and operational authorities may utilize the following channels.
• maritime radio communication
• digital communication systems
• emergency contact channels
① Neutral Transit Frequency
• Neutral-certified vessels may use a Neutral Transit Frequency.
• This frequency may serve the following purposes.
• identification of neutral vessels
• transit coordination communication
• emergency response communication
② Electronic Safe Signal
• Neutral-certified vessels may transmit an Electronic Safe Signal.
• This signal may provide the following functions.
• identification of neutral-certified vessels
• visibility within radar and navigation systems
• reduction of accidental attack or misidentification by belligerent parties
• The Electronic Safe Signal may be integrated with the neutral certification system.
③ The signal is intended to technically distinguish civilian vessels from military operations.

Article 7. High-Risk Area Transit Procedures
① When transiting high-risk maritime areas, vessels shall comply with the following procedures.
• maintaining certification status
• following designated navigation corridors
• maintaining communication channels
• complying with operational authority guidance

Article 8. Emergency Response
① When the following situations occur, vessels may report immediately.
• attack or threat
• attempted seizure
• obstruction of navigation
• other emergency situations
② Reports may include the following information.
• time of incident
• location of incident
• description of the situation
• damage or impact assessment

Article 9. Incident Response Procedures
① When incidents occur, operational authorities may take the following actions.
• issuing navigation warnings
• notifying nearby vessels
• informing relevant institutions
• registering the incident record

Article 10. Information Recording
① All incident and navigation-related information may be recorded.
② Records may include the following information.
• vessel information
• position data
• incident reports
• response actions taken

Article 11. Cooperation Structure
① Operation of this manual may be based on cooperation among the following actors.
• international maritime organizations
• neutral declaration states
• shipping and logistics institutions
• maritime safety organizations

Article 12. Operational Continuity
① This manual may be applied under the following circumstances.
• armed conflict situations
• maritime security tensions
• navigation within high-risk maritime areas

Core Principle
Safe transit of civilian vessels is maintained through international cooperation.
