Civilian Survival Logistics Protection Framework
Concept Framework

Submitted by: Jungyun Kwon, Citizen of the Republic of Korea and Global Citizen
Date: March 15, 2026 (KST)

Article 1. Background
① Modern armed conflicts, even when they begin in a limited geographic area, can rapidly affect the global economy and critical infrastructure necessary for human survival.
• In particular, when conflicts occur in global maritime chokepoints, international logistics systems may be immediately affected.
• International maritime transportation
• Food supply
• Energy supply
• Medical supply transportation
• Global supply chains
② These impacts may cause severe economic and humanitarian consequences not only for the parties directly involved in the conflict but also for countries and civilian populations not directly connected to the conflict.
③ Therefore, the international community may require an institutional framework capable of protecting civilian logistics systems essential for human survival even during armed conflict.

Article 2. Purpose
① The purpose of this framework is as follows.
• Maintaining the stability of global food and energy supply
• Minimizing humanitarian harm to civilian populations
• Reducing pressure for military intervention by third-party states
• Maintaining international logistics flows during conflict
• Preserving diplomatic and negotiation space

Article 3. Fundamental Principles
① Track Separation Principle
The following domains shall be structurally separated.
• Diplomatic track
• Political track
• Military track
• Civilian survival logistics track
② Protection of Civilian Survival Infrastructure
The following logistics are considered protected.
• Food
• Energy
• Medicine
• Essential civilian goods
• General commercial cargo
The following items are excluded from protection.
• Weapons
• Ammunition
• Military supplies
• Troop transport
• Military operational equipment
③ Neutral Participation Principle
Participation by neutral declaration states in the protection of civilian logistics shall not be considered military participation in the conflict.
④ Post-Decision Principle on Military Intervention
Military intervention shall not be assumed immediately upon the outbreak of a conflict.
The decision may follow:
• international legal fact-finding
• international investigation or institutional review
• consultation with allied states
• constitutional and domestic legal procedures of each state
Each state retains sovereign authority to determine its final position.

Article 4. International Legal Basis
① This framework is designed to operate in harmony with the following principles of international law.
• international maritime navigation principles
• rights of neutral states
• international humanitarian law principles
• international dispute resolution principles
② Particular consideration is given to consistency with the following international norms.
• international maritime regulations
• protection of civilians during armed conflict
• customary practices related to neutral state status
• international dispute resolution procedures

Article 5. Sovereign Participation Principle
① Participation in this framework is based on the sovereign decision of each state.
② This framework does not modify or invalidate any existing military alliances, treaties, or defense obligations of participating states.
③ Each state may independently determine whether or not to engage in military intervention regardless of participation in this framework.

Article 6. Definitions
① Belligerent State
A state that directly participates in armed hostilities or provides substantial cooperation in military operations.
② Non-Belligerent State
A state that does not participate militarily in the conflict.
③ Neutral Declaration State
A state that formally declares the following principles.
• no military participation on behalf of any belligerent party
• no political endorsement of any belligerent party
• no provision of military bases or territorial use
• participation in negotiation, mediation, or humanitarian activities
These activities shall not be considered military or political alignment.
• responsibility determination for the conflict follows international legal procedures
• neutral declaration states serve as key participants in the civilian survival logistics protection system

Article 7. Obligations of Neutral Declaration States
① Prohibition of Military Participation
• No military participation on behalf of any belligerent party.
② Prohibition of State-Level Military Assistance
The following actions shall not be provided.
• troop deployment
• support for military operations
• military intelligence support
• provision of weapons without commercial basis
• military training
③ Exception for Lawful Commercial Trade
Lawful commercial trade may be permitted under the following conditions.
• based on civilian commercial contracts
• compliant with international export control regulations
• not considered state-level military intervention
④ International Cargo Verification Mechanism
To ensure neutrality of commercial transactions, an International Cargo Verification Mechanism may be utilized.
The mechanism may perform the following functions.
• verification of declared cargo information
• verification of transportation routes
• identification of prohibited items
• verification of civilian survival logistics cargo
Cargo that passes verification procedures may be considered neutral civilian cargo.
⑤ Maintenance of Political Neutrality
• No political endorsement of any belligerent party.
⑥ Prohibition of Territorial or Military Base Use
The following shall not be provided for military operations of belligerent states.
• military bases
• territory
• territorial waters
• airspace
⑦ Restrictions on Port and Airport Use
The following uses shall not be permitted.
• offensive military operations
• military logistics resupply
• troop movement
⑧ Prevention of Territorial Use for Hostilities
States shall take reasonable measures to prevent their territory from being used as a base for military operations.

Article 8. Civilian Transit Protection System
① Vessels transporting civilian survival logistics may receive protection through a neutral certification system.
② The certification system may include the following components.
• vessel registration
• cargo verification
• navigation information sharing
• procedures for transit through risk areas
• digital certification

Article 9. International Investigation in Case of Violations
① If attacks occur against civilian survival logistics or neutral-certified vessels, an international investigation procedure may be initiated.
② The investigation may include the following elements.
• incident registration
• fact-finding investigation
• responsibility assessment procedures
• review of compensation for damages

Article 10. Damage Recording and International Responsibility
① When attacks or damage occur against civilian survival logistics, the incident may be recorded in an international registry.
② These records may be used for the following purposes.
• international investigations
• dispute resolution procedures
• responsibility determination
• compensation assessment

Article 11. International Cooperation Structure
① This framework may operate through cooperation among the following actors.
• international maritime organizations
• neutral-oriented states
• shipping and logistics institutions
• mediation support states
② The detailed operational structure may be defined in a separate document.

Article 12. Expected Outcomes
① This framework aims to achieve the following outcomes.
• stabilization of global food and energy supply
• reduction of humanitarian harm to civilian populations
• reduction of military intervention pressure on third-party states
• preservation of diplomatic negotiation space
• stability of international maritime logistics

Guiding Principle
War is conducted by states, but the logistics necessary for human survival must not become targets of war.
