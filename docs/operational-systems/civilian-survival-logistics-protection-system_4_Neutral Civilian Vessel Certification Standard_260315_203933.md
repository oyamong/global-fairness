Neutral Civilian Vessel Certification Standard
Certification Standard

Submitted by: Jungyun Kwon, Citizen of the Republic of Korea and Global Citizen
Date: March 15, 2026 (KST)

Article 1. Purpose
① The purpose of this standard is as follows.
• defining neutral transit certification criteria for civilian vessels
• identifying vessels eligible for protection
• protecting civilian survival logistics during conflict situations
• supporting safe maritime transit

Article 2. Scope of Application
① This standard applies to the following categories of vessels.
• civilian merchant vessels
• food transport vessels
• energy transport vessels
• medical supply vessels
• vessels transporting essential civilian goods
• general commercial cargo vessels

Article 3. Certification Eligibility
① In order to receive neutral vessel certification, the following conditions must be met.
• the vessel must be a civilian vessel
• the vessel must transport protected cargo
• vessel registration procedures must be completed
• the operator must consent to navigation data sharing

Article 4. Vessel Registration Information
① When applying for vessel certification, the following information shall be submitted.
• vessel IMO number
• vessel name
• flag state
• shipping company
• vessel type
• cargo type
• port of departure
• destination port
• intended route

Article 5. Cargo Verification
① Cargo eligible for certification must fall into the following categories.
• food
• energy
• medicine
• essential civilian goods
• general commercial cargo
② The following cargo is excluded from certification.
• weapons
• ammunition
• military supplies
• troop transport equipment
• military operational equipment

Article 6. Certification Issuance
① After completion of the registration procedure, a neutral transit certification may be issued to the vessel.
② Certification may consist of the following elements.
• Neutral Vessel ID
• digital certification document
• QR code certification
③ Multi-Signature Certification
Certification issuance may follow a multi-signature approval structure involving multiple institutions.
Purpose
• preventing unilateral certification by a single state
• ensuring credibility of the certification system
• establishing shared international responsibility
Examples of approving authorities
• certification authorities of neutral declaration states
• international maritime organizations
• neutral operational authorities
Certification may be issued only after approval by at least two participating authorities.

Article 7. Certification Validity
① Neutral vessel certification shall remain valid for a limited period.
② Certification may be renewed in the following situations.
• route change
• cargo modification
• extended voyage duration

Article 8. Certification Identification
① Certified vessels shall display or maintain the following identification elements.
• Neutral Vessel ID
• active AIS navigation system
• navigation information sharing capability

Article 9. Certification Revocation and Preventive Suspension
① Certification may be revoked in the following situations.
• submission of false information
• transportation of prohibited cargo
• violation of navigation rules
• involvement in military activities
② Preventive Suspension
Certification may be temporarily suspended under the following circumstances.
• suspicion that vessel communication data is being used for military purposes
• indications of support for military operations
• suspected cargo alteration or seal tampering
• abnormal navigation data patterns
Such measures shall be considered preventive safety actions.

Article 10. Information Sharing
① Certification-related information may be shared for the following purposes.
• navigation safety support
• response to risk situations
• incident recording
② Information sharing shall comply with applicable laws and international regulations.

Article 11. Record Keeping
① Vessel certification information may be maintained as official records.
② Records may include the following information.
• vessel registration information
• certification issuance records
• certification renewal records
• certification revocation records

Article 12. Cooperation
① This certification system may operate through cooperation among the following entities.
• international maritime organizations
• neutral declaration states
• shipping and logistics institutions

Article 13. Technical Infrastructure
The vessel certification system may include the following technological components.
① Blockchain Cargo Registry
• cargo information may be recorded in a blockchain-based cargo registry system
Purpose
• preventing manipulation of cargo information
• maintaining transparent transportation records
• enabling international verification
② Neutral Vessel ID
• certified vessels may receive a Neutral Vessel ID
Functions
• vessel identification
• verification of certification status
• management of transit records
③ Global Maritime Corridor Map
• neutral-certified vessels may navigate using routes defined by a Global Maritime Corridor Map
This system may provide
• safe transit corridor identification
• high-risk maritime area information
• support for transit timing coordination

Core Principle
Civilian vessels may be protected through neutral certification.
