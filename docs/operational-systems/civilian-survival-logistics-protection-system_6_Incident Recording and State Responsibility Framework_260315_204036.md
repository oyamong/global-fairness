Incident Recording and State Responsibility Framework
Accountability Framework

Submitted by: Jungyun Kwon, Citizen of the Republic of Korea and Global Citizen
Date: March 15, 2026 (KST)

Article 1. Purpose
① The purpose of this framework is as follows.
• internationally recording attacks or threats against civilian survival logistics
• preserving factual information related to incidents
• supporting international investigation and dispute resolution procedures
• assisting responsibility assessment and compensation processes

Article 2. Scope of Application
① This framework applies to the following situations.
• attacks against neutral-certified vessels
• threats against neutral-certified vessels
• attempted seizure of vessels
• obstruction of navigation
• disruption of civilian survival logistics transport
• other maritime safety threats

Article 3. Incident Reporting
① When the following situations occur, vessels or relevant institutions shall report the incident.
• armed attack
• armed threat
• navigation blockade
• attempted seizure
• communication threat
• other safety threats

Article 4. Incident Reporting Information
① Incident reports should include the following information whenever possible.
• vessel IMO number
• vessel name
• flag state
• location of the incident
• time of the incident
• cargo type
• type of incident
• damage description
• relevant communication records

Article 5. Incident Recording
① Reported incidents may be registered as international incident records.
② Records may include the following information.
• incident report
• location data
• vessel information
• navigation data
• damage description
③ Blockchain Incident Registry
• Incident records may be stored in a distributed ledger (blockchain-based) incident registry system.
The system may provide the following functions.
• prevention of record manipulation
• simultaneous storage across multiple national servers
• prevention of record deletion or alteration
• preservation of evidence for international investigations
• When records are created, data may be simultaneously stored on multiple neutral-state servers.

Article 6. Information Preservation
① Incident-related information may be preserved for the following purposes.
• international investigation
• dispute resolution procedures
• fact verification
• responsibility determination
② Information preservation shall comply with applicable international norms and legal frameworks.

Article 7. International Cooperation
① Incident records may be used in the following procedures.
• international investigation
• mediation processes
• international dispute resolution procedures
② Relevant institutions may cooperate when necessary.

Article 8. Responsibility Principle
① When attacks or damage occur against civilian survival logistics, such acts may be assessed under principles of state responsibility under international law.
② Responsibility assessment procedures may include the following.
• international investigation
• fact-finding procedures
• dispute resolution mechanisms

Article 9. Compensation for Damage
① When damage occurs, the following elements may be considered.
• vessel damage
• cargo damage
• losses resulting from navigation obstruction
• recovery and repair costs
② Decision-Maker Responsibility Review
• In assessing compensation and responsibility, the following elements may be examined.
• state responsibility
• authorization structures
• decision-making processes
• Where serious violations are confirmed, international review procedures concerning responsible decision-makers may be initiated.
Attacks against civilian survival logistics may therefore become subject to both state responsibility and review of individual decision-maker accountability.

Article 10. Automatic International Investigation Trigger
① The following incidents may trigger automatic international investigation procedures.
• attacks on neutral-certified vessels
• seizure of civilian survival logistics vessels
• blockade of civilian maritime transit
• damage to civilian vessels
② Automatic investigation procedures may include the following steps.
• automatic incident registration
• international investigation request
• initiation of fact-finding procedures

Article 11. Cooperation
① Operation of this framework may be based on cooperation among the following actors.
• international maritime organizations
• neutral declaration states
• shipping and logistics institutions
• relevant international institutions

Article 12. Use of Records
① Incident records may be used for the following purposes.
• improvement of maritime safety
• prevention of future conflicts
• policy and regulatory development

Core Principle
Attacks against civilian survival logistics must be recorded and responsibility may be examined.
