War-Linked Asset Management & Maritime Logistics Stabilization Package in High-Risk Geopolitical Instability (WARM-P)

The General Assembly,
Recalling the purposes and principles of the Charter of the United Nations,
Reaffirming that the right to life is a fundamental principle of international law and forms a foundation of international peace and security,
Recognizing that large-scale violence, civilian harm, and systemic instability may be facilitated directly or indirectly by financial flows and opaque asset structures,
Considering that military tensions and armed conflict may cause severe harm to civilians and may generate major disruption to global maritime transport, energy supply chains, and the distribution of essential goods—including food and medicines—as well as goods and services necessary for ordinary livelihoods,
Emphasizing that the continuity of international maritime logistics constitutes a core global public good for the protection of the right to life and for international peace,
Recalling that all Member States bear a shared responsibility for the maintenance of international peace and security under the Charter of the United Nations,
Expressing concern that abnormal financial gains and asset concealment structures exploiting war or geopolitical crises may undermine international public order and market fairness,
Emphasizing the need to strengthen financial transparency and accountability while respecting State sovereignty and due process under international law,
Noting that financial opacity may affect maritime insurance premiums, credit ratings, and global financial risk assessments,
Noting that abnormal windfall gains arising from high-risk geopolitical instability may reasonably be linked to resources necessary for public stability and recovery,
Recognizing that the present package is not a sanctions regime but a transparency-based risk management framework, and that market, insurance, and supervisory risk costs arising from persistent opacity or refusal to cooperate may be attributable to relevant actors,
Recognizing further that the present package is continuous with existing Risk-Based Approach (RBA), National Risk Assessment (NRA) frameworks, beneficial ownership transparency measures, and global public goods protection efforts, and is intended to complement and strengthen their effective implementation,

Adopts the following:

Article 1 (Establishment)
1. Approves the establishment of the War-Linked Asset Management & Maritime Logistics Stabilization Package (WARM-P) to promote the tracing, provisional freezing, confiscation, and public-interest redistribution of assets reasonably assessed to be linked to high-risk geopolitical instability.
2. Decides that the present mechanism shall operate as a preventive and protective financial governance framework aimed at safeguarding the right to life and strengthening international public safety, and shall not constitute a punitive regime.

Article 2 (Scope of Application)
1. Decides that this package shall apply to financial flows and asset structures reasonably assessed to be linked to high-risk geopolitical instability, defined as situations involving armed conflict, escalating military tension, maritime or aviation security threats, or severe disruption of essential supply chains creating substantial risks to public safety and the protection of life.
2. Notes that the scope may include:(a) Financing activities materially contributing to instability or deriving significant gains therefrom;(b) Direct or indirect financial structures linked to arms procurement or military contracts;(c) Abnormal financial positions established within a reasonably defined period prior to escalation;(d) Abnormal profits derived from exploiting excessive maritime insurance, freight, or energy price movements(e) Beneficial ownership concealment structures intended to evade sanctions or obscure accountability.
3. Emphasizes that the present resolution does not designate any State as a sanctions target and applies an actor-based accountability framework centered on individuals, organizations, and beneficial owners.

Article 3 (Recommendation of Emergency Measures)
1. Recommends that Member States, in accordance with domestic law, consider provisional freezes of suspect assets.
2. Encourages Member States to utilize Financial Intelligence Units (FIUs) to pursue beneficial-ownership-based tracing of relevant fund flows.
3. Recommends that confiscation or forfeiture procedures be initiated within a reasonable period, typically 90 days, where appropriate.
4. Emphasizes that these recommendations are non-binding and that Member States retain autonomous judgment consistent with domestic law and essential security interests.
5. Clarifies that this resolution does not render any international organization a legal guarantor or jointly responsible entity for measures taken by Member States.

Article 4 (Allocation Structure)
1. Decides that recovered assets may be allocated to:(a) Victim Recovery Window, including medical, housing, livelihood, refugee protection, and reconstruction support;(b) Maritime Logistics Stabilization Window, including support for essential food and medicine transport, vulnerable country energy stabilization, maritime route safety, and insurance premium stabilization measures.
2. Emphasizes that allocation shall be conducted in accordance with transparency, non-discrimination, proportionality, and due process principles.
3. Decides that recovered assets shall be distributed with regard to a reasonable linkage between the origin of the gains and international public costs.

Article 5 (Strengthening Financial Transparency)
1. Calls upon Member States to strengthen domestic measures enhancing beneficial ownership transparency.
2. Encourages disclosure regarding abnormal financial positions established immediately prior to escalation events.
3. Calls upon Member States to strengthen oversight of professional service providers facilitating concealment or evasion.
4. Encourages Member States to consider institutional arrangements linking abnormal windfall gains to public recovery costs.
5. Notes that vulnerabilities linked to high-risk geopolitical instability may be reflected within National Risk Assessments (NRA) and Risk-Based Approach (RBA) supervisory priorities consistent with FATF principles.

Article 6 (Cooperation of Financial Institutions)
1. Invites financial institutions to cooperate with the present package and notes that cooperating institutions may be recognized in international reporting.
2. Encourages Member States to consider supervisory efficiency measures for cooperating institutions.
3. Encourages risk-based classification of non-cooperating institutions where appropriate.
4. Notes that financial opacity associated with non-cooperation may be considered within risk assessment, insurance premium setting, and supervisory processes.
5. Decides that implementation levels may be transparently graded and periodically reassessed.

Article 7 (Procedural Safeguards)
1. Decides that all measures under this package shall comply with due process.
2. Affirms that affected parties shall receive notice, an opportunity to be heard, and independent review.
3. Notes that Member States may consider applicable security exception clauses under international investment agreements.

Article 8 (Transparency and Audit)
1. Decides that distribution of recovered assets shall be transparently recorded.
2. Requests independent audits and periodic reporting.
3. Encourages appropriate civil society access to information.

Article 9 (Follow-Up)
1. Requests the Secretary-General to report within one year on implementation.
2. Invites Member States to assess effectiveness and propose improvements.
3. Requests the Secretary-General to consider complementary linkages with existing global early-warning and supply-chain stabilization mechanisms, including USB GLOBAL.
4. Requests further consultation with relevant international financial institutions regarding governance and transparency assessment references.
5. Decides that risk indicator generation, asset determination, and allocation/audit functions shall remain institutionally separated and independently supervised.

Final Provisions
The present resolution shall not infringe upon State sovereignty and shall be implemented within the framework of international law and domestic legal systems. It shall not replace or limit the authority of the United Nations Security Council and shall function as a complementary transparency and risk-management framework.
