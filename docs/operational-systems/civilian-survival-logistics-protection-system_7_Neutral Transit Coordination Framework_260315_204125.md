Neutral Transit Coordination Framework
Coordination Framework

Submitted by: Jungyun Kwon, Citizen of the Republic of Korea and Global Citizen
Date: March 15, 2026 (KST)

Article 1. Purpose
① The purpose of this framework is as follows.
• defining the operational structure of the civilian survival logistics protection system
• establishing the governance structure for the neutral transit certification system
• enabling international cooperation for vessel transit procedures
• connecting incident recording and response mechanisms

Article 2. Operational Principles
① Operations shall be based on the following principles.
• neutrality
• transparency
• speed
• international cooperation
② The operational structure shall not be monopolized or controlled by a single state and may function through the participation of multiple cooperating institutions and states.

Article 3. Operational Structure
① The operational structure may consist of the following components.
1. Technical Standards Authority
Role
• management of vessel certification standards
• provision of maritime safety technical standards
• support for certification system standardization
Example
• International Maritime Organization or other international maritime institutions
2. Certification Management Authority
Role
• management of vessel registration
• management of certification issuance
• maintenance of certification data
This role may be performed by maritime-oriented states or designated institutions.
3. Regional Operational Authorities
Role
• coordination of transit through high-risk maritime areas
• provision of navigation safety information
• support for vessel communications
Regional mediator states or relevant institutions may participate in this role.
4. Incident Recording and Responsibility Authority
Role
• preservation of incident records
• management of accident data
• support for international investigations
Neutral-oriented states or institutions may participate in this role.
5. Mediation and Diplomatic Support Authority
Role
• diplomatic cooperation support
• conflict situation coordination
• maintenance of negotiation channels
States or institutions with mediation experience may participate in this role.

Article 4. Global Neutral Maritime Coordination Network
① For the operation of this framework, a Global Neutral Maritime Coordination Network may be established.
② The network may perform the following functions.
• operation of the neutral transit system
• management of vessel certification networks
• global transit data management
• integration with incident recording systems
③ The network structure may include the following components.
• neutral operational centers
• regional transit coordination centers
• digital certification management systems

Article 5. Operational Cooperation
① The operational structure may be formed through cooperation among the following actors.
• neutral declaration states
• international maritime organizations
• shipping and logistics institutions
• maritime safety institutions
② Participating institutions may cooperate according to their respective roles.

Article 6. Information Sharing
① The following information may be shared among operational authorities.
• vessel certification information
• navigation data
• incident reports
• high-risk maritime area information
② Secure Data Sharing
• Civilian vessel navigation data may be transmitted through encrypted communication systems.
• Access to such data may be restricted to neutral operational authorities.
③ Restricted Information Disclosure
• Only limited information may be provided to belligerent parties or external institutions.
• neutral certification status
• transit authorization status
• Vessel location and cargo information may be restricted in order to prevent military misuse.

Article 7. Operational Document Integration
① This framework shall operate in coordination with the following documents.
• Civilian Survival Logistics Protection Framework
• Emergency Neutral Civilian Transit Declaration
• Civilian Maritime Transit Protection Protocol
• Neutral Civilian Vessel Certification Standard
• Rapid Operational Coordination Manual
• Incident Recording and State Responsibility Framework
② These documents operate as complementary components of a unified system.

Article 8. Strategic Maritime Corridors
① This framework may apply to the following Strategic Maritime Corridors.
• international maritime chokepoints
• major energy transportation routes
• critical global supply chain routes
② Operational authorities may perform the following functions.
• transit safety management
• provision of high-risk maritime area information
• support for civilian maritime transit protection

Article 9. Operational Continuity
① This framework may be applied under the following circumstances.
• armed conflict situations
• maritime security tensions
• navigation through high-risk maritime areas
② Operational structures may be adjusted through consultation among participating institutions when necessary.

Core Principle
The safe movement of civilian survival logistics shall be maintained through neutral international cooperation.
