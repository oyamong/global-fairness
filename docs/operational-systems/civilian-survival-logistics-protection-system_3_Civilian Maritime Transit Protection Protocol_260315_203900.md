Civilian Maritime Transit Protection Protocol
Operational Protocol

Submitted by: Jungyun Kwon, Citizen of the Republic of Korea and Global Citizen
Date: March 15, 2026 (KST)

Article 1. Purpose
① The purpose of this protocol is as follows.
• ensuring the safe movement of civilian survival logistics even during armed conflict
• maintaining international maritime logistics flows
• supporting safe transit for neutral-certified vessels
• separating armed conflict from civilian commercial activities

Article 2. Scope of Application
① This protocol applies to the following categories of vessels.
• civilian merchant vessels
• food transport vessels
• energy transport vessels
• medical supply vessels
• vessels transporting essential civilian goods
• general commercial cargo vessels

Article 3. Protected Cargo
① The following cargo shall be considered protected.
• food
• energy
• medicine
• essential civilian goods
• general commercial cargo
② The following cargo is excluded from protection.
• weapons
• ammunition
• military supplies
• troop transport equipment
• military operational equipment

Article 4. Neutral Vessel Certification
① Civilian vessels may receive protection through neutral transit certification.
② Certification eligibility requires the following conditions.
• the vessel must be a civilian vessel
• the vessel must transport protected cargo
• vessel registration procedures must be completed
• the operator must consent to navigation data sharing
③ Digital Cargo Sealing
Digital cargo sealing may include the following functions.
• creation of cargo verification records
• issuance of digital sealing codes
• tracking of transportation routes
• verification of cargo integrity

Article 5. Vessel Registration
① Prior to transit, vessels shall register the following information.
• vessel IMO number
• vessel name
• flag state
• shipping company
• cargo type
• port of departure
• destination port
• intended route

Article 6. Digital Certification
① Upon completion of registration, a neutral transit certification shall be issued to the vessel.
② Certification may include the following formats.
• digital certification ID
• QR code certification
• electronic certification document
③ Certification validity shall be maintained for a limited period.

Article 7. Navigation Rules
① Neutral-certified vessels shall comply with the following rules.
• the AIS navigation system must remain active at all times
• designated transit corridors shall be used
• certification identification shall be displayed on the vessel
• designated communication channels shall be maintained

Article 8. Navigation Information Sharing
① Vessels shall share the following information with the operational authority.
• real-time vessel position
• navigation status
• estimated time of arrival
② Such information may be used to ensure navigation safety.

Article 9. Data Security Protocol
① Vessel location and navigation data may be transmitted to neutral operational authorities through encrypted communication systems.
② Belligerent parties may receive only limited information.
• neutral certification status
• transit approval status
③ Detailed vessel position data and cargo information may be restricted to prevent military misuse.

Article 10. Transit Coordination
① Operational authorities may perform the following functions.
• adjusting transit timing through high-risk areas
• coordinating vessel transit order
• providing navigation safety advisories
② Asymmetric Transit Window
In certain high-risk maritime areas, asymmetric transit windows may be implemented.
• separating civilian vessel movement from military operations in time
• reducing the risk of misidentification or accidental attack
• improving overall transit safety
Example operational approach
• allowing neutral-certified vessels to transit during designated time windows
• separating civilian transit periods from military operational periods
• maintaining secure maritime corridors

Article 11. Incident Reporting
① When the following situations occur, vessels shall report immediately.
• attack
• threat
• attempted seizure
• obstruction of navigation
• other safety threats
② Reports may include the following information.
• time of incident
• location
• description of the situation

Article 12. Damage Recording
① When incidents occur, the following information may be recorded.
• vessel IMO number
• vessel position
• time of incident
• cargo information
• description of damage
② Such records may be used in international investigation or dispute resolution procedures.

Article 13. Cooperation
① This protocol may operate through cooperation among the following actors.
• international maritime organizations
• neutral declaration states
• shipping and logistics institutions

Article 14. Operational Structure
① Operation of this protocol may rely on the following cooperative structures.
• technical standards authority
• certification management authority
• regional operational authorities
• mediation support institutions
② Detailed operational structures may be defined in a separate framework document.

Article 15. Governing Principles
① This protocol operates according to the following principles.
• protection of civilian survival logistics
• separation of armed conflict from commercial activities
• protection of neutral-certified vessels
• international cooperative governance

Core Principle
Civilian survival logistics must not become targets of war.
