Civilian Survival Logistics Protection System
International Framework Package
Protecting Civilian Survival Logistics During Armed Conflict

Submitted by: Jungyun Kwon, Citizen of the Republic of Korea and Global Citizen
Date: March 15, 2026 (KST)

Package Overview
This package proposes an international cooperative framework designed to support the protection of civilian survival logistics during armed conflict and to reduce disruptions to global supply chains.

The proposed system focuses on:
• maintaining the safe movement of food, energy, medicine, and essential civilian goods
• enabling neutral maritime transit during periods of armed conflict
• reducing escalation pressures on third-party states
• supporting stability of global trade and humanitarian supply systems
The framework is designed to operate in alignment with existing principles of international law, international humanitarian law, and maritime safety cooperation.

Core Principle
War is conducted by states, but the logistics necessary for human survival must not become targets of war.

Document Structure
The package consists of seven complementary documents, each addressing a specific operational layer of the proposed system.

1. Civilian Survival Logistics Protection Framework
Concept Framework
Defines the core principles and legal alignment for protecting civilian survival logistics during armed conflict.
Key elements:
• separation between military conflict and civilian logistics flows
• protection of global supply chains
• neutral participation principles
• international cooperation mechanisms

2. Emergency Neutral Civilian Transit Declaration
Joint Emergency Declaration
Provides a diplomatic mechanism allowing states to support civilian logistics protection without becoming parties to the conflict.
Key elements:
• conflict containment principles
• non-belligerent participation
• political neutrality framework
• maritime stability cooperation

3. Civilian Maritime Transit Protection Protocol
Operational Protocol
Defines operational rules for protected civilian maritime transit during conflict situations.
Key elements:
• neutral vessel certification
• protected cargo categories
• navigation rules for conflict-affected waters
• asymmetric transit windows
• encrypted operational data sharing

4. Neutral Civilian Vessel Certification Standard
Certification Standard
Defines technical and administrative standards for neutral civilian vessel certification.
Key elements:
• vessel registration procedures
• cargo verification mechanisms
• multi-institution certification approval
• Neutral Vessel ID system
• blockchain-supported cargo registry

5. Rapid Operational Coordination Manual
Operational Manual
Provides operational procedures for real-time maritime transit coordination.
Key elements:
• rapid vessel registration
• automated cargo verification
• real-time navigation coordination
• neutral transit communication systems
• emergency response procedures

6. Incident Recording and State Responsibility Framework
Accountability Framework
Defines procedures for international documentation of incidents involving civilian survival logistics.
Key elements:
• incident reporting system
• distributed incident registry
• automatic investigation triggers
• responsibility assessment procedures

7. Neutral Transit Coordination Framework
Governance and Coordination Framework
Defines the governance structure required to operate the system.
Key elements:
• neutral maritime coordination network
• certification management bodies
• regional coordination centers
• incident reporting integration
• multi-state participation structure

System Architecture
The seven documents form a layered architecture:
Concept Framework
↓
Emergency Declaration
↓
Transit Protection Protocol
↓
Vessel Certification Standard
↓
Operational Coordination Manual
↓
Incident Accountability Framework
↓
Neutral Transit Coordination Framework

Together they establish a neutral international maritime protection mechanism for civilian survival logistics.

Purpose of the Framework
The objective of the proposed system is to help ensure that during armed conflict:
• essential global supply chains remain functional
• civilian populations are protected from supply disruptions
• maritime trade continues safely where possible
• pressures for third-party military escalation are reduced
• diplomatic space for conflict resolution remains available

Guiding Principle
Civilian survival logistics should remain protected even in situations of armed conflict.
