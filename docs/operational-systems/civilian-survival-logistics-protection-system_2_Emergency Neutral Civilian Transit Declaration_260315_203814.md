Emergency Neutral Civilian Transit Declaration
Joint Emergency Declaration

Submitted by: Jungyun Kwon, Citizen of the Republic of Korea and Global Citizen
Date: March 15, 2026 (KST)

Preamble
① We recognize that ongoing or potential armed conflicts may severely disrupt global flows of food, energy, medicine, and civilian commercial logistics.
② We are concerned that even when conflicts originate in limited geographic areas, their consequences may extend to civilian populations across the world.
③ We affirm the principle that logistics essential for human survival must not become targets of military confrontation.
④ Accordingly, we jointly declare the following in order to protect civilian survival logistics and to maintain stability in international maritime transit.

Article 1. Purpose
① The purpose of this declaration is as follows.
• protect civilian survival logistics
• maintain international logistics stability during armed conflict
• reduce pressure for military intervention by third-party states
• preserve diplomatic space for negotiation and conflict resolution

Article 2. Track Separation Principle
① Signatory states shall structurally separate the following domains.
• diplomatic track
• political track
• military track
• civilian survival logistics track
② Civilian survival logistics shall be regarded as a protected domain separate from military operations.

Article 3. Protection of Civilian Survival Logistics
① Signatory states confirm that the following logistics constitute internationally protected civilian goods.
• food
• energy
• medicine
• essential civilian goods
• general commercial cargo
② The following items are excluded from protection.
• weapons
• ammunition
• military supplies
• troop transport
• military operational equipment

Article 4. Conflict Containment Principle
① One of the core objectives of this declaration is to reduce the risk of international escalation of armed conflict.
② States other than the belligerent parties shall refrain, to the greatest extent possible, from military intervention.
③ Activities aimed at protecting civilian survival logistics shall be regarded as international cooperative activities separate from military participation.
④ This declaration operates as a neutral cooperation framework intended to mitigate the international expansion of armed conflict.

Article 5. Non-Belligerent Military Participation
① Participating states in this declaration adopt the principle of refraining from military participation in the conflict unless they are direct belligerent parties.
② This declaration is designed as a cooperative mechanism intended to limit the international expansion of armed conflict.

Article 6. Neutral Declaration
① States participating in this declaration affirm the following commitments.
• no military participation on behalf of any belligerent party
• no political endorsement of any belligerent party
• participation in negotiation, mediation, or humanitarian assistance activities may be conducted
② The following actions shall not be regarded as violations of political neutrality.
• humanitarian condemnation of violations of international humanitarian law
• humanitarian statements in accordance with resolutions of international organizations
• expressions of humanitarian concern regarding civilian harm
③ Such actions must satisfy the following conditions.
• they shall not support the military operations of any belligerent party
• they shall not justify military intervention or military assistance
• they shall not interfere with the operation of the neutral transit protection system
④ Participation in this declaration shall not infringe upon the sovereign authority or independent policy decisions of any state.
• each state may determine the scope of its participation in accordance with its constitution, domestic laws, and foreign policy.
⑤ Participation in this declaration is based on the sovereign decision of each state.
⑥ Signatory states respect each other's sovereign diplomatic and security decisions.

Article 7. Flexible Participation
① Participation in this declaration is voluntary.
② Participation in this declaration does not create military, security, or alliance obligations.
③ States may support the protection of civilian survival logistics without altering their existing defense commitments, diplomatic positions, or international alliances.
④ States may determine their level of participation according to their national policies and operational capacity.

Article 8. Restrictions on Military Participation
① Signatory states shall not undertake the following actions.
• deployment of military forces
• support for military operations
• military intelligence assistance
• provision of weapons without commercial basis
• military training support
② Lawful commercial trade may be permitted under the following conditions.
• compliance with international export control regulations
• based on civilian commercial contracts
• not considered state-level military intervention
③ The neutrality of commercial transactions may be verified through an International Cargo Verification Mechanism.

Article 9. Prohibition of Territorial or Military Base Use
① Signatory states shall not permit their territory to be used for military operations by belligerent parties.
• national territory
• territorial waters
• national airspace
• military bases

Article 10. Restrictions on Port and Airport Use
① Signatory states shall not permit their ports or airports to be used for the following purposes.
• offensive military operations
• military logistics resupply
• troop movement

Article 11. Cooperation for Civilian Transit Protection
① Signatory states cooperate in establishing systems to support the safe movement of civilian survival logistics.
• neutral vessel certification systems
• civilian transit protection protocols
• risk-area transit procedures
• digital certification systems
② Navigation data provided by civilian vessels may be transmitted through encrypted communication systems.
③ Access to such data may be restricted to neutral operational authorities.
④ Belligerent parties and external actors may only receive limited information.
• neutral certification status
• transit approval status
⑤ Precise vessel position, routes, and cargo data may be restricted in order to prevent military misuse.

Article 12. Post-Decision Principle on Military Intervention
① Signatory states shall not immediately determine military intervention in response to armed conflict.
② Decisions regarding participation in hostilities may be made only after the following processes.
• international legal fact-finding
• international investigation or institutional review
• consultation with allied states
• constitutional and domestic procedures of each state
③ Each state retains sovereign authority to make its final decision.

Article 13. International Cooperation
① Signatory states may cooperate with the following actors to protect civilian survival logistics.
• international maritime organizations
• neutral-oriented states
• shipping and logistics institutions
② Cooperation structures may operate according to a separate operational framework document.

Article 14. Incident Recording and Responsibility
① If attacks or damage occur against civilian survival logistics or neutral-certified vessels, the incident may be recorded in an international registry.
② Incident records may be managed by the following entities.
• joint investigative teams of signatory states
• neutral observation systems
• Digital Neutral Observatory
③ Recorded incidents may be used for the following procedures.
• international investigations
• dispute resolution procedures
• responsibility determination
• compensation review
④ Attempts to pressure participating states—militarily, diplomatically, or economically—to alter their participation in this system may also be recorded.
⑤ Such actions may be evaluated as external interference with the civilian survival logistics protection system.

Article 15. Automatic Activation Trigger
① The civilian transit protection cooperation provisions of this declaration may be activated when the following situations occur.
• military tensions in major maritime chokepoints
• attacks on civilian vessels or obstruction of maritime transit
• severe disruption of global supply chains
② Following activation, signatory states shall review the operation of the civilian transit protection cooperation system.

Article 16. Scope of Application
① This declaration is not limited to a specific conflict.
② It may apply in the following situations.
• maritime armed conflict
• maritime tension situations
• threats to international maritime logistics

Final Declaration
① States participating in this declaration affirm their commitment to cooperate in protecting logistics essential for human survival even during armed conflict.

Guiding Principle
War is conducted by states, but the logistics necessary for human survival must not become targets of war.
