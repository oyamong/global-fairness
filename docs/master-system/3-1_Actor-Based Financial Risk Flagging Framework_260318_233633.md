3-1. Actor-Based Financial Risk Flagging Framework

Submitted by: Jungyun Kwon, Citizen of the Republic of Korea and Global Citizen
Date: March 19, 2026 (KST)

Article 1. Purpose
① This framework defines a system for generating and transmitting Financial Risk Flags concerning actors and associated networks linked to acts of aggression or structurally threatening conduct.
② The purposes of this framework are as follows:
• Sharing risk awareness within the global financial system
• Encouraging market-based autonomous responses
• Preventing the spread of risk through financial flows
Article 2. Scope of Application
① This framework applies to the following:
• States, organizations, and individuals determined to be actors of aggression
• Supporting and beneficiary networks identified as Associated Actors
• Financial and industrial actors within high-risk influence structures
② The scope of application extends beyond direct actors to include connected networks.
Article 3. Financial Risk Flag Definition
① A Financial Risk Flag refers to a standardized signal indicating the risk status of a specific actor or network within the financial system.
② A risk flag shall include the following information:
• Actor identification details
• Scope of associated networks
• Type of risk
• Level of risk
• Time of application and duration
Article 4. Risk Classification Levels
① Risk shall be classified into the following levels:
• Level 1: Monitoring
• Level 2: Elevated Risk
• Level 3: High Risk
• Level 4: Critical Risk
② Risk levels shall be determined based on the following factors:
• Severity of conduct
• Share of responsibility
• Potential for network propagation
• Impact on the financial system
Article 5. Risk Flag Generation Criteria
① A risk flag shall be generated where one or more of the following conditions are met:
• Determination of aggression or structural threat
• Identification of Associated Actors
• Repeated linkage within financial networks
• Indicators of influence propagation through financial flows
② Generation shall be based on automated analysis, but final confirmation shall be subject to Human Final Authority review.
Article 6. Network-Linked Risk Expansion
① Risk flags may extend beyond a single actor to connected networks.
② Expansion criteria include:
• Financial flow linkages
• Joint investment or shared profit structures
• Repeated transactional relationships
• Shared influence structures
③ Expansion shall be conducted in a limited manner in accordance with the principle of proportionality.
Article 7. Financial System Signaling
① Risk flags may be transmitted to the following institutions and systems:
• Financial institutions (banks, investment firms, etc.)
• Exchanges and payment networks
• International financial cooperation bodies
② The following digital asset-related entities may also be included:
• Centralized Exchanges (CEXs)
• Core infrastructure of decentralized finance (DeFi)
• Stablecoin issuers and payment networks
• Digital asset custodians
③ Signals shall be transmitted in standardized formats, and each institution shall take autonomous risk management measures based on such signals.
Article 8. Market-Based Response Principle
① This framework adopts market-based autonomous response rather than mandatory sanctions.
② Financial institutions and market participants may choose measures such as:
• Restriction or suspension of transactions
• Withdrawal or suspension of investments
• Adjustment of risk premiums
• Enhanced due diligence
③ All measures shall be based on the independent judgment of each institution.
④ Measures under this framework may be interpreted as:
• Actions addressing international security and systemic risk
• Risk avoidance measures under exceptional circumstances
⑤ Legal disputes arising from such measures may be considered under the framework of force majeure in international security or legitimate risk avoidance conduct.
Article 9. Safeguards & Proportionality
① Risk flags shall be designed to avoid excessive financial disruption.
② The following safeguards shall apply:
• Protection of civilian economic activity
• Maintenance of essential industries and infrastructure
• Consideration of global supply chain stability
③ Where excessive risk propagation is detected, the system shall automatically adjust.
Article 10. Right to Clarification
① Entities subject to risk flags shall have the right to clarification.
② Clarification may include:
• Denial of association
• Argument for reduction of responsibility scope
• Claims of data error or misidentification
• Requests for humanitarian exceptions
③ Where significant errors are confirmed, the risk flag shall be modified or removed.
④ Where the following conditions are met, risk levels may be immediately adjusted:
• Voluntary freezing of relevant assets
• Withdrawal from high-risk regions or networks
• Termination of relationships with high-risk conduct
• Cooperation with investigation and verification processes
⑤ Upon fulfillment of such conditions, the following measures may be applied:
• Real-time downgrade of risk level
• Reduction of scope of application
• Partial restoration of financial access
Article 11. Transparency & Traceability
① All risk flags shall be recorded and traceable.
② The following information shall be included:
• Basis for generation
• Scope of application
• History of modifications
• Review records
③ Records shall be used for ex post review and accountability tracking.
Article 12. Human Final Authority
① High-risk flags (Level 3 and above) shall be subject to human review.
② Human decision-makers shall have the authority to:
• Suspend or remove flags
• Adjust risk levels
• Limit scope
• Approve exceptions
③ Automated systems shall not independently finalize financial risk designations.
Article 13. System Integration & Framework Linkage
① This framework is structurally integrated with other core frameworks within the Global Collective Defense System.
② Risk flags shall be generated based on the determinations and analytical outputs of the following frameworks:
• Aggression Determination Framework
• Actor Identification & Attribution Framework
• Associated Actor Identification Protocol
• Responsibility Attribution & Allocation Framework
③ Financial risk flags under this framework may be applied in conjunction with the following systems:
• Actor Isolation & Risk Ring-Fencing Protocol
• Network Containment & Influence Suppression Framework
④ Application and interpretation across frameworks shall follow these principles:
• Consistency in evidence-based assessments
• Alignment between responsibility levels and financial risk classifications
• Proportionality between risk level and market response intensity
⑤ This framework does not constitute an independent sanctions mechanism, but functions as the system’s market-based risk signaling and early response activation stage.
