5-2. Judicial Execution & Asset Recovery Protocol

Submitted by: Jungyun Kwon, Citizen of the Republic of Korea and Global Citizen
Date: March 19, 2026 (KST)

Article 1. Purpose
① This protocol defines the procedures for carrying out actual judicial execution and asset recovery based on responsibility structures, evidence packages, and victim impact records generated through the system.
② The purposes are as follows:
• Ensuring the effectiveness of judicial proceedings
• Securing the rapid recovery of illicit assets
• Realizing meaningful reparations for victims
③ This protocol may include the following:
• Execution of reparations using recovered assets
• Linkage to supplementary state responsibility for reparations not fully satisfied through individual responsibility
Article 2. Fundamental Principle
① This protocol follows the following principles:
• Principle of judicial-authority-centered execution
• Compliance with proportionality and due process
• Priority principle of victim protection
• Execution based on international cooperation
② This system does not itself render judgments, and operates on the basis of requests and rulings issued by judicial authorities.
③ Reparations shall follow the following structure:
• First: recovered assets of individuals and networks
• Second: supplementary reparative responsibility of the state
• Third: public or international funds
Article 3. Execution Trigger
① Execution procedures may be initiated where one or more of the following conditions are met:
• A court judgment or order
• An official request from an investigative authority or prosecutor
• A request from an international judicial body
• A legally recognized urgent need for asset preservation
② All execution shall be carried out on a lawful basis.
③ This execution may also apply to cases involving:
• States
• Protected Entities
• Non-state actors
④ Execution of reparations may also proceed in parallel where:
• The scale of harm has been clearly established
• An urgent need for victim recovery has been recognized
Article 4. Pre-Execution Asset Securing
① Measures for asset protection may be carried out prior to execution, including:
• Monitoring of asset movements
• Linkage to conditional restriction systems
• Temporary immobilization of high-risk assets
② Where an attempt to flee or conceal assets is detected:
• Restriction levels may be escalated immediately
• Expanded application across the full network may be triggered
③ Asset securing shall serve the following purposes:
• Securing resources for future reparations
• Preserving assets for the protection of victims
④ The following shall be considered in the course of execution:
• Maintenance of essential public functions of the state
• Stability of civilian economic systems
• Minimization of humanitarian impact
⑤ Execution measures shall follow the following principles:
• Proportionality
• Minimum impairment
• Gradual application
Article 5. Asset Identification & Mapping
① Assets subject to recovery shall be identified according to the following criteria:
• Directly owned assets
• Nominee-held and indirect assets
• Network-linked assets
• Virtual and digital assets
② All assets shall be linked to:
• Responsibility Dossier
• Asset Flow Analysis
• Network Mapping
③ Asset identification may include connections to the following:
• States
• Protected Entities
• Structures affecting populations and communities
④ Asset identification shall be directly linked to reparative burden-sharing structures.
Article 6. Asset Freezing & Restriction
① Where a legal basis has been secured, the following measures may be carried out:
• Asset freezing
• Transaction blocking
• Account access restriction
• Blocking of virtual asset transfers
② Restrictions shall follow the following criteria:
• Proportionality
• Principle of minimum impairment
• Consideration of economic system stability
③ Frozen assets may be used as resources for reparations.
④ Where the following conditions are met, asset restriction or freezing measures may be applied in a limited or phased manner:
• The relevant state or actor is included in an aggression-related participation structure, but
• The level of responsibility is limited or indirect
⑤ In such cases, the following may apply:
• Restrictions limited to specific assets
• Protection of assets necessary for maintaining core public functions
• Phased or conditional restrictive measures
⑥ Such determinations shall follow:
• Responsibility Attribution & Allocation Framework
• State Responsibility Differentiation & Attribution Framework
Article 7. Recovery Execution
① Asset recovery may be carried out through the following means:
• Confiscation
• Recovery
• Fines and monetary penalties
• International cooperative recovery
② Recovery procedures shall proceed in the following stages:
• Asset confirmation
• Legal authorization
• Execution of recovery
• Recording and verification
③ Recovered assets shall be used first as resources for reparations.
④ Where recovered assets do not fully satisfy the total harm, the following may apply:
• Activation of supplementary state reparative responsibility
• Linkage to public or international funds
⑤ Where the following conditions are met, the scope of recovery may be adjusted:
• The relevant assets are connected to an aggression-related participation structure
• The harm is classified as Self-Risk Participation Damage
⑥ In such cases, the following may apply:
• Adjustment of recovery priority
• Restriction on conversion into reparations resources
• Exclusion of recovery for specific categories of harm
⑦ Such determinations shall follow:
• Victim Impact & Damage Documentation Framework
• State Responsibility & Reparations Framework
Article 8. Escrow & Interim Holding Mechanism
① Assets subject to dispute shall be handled in the following manner:
• Deposit into escrow accounts administered by trusted institutions
② Such assets shall be returned or recovered where the following conditions are met:
• Final legal determination
• Resolution of risk
• Acceptance of clarification
③ Escrow-held assets may include:
• Preservation of principal
• Return including interest or related proceeds
④ Escrow-held assets may be converted into reparations resources.
Article 9. Anti-Evasion Enforcement
① Where the following conduct is identified, strengthened measures shall apply immediately:
• Use of nominee accounts
• Circumvention transfers through virtual assets
• Distributed concealment across networks
② In such cases:
• The entire network may be escalated to Level 4
• All associated assets may be immediately restricted
• Additional investigation may be initiated automatically
③ Evasion attempts may be reflected as aggravating factors in reparative responsibility.
Article 10. Smart Contract Integration
① Execution relating to virtual assets may be linked to smart contracts.
② Automatic execution may be possible where the following conditions are met:
• Confirmation that specified conduct has ceased
• Satisfaction of legal conditions
• Verification through on-chain data
③ This may enable the following:
• Automatic restriction
• Automatic release
• Transparent execution records
④ Smart contracts may also be used in reparations distribution processes.
Article 11. Victim Compensation & Distribution
① Recovered assets shall be distributed according to the following criteria:
• Scale of harm
• Type of harm
• Responsibility ratio
② The following structures may be applied:
• Direct compensation for victims
• Compensation for collective harm
• Recovery and reconstruction funds
③ Eligible recipients of reparations may include:
• Individuals
• Groups
• Communities
• States
• Protected Entities
④ Reparations may be carried out according to the following principles:
• Advance payment followed by post-settlement adjustment
• Priority payment for victim protection
⑤ Where recovered assets are insufficient, the following may apply:
• Supplementary state reparative responsibility
• Linkage to international cooperative funds
⑥ Where the following conditions are met, reparations for the relevant harm may be restricted or excluded:
• The harm is causally linked to aggression-related participation
• The harm arose during defensive or responsive action to aggression
⑦ In such cases, the harm may be classified as Structural Participation Impact.
⑧ Exceptions may apply where the following conditions are proven:
• Involuntary or coerced participation
• Absence of meaningful choice
• Active efforts undertaken to minimize harm
⑨ Such determinations shall follow:
• Victim Impact & Damage Documentation Framework
• Responsibility Attribution & Allocation Framework
• State Responsibility Differentiation & Attribution Framework
Article 12. Multi-Jurisdiction Coordination
① Where multiple jurisdictions are involved, the following shall apply:
• Principle of simultaneous submission
• Priority consideration of international legal standards
• Priority consideration of the place where harm occurred
② Through international cooperation, the following may be carried out:
• Asset tracing
• Recovery cooperation
• Information sharing
③ Execution of reparations may also be carried out through multi-jurisdictional cooperation.
Article 13. Human Final Authority
① Major stages of execution shall be subject to human review.
② Human decision-makers shall have the authority to:
• Approve or suspend execution
• Adjust scope
• Exclude specific assets
• Order emergency suspension
③ Execution and distribution of reparations shall also be subject to human review.
Article 14. Transparency & Auditability
① All execution processes shall be recorded.
② Records shall include the following:
• Basis for execution
• Asset movement records
• Recovery details
• Reparations details
③ All records shall be used for ex post audit and legal verification.
④ Financial flows and distribution structures related to reparations shall likewise be recorded and subject to audit.
Article 15. System Integration & Enforcement Continuity
① This protocol is fully integrated with the risk containment, responsibility analysis, victim documentation, and reparations systems within the Global Collective Defense System.
② Pre-execution asset securing and risk containment shall remain consistent with:
• Provisional Measures & Risk Containment Protocol
③ Assets subject to execution and responsibility structures shall be identified on the basis of:
• Responsibility Dossier Generation System
• Responsibility Attribution & Allocation Framework
④ Reparations criteria and distribution structures shall be directly linked to:
• Victim Impact & Damage Documentation Framework
• State Responsibility & Reparations Framework
⑤ Execution under this protocol shall remain consistent with the following evidence and submission systems:
• Legal Evidence Packaging Protocol
• Chain of Custody & Evidence Integrity Framework
• Multi-Jurisdiction Judicial Submission Protocol
⑥ This protocol shall operate continuously through the following stages:
• Asset securing → Asset freezing → Asset recovery → Reparations execution
⑦ This protocol functions as the Final Enforcement Layer within the overall system, transforming responsibility, evidence, and harm into actual asset recovery and reparations.
