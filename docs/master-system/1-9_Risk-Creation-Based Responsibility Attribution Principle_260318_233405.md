1-9.Risk-Creation-Based Responsibility Attribution Principle

Article 1. General Principle
① Where an act of aggression creates an underlying risk that foreseeably results in harm, the primary responsibility for such harm may, in principle, be attributed to the aggressor State.
② This principle applies to harm arising not only from direct consequences of the act of aggression but also to harm suffered by third States or other protected entities in the course of defensive actions undertaken in response to such aggression.
Article 2. Limitation
① The attribution of primary responsibility to the aggressor State shall not preclude the separate assessment of responsibility of a defending State where there is a manifest failure to comply with the principles of necessity, proportionality, and minimization of harm to civilians and third States.
② In such cases, responsibility may be concurrently attributed, and the allocation of responsibility shall be determined in accordance with the respective level of contribution, degree of fault, and causal linkage to the harm.
Article 3. System-wide Applicability
① This principle shall apply across the entire Global Collective Defense System and shall guide the interpretation and application of the following frameworks and protocols:
• Responsibility Attribution & Allocation Framework
• State Responsibility Differentiation & Attribution Framework
• Victim Impact & Damage Documentation Framework
• Judicial Execution & Asset Recovery Protocol
• State Responsibility & Reparations Framework
② This principle shall function as a general interpretative rule for all attribution, allocation, enforcement, and reparation mechanisms within the system.
