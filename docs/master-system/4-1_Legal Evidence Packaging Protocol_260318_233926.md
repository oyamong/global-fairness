4-1. Legal Evidence Packaging Protocol

Submitted by: Jungyun Kwon, Citizen of the Republic of Korea and Global Citizen
Date: March 19, 2026 (KST)

Article 1. Purpose
① This protocol defines the procedures for structuring all data collected and analyzed by the system into Legal Evidence Packages compliant with international judicial standards.
② The purposes are as follows:
• Ensuring usability in international judicial and legal proceedings
• Strengthening legal consistency and reliability of evidence
• Clarifying responsibility attribution
Article 2. Definition of Legal Evidence Package
① A Legal Evidence Package refers to an integrated documentation set that includes:
• Actor identification information
• Analysis of actions and responsibilities
• Network structures
• Asset flows and influence structures
• Evidence data and records
② The package shall consist of a structured body of evidence, not a single item.
Article 3. Scope of Evidence
① Evidence shall include the following domains:
• Conduct evidence (acts and incident records)
• Network evidence (association structures)
• Financial evidence (fund flows and asset structures)
• Technical evidence (digital logs and system data)
• Impact evidence (damage and consequences)
② All evidence shall be structured in an interconnected manner.
③ Impact evidence may include the following protected subjects:
• States
• Protected Entities
• Populations and communities
④ Where human evidence is included, the following protection measures shall apply:
• De-identification of witness identity
• Technical masking of sensitive information
• Separation of identifiable data
• Simultaneous preservation of evidentiary value and identity protection
Article 4. Evidence Collection Standards
① Evidence collection shall adhere to the following standards:
• Verified source reliability
• Preservation of data integrity
• Maintenance of temporal continuity
• Capability for independent verification
② A minimum of two independent sources shall be required.
③ The system shall comply with the following principle:
• When collecting evidence indicating violations by a specific actor,
• evidence that may mitigate or refute responsibility shall be collected concurrently
④ Exculpatory evidence shall meet the following criteria:
• Inclusion within the same incident scope
• Structuring in equivalent format
• Equal weighting during analysis
Article 5. Evidence Structuring
① Evidence shall be organized in the following structure:
• Incident Overview
• Actor Analysis
• Network Mapping
• Asset Flow Analysis
• Responsibility Attribution
• Victim / Protected Entity Impact
• Legal Qualification
② All components shall be logically interconnected.
Article 6. Chain of Custody Integration
① All evidence shall maintain a Chain of Custody from collection to submission.
② The following elements shall be included:
• Collection timestamp
• Storage and access records
• Modification history
• Verification records
③ Integrity verification must be ensured.
④ The following procedures may be applied during evidence collection:
• Generation of unique identifiers (hash values)
• Registration in tamper-resistant recording systems
• Establishment of verifiable integrity standards
⑤ These mechanisms shall serve to verify whether any post-collection alteration has occurred.
Article 7. Standardization for International Use
① Evidence packages shall be standardized for use by:
• International Criminal Court (ICC)
• International Court of Justice (ICJ)
• UN investigative bodies
• National judicial authorities
② International legal standards and evidentiary requirements shall be incorporated.
Article 8. Digital Evidence Integration
① Digital evidence shall include:
• Log data
• Communication records
• Transaction records
• System analysis outputs
② All digital evidence shall incorporate anti-tampering mechanisms.
③ The following protective measures may be applied:
• Partial de-identification of data structures
• Minimization of source exposure
• Data handling to protect analytical methodologies
④ These measures serve the following purposes:
• Protection of collection methodologies
• Prevention of reverse engineering
• Ensuring sustainability of investigative systems
Article 9. Responsibility Mapping
① The evidence package shall clearly identify:
• Direct responsible actors
• Indirect responsible actors
• Associated actors
• Responsibility allocation ratios
② Responsibility structures shall be analyzed on a network basis and shall include linkages to affected states or protected entities.
Article 10. Victim Impact Documentation
① Impact documentation shall include:
• Human impact
• Economic damage
• Social consequences
• Long-term effects
② Impact may be recorded with respect to:
• States
• Protected Entities
• Populations and communities
③ Impact shall be documented both quantitatively and qualitatively.
Article 11. Review & Validation
① All evidence packages shall undergo the following processes:
• Technical verification
• Legal review
• Structural consistency review
② Additional materials may be required where necessary.
Article 12. Human Final Authority
① Finalization of evidence packages shall require human review.
② Human decision-makers shall have the authority to:
• Suspend submission
• Request modifications
• Adjust scope
• Direct additional investigation
Article 13. Submission Readiness
① An evidence package shall be deemed ready for submission when the following conditions are met:
• Compliance with legal standards
• Secured evidentiary integrity
• Clarity of responsibility structure
• Conformity with international standards
② Final validation procedures shall be conducted prior to submission.
Article 14. Transparency & Auditability
① All processes related to evidence generation and structuring shall be recorded.
② Records shall include:
• Evidence sources
• Analytical processes
• Review records
• Modification history
③ Records shall be used for ex post audit and legal verification.
Article 15. System Integration & Framework Linkage
① This protocol is structurally integrated with all analytical and response frameworks within the Global Collective Defense System.
② Evidence packages shall be constructed based on data and analytical outputs generated by:
• Aggression Determination Framework
• Actor Identification & Attribution Framework
• Associated Actor Identification Protocol
• Responsibility Attribution & Allocation Framework
③ Network and asset information generated by the following frameworks shall be integrated into evidence packages:
• Influence-Linked Asset Monitoring Framework
• Extended Network Risk Propagation Protocol
• Network Containment & Influence Suppression Framework
④ Measures and response records generated by the following frameworks may be included as evidence:
• Conditional Asset Restriction Protocol
• Actor Isolation & Risk Ring-Fencing Protocol
• Financial Network Alert & Integration Protocol
⑤ This protocol integrates outputs from all frameworks into a
structured evidentiary system suitable for submission in international judicial proceedings.
⑥ Data integration across frameworks shall follow these principles:
• Maintenance of temporal continuity
• Logical linkage across evidence elements
• Alignment between responsibility structures and evidence
• Equal structural incorporation of exculpatory evidence
⑦ This protocol does not operate as an independent analytical system, but functions as the system’s
final integration stage for legal submission and judicial utilization.
