PHASE 1: Operational Detection & Response Infrastructure
3-4. Influence-Linked Asset Monitoring Framework

Submitted by: Jungyun Kwon, Citizen of the Republic of Korea and Global Citizen
Date: March 19, 2026 (KST)

Article 1. Purpose
① This framework defines a system for the identification, tracking, and monitoring of influence-linked assets connected to aggressors and associated networks.
② The purposes are as follows:
• Identifying power-based asset concealment structures
• Tracking network-based asset movements
• Disrupting indirect benefit structures
Article 2. Definition of Influence-Linked Assets
① Influence-linked assets refer to:
• Assets under the influence of actors or associated networks
• Assets that are controllable or dominable even without direct ownership
• Assets to which benefits are structurally attributable
② The scope includes:
• Financial assets (accounts, investments, securities, etc.)
• Virtual and digital assets
• Corporate equity and investment structures
• Trusts, funds, and indirect investment structures
• Assets held in the name of third parties
Article 3. Scope of Monitoring
① Monitoring applies to:
• Aggressor actors
• Associated actors
• Extended networks
• Asset holders within influence structures
② Monitoring shall be conducted not at the level of individual assets, but at the level of asset networks.
③ The activities of the following professional facilitators (Gatekeepers) may also be subject to monitoring:
• Legal advisory and structuring services
• Accounting and auditing services
• Trust and asset management services
• Corporate formation and nominee structuring services
④ Such facilitating activities shall be assessed based on their role in asset concealment or circumvention structures.
Article 4. Identification Criteria
① Influence-linked assets shall be identified based on the following criteria:
• Existence of effective control or dominance
• Connectivity of financial flows
• Repetitive transactions and circular structures
• Patterns of ownership dispersion or concealment
• Benefit attribution structures
② Determination shall be based on substantive influence rather than formal ownership.
Article 5. Asset Network Mapping
① The system shall analyze structural relationships among assets.
② The analysis shall include:
• Financial flow pathways
• Ownership and investment structures
• Connected actor networks
• Points of influence concentration
③ Core Asset Nodes shall be prioritized for identification.
④ The system may include the following analytical capabilities:
• Simulation of complex ownership structures
• Reconstruction of multi-layered investment and circumvention structures
• Predictive modeling of Ultimate Beneficial Ownership (UBO)
• Network-based influence concentration analysis
⑤ Such analysis may be conducted through digital representation (virtual modeling) of asset structures to infer effective control relationships.
Article 6. Monitoring Levels
① Asset monitoring shall be classified into the following levels:
• Level 1: Passive Monitoring
• Level 2: Enhanced Monitoring
• Level 3: Active Tracking
• Level 4: Strategic Surveillance
② Monitoring levels shall be adjusted according to risk levels.
Article 7. Dynamic Asset Tracking
① Asset monitoring shall be conducted in real-time or near real-time.
② The following elements shall be tracked:
• Asset movements
• Transaction frequency and patterns
• Network expansion
• Structural changes in assets
③ Asset movements shall be analyzed in conjunction with risk signals.
④ Where the following high-risk indicators are detected, response measures may be strengthened:
• Sudden asset movements
• Transfers to tax havens or opaque jurisdictions
• Formation of multi-layered dispersal structures
• Abnormal changes in transaction patterns
⑤ In such cases, the following measures may be applied:
• Transmission of urgent risk alerts to financial institutions
• Recommendation of transaction review or temporary suspension
• Requests for additional due diligence
• Recommendations for rapid response to high-risk assets
⑥ These measures are intended as pre-emptive risk mitigation, and shall be applied at the discretion of each institution.
Article 8. Cross-Border Monitoring
① Asset monitoring shall be conducted across national boundaries.
② The following elements shall be included:
• Multinational financial networks
• International payment systems
• Overseas investments and corporate structures
• Tax avoidance structures
③ Monitoring shall be linked with international cooperation mechanisms.
Article 9. Financial System Integration
① Asset monitoring shall be integrated with financial networks.
② The following information may be utilized:
• Transaction data
• Account flows
• Investment activities
• Network linkage data
③ Integration shall comply with applicable data protection standards.
Article 10. Safeguards & Proportionality
① Monitoring shall adhere to the following principles:
• Protection of legitimate economic activity
• Prevention of excessive surveillance
• Principle of minimal intervention
• Data-driven decision-making
② Innocent actors and assets shall be protected.
Article 11. Right to Clarification
① Subjects of monitoring shall have the right to clarification.
② Clarification may include:
• Denial of ownership or control
• Explanation of transaction legitimacy
• Denial of association
• Identification of data errors
③ Monitoring levels may be adjusted based on clarification outcomes.
Article 12. Human Oversight
① High-risk monitoring (Level 3 and above) shall be subject to human review.
② Human decision-makers shall have the authority to:
• Reduce monitoring scope
• Exclude specific assets
• Adjust monitoring levels
• Request additional verification
Article 13. Transparency & Traceability
① All asset monitoring processes shall be recorded.
② Records shall include:
• Monitored assets
• Connected actors
• Basis for monitoring
• Asset movement history
③ Records may be used as evidence in subsequent legal proceedings.
Article 14. System Integration & Framework Linkage
① This framework is structurally integrated with other core frameworks within the Global Collective Defense System.
② Asset monitoring shall be conducted based on actor and network data identified through:
• Associated Actor Identification Protocol
• Actor Identification & Attribution Framework
③ Asset information identified under this framework may be used for risk analysis and propagation in:
• Actor-Based Financial Risk Flagging Framework
• Extended Network Risk Propagation Protocol
④ Asset-based risk and movement data may be transmitted to the financial system through:
• Financial Network Alert & Integration Protocol
⑤ Monitoring results may be applied in conjunction with:
• Network Containment & Influence Suppression Framework
• Actor Isolation & Risk Ring-Fencing Protocol
⑥ Asset monitoring and risk evaluation shall remain aligned with:
• Responsibility Attribution & Allocation Framework
⑦ This framework does not constitute an independent sanctions mechanism, but functions as the system’s stage for identifying asset-based influence structures and conducting financial risk analysis.
