4-3. Multi-Jurisdiction Judicial Submission Protocol

Submitted by: Jungyun Kwon, Citizen of the Republic of Korea and Global Citizen
Date: March 19, 2026 (KST)

Article 1. Purpose
① This protocol defines a system for preserving, structuring, and managing evidence so that it remains immediately ready for submission upon request across multiple jurisdictions (domestic and international judicial bodies).
② The purposes are as follows:
• Ensuring the speed and efficiency of judicial proceedings
• Preventing evidence degradation and procedural delay
• Enabling compatibility with multi-jurisdictional legal systems
Article 2. Fundamental Principle
① The system does not perform direct judicial enforcement functions.
② All evidence submission shall follow these principles:
• On-Demand Submission
• Non-Enforcement Principle
• Human Final Authority
③ The system operates under the principle:
“It does not decide; it maintains readiness.”
Article 3. Immediate Submission Readiness
① All evidence packages shall remain in a state that is:
Immediately deliverable upon request by domestic courts, investigative authorities, prosecutorial bodies, or international judicial institutions without additional preparation.
② The structure shall allow submission without additional analysis, reconstruction, or revalidation.
Article 4. Scope of Applicable Jurisdictions
① This protocol applies to all of the following jurisdictions:
• National criminal and civil courts
• State investigative and prosecutorial authorities
• International judicial bodies and tribunals
• International investigative and prosecutorial entities
② It is not limited to any single jurisdiction and allows for parallel multi-jurisdictional response.
Article 5. Requesting Authority
① Requests for evidence submission may be made by the following entities:
1. Judicial Authorities
• Domestic courts at all levels
• International tribunals
2. Prosecutorial Authorities
• Prosecutors and charging authorities
• International criminal prosecutorial bodies
3. Investigative Authorities
• National investigative agencies
• Independent investigative bodies
• International commissions of inquiry
② All requests may undergo the following verification procedures:
• Confirmation of legal authority of the requesting entity
• Review of judicial legitimacy of the request purpose
• Assessment of appropriateness of the request scope
③ Verification of legitimacy may include:
• Compatibility with legal procedures of the jurisdiction
• Consistency with international human rights standards
• Comparison with similar case patterns
• Assessment of excessive targeting of specific subjects
④ Where substantial doubts arise regarding legitimacy:
• Submission may be suspended
• Additional clarification may be requested
• Submission scope may be limited
Article 6. On-Demand Submission Mechanism
① Evidence shall be submitted only upon request.
② In the absence of a request:
• Automatic submission is prohibited
• External disclosure is prohibited
• Preemptive intervention is prohibited
③ Upon receipt of a request:
• Submission procedures shall be initiated immediately
• Delivery shall occur without delay
Article 7. Multi-Jurisdiction Compatibility
① Evidence packages shall be structured to ensure compatibility with:
• Domestic criminal law
• Civil law
• Financial and regulatory law
• International law
② Evidence shall be maintained in a form adaptable to multiple legal standards.
③ Where discovery or disclosure is requested by courts:
• Exculpatory evidence shall be provided to ensure defense rights
• Equal integrity standards shall be maintained
• Structural consistency of evidence packages shall be preserved
④ These procedures ensure judicial fairness and balance.
Article 8. Evidence Integrity & Preservation
① All evidence shall meet the following requirements:
• Preservation of integrity
• Maintenance of Chain of Custody
• Protection against tampering
② Evidence shall always be maintained in a state of legal admissibility.
Article 9. Non-Enforcement Clause
① The system shall not perform the following actions:
• Arrest
• Asset freezing
• Sanctions enforcement
• Legal adjudication
② All enforcement actions fall within the authority of competent judicial bodies of the relevant jurisdiction.
Article 10. Jurisdiction Neutrality
① The system shall not be subordinated to any specific state or institution.
② For all jurisdictions:
• Equal standards shall be applied
• Equal access shall be provided
• Non-discriminatory response shall be ensured
Article 11. Submission Process
① Upon receipt of a request, the following procedures shall be conducted:
• Verification of requesting authority
• Confirmation of submission scope
• Extraction of relevant evidence packages
• Secure transmission
② Where multiple jurisdictional requests occur simultaneously:
• Parallel submission shall be applied where feasible
• Common scope shall take precedence in case of overlap
• Conflicting requests shall be resolved through human review
③ The submission process shall adhere to:
• Prohibition of jurisdictional bias
• Minimization of information asymmetry across jurisdictions
• Maintenance of judicial fairness
④ Submitted evidence may relate to:
• States
• Protected Entities
• Populations and communities
Article 12. Security & Confidentiality
① Evidence submission shall adhere to:
• Encrypted transmission
• Access control
• Confidentiality protection
② Unauthorized access or disclosure is prohibited.
Article 13. Transparency & Auditability
① All submission processes shall be recorded.
② Records shall include:
• Requesting entity
• Time of request
• Scope of submission
• Transmission records
③ Records shall be used for post hoc audit.
Article 14. Human Final Authority
① Final submission shall be subject to human review.
② Human decision-makers shall have the authority to:
• Approve or suspend submission
• Adjust scope
• Request additional verification
Article 15. System Integration & Evidence Flow Linkage
① This protocol is fully integrated with all analytical and evidence-generation systems within the Global Collective Defense System.
② All submitted evidence shall be based on outputs generated and structured by:
• Legal Evidence Packaging Protocol
• Chain of Custody & Evidence Integrity Framework
③ Event, actor, and responsibility structures generated by the following frameworks shall be included in submitted evidence:
• Aggression Determination Framework
• Actor Identification & Attribution Framework
• Responsibility Attribution & Allocation Framework
④ Network and asset data generated by the following systems shall be integrated into evidence packages:
• Influence-Linked Asset Monitoring Framework
• Financial Network Alert & Integration Protocol
• Extended Network Risk Propagation Protocol
⑤ This protocol functions as the system’s
final stage for delivering all generated data in a form suitable for submission to multi-jurisdictional judicial authorities.
⑥ During submission, all data shall maintain:
• Integrity and Chain of Custody
• Logical linkage across evidence
• Inclusion of exculpatory evidence
⑦ This protocol does not perform independent judgment or enforcement, but operates as a
Neutral Judicial Interface Layer for submission within the overall system.
