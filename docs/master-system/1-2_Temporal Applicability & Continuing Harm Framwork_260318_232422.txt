1-2. Temporal Applicability & Continuing Harm Framework

Submitted by: Jungyun Kwon, Citizen of the Republic of Korea and Global Citizen
Date: March 19, 2026 (KST)

Article 1. Purpose
① This framework defines the temporal applicability of acts of aggression and related conduct, and establishes criteria under which continuing harm or serious violations allow for judicial assessment and accountability, even for past events.
② The purposes are as follows:
• Prevention of accountability gaps for past acts of aggression
• Recognition of the present nature of continuing harm structures
• Protection of victims and realization of reparations
• Ensuring the effectiveness of international justice
③ This framework operates as a non-punitive, normative standard, and final determinations of responsibility shall be made through relevant judicial procedures and subsequent frameworks.
Article 2. General Principle of Temporal Applicability
① As a general rule, all determinations of responsibility shall follow the legal standards applicable at the time of the conduct.
② This framework maintains consistency with the following principles of international law:
• Nullum crimen sine lege
• Fundamental principles of international human rights law and international criminal law
③ This framework does not create new liability but serves to assess the continuity and present relevance of existing violations.
④ Accordingly, this framework does not introduce retroactive criminal punishment.
Article 3. Continuing Harm Doctrine
① Where the following conditions are met, a situation may be assessed as Continuing Harm:
• The harm persists to the present
• Structural effects remain unresolved
• The condition of harm is repeated or expanding
② The following may be recognized as continuing harm:
• Ongoing occupation or effective control of territory
• Continued refugee or forced displacement situations
• Persistent structural collapse of economic systems
• Long-term impacts of environmental destruction
• Continued degradation of survival foundations due to infrastructure destruction
③ Where continuing harm is recognized:
• The situation shall be assessed as ongoing in the present
• Responsibility analysis, asset preservation, and reparations processes may be applied
④ Determination of continuing harm shall be aligned with:
• Victim Impact & Damage Documentation Framework
• Responsibility Attribution & Allocation Framework
Article 4. Exceptional Retroactive Consideration
① Where one or more of the following conditions are met, exceptional review of past conduct may be considered:
• Large-scale civilian harm
• Systematic or widespread human rights violations
• Long-term or structural harm
• Significant impact on international peace and security
② Such application must satisfy the following criteria:
• Consistency with fundamental principles of international law
• Availability of sufficient evidence
• Minimization of potential political misuse
• Preservation of judicial fairness
③ This Article maintains consistency with the following categories:
• War crimes
• Genocide
• Crimes against humanity
④ This Article is limited to responsibility assessment and restorative measures, and does not introduce retroactive criminal punishment.
Article 5. Protected Entities & Non-State Context
① This framework applies not only to states but also to the following:
• Protected Entities
• Occupied territories
• Territories with de facto governance structures
• Populations and communities
② Regardless of state status, this framework applies where violations of survival foundations exist.
Article 6. Evidentiary Requirements
① The following standards shall apply:
• Legal Evidence Package
• Chain of Custody
• Multi-source verification
② The following elements shall be included:
• Proof of continuity of harm
• Analysis of structural impact
• Documentation of temporal evolution
③ Evidentiary standards shall be aligned with:
• Legal Evidence Packaging Protocol
• Chain of Custody & Evidence Integrity Framework
Article 7. Responsibility Continuity
① Where continuing harm is recognized:
• Responsibility of the original actor shall be maintained
• Responsibility of subsequent or sustaining actors shall be added
• Responsibility of structural contributors shall be included
② Responsibility shall not be extinguished solely by the passage of time.
③ The Responsibility Dossier shall reflect:
• Temporal expansion of responsibility
• Changes in contribution levels
• Records of continuity
④ Assessment of responsibility levels shall be aligned with:
• State Responsibility Differentiation & Attribution Framework
Article 8. Temporal Scope of Asset Recovery
① The scope includes:
• Assets acquired in the past
• Ongoing revenue streams
• Structural or derived benefits
② The following approaches shall be applied:
• Long-term tracking
• Expanded network identification
• Analysis linking past and present
③ This Article shall be aligned with:
• Judicial Execution & Asset Recovery Protocol
• State Responsibility & Reparations Framework
Article 9. Victim Compensation & Restitution
① The following measures may be applied:
• Individual compensation
• Collective reparations
• Recovery and reconstruction
② The following criteria shall apply:
• Scale of harm
• Duration of harm
• Structural impact
③ Applicable beneficiaries include:
• Individuals
• Groups
• Communities
• States
• Protected Entities
④ The reparations structure shall be aligned with:
• Victim Impact & Damage Documentation Framework
• State Responsibility & Reparations Framework
Article 10. Safeguards Against Abuse
① The following principles shall apply:
• Prohibition of politically selective application
• Prevention of indiscriminate reopening of past conflicts
• Exclusion of claims lacking evidence
② The following standards shall apply:
• Objectivity
• Consistency with international standards
• Multi-layer verification
③ This framework shall not be used as a tool for political or selective imposition of responsibility.
Article 11. Human Final Authority
① Determination of continuing harm and retroactive consideration shall be subject to human review.
② Human decision-makers shall have the authority to:
• Approve or reject application
• Adjust scope of application
• Request additional verification
Article 12. Integration with System Architecture
① This framework is integrated with:
• Definition of Aggression
• Aggression Determination Framework
• Legal Evidence Packaging Protocol
• Responsibility Dossier Generation System
• Judicial Execution & Asset Recovery Protocol
• Actor Identification & Attribution Framework
② Additional integration includes:
• Victim Impact & Damage Documentation Framework
• State Responsibility & Reparations Framework
③ Temporal consistency across all system components shall be ensured.
Article 13. Transparency & Auditability
① All applications of this framework shall be recorded.
② Records shall include:
• Basis for application
• Decision criteria
• Evidence materials
• Review processes
③ Such records shall be used for post-audit and international verification.
