4-5. Cooperative Disclosure & Conditional Immunity Protocol

Submitted by: Jungyun Kwon, Citizen of the Republic of Korea and Global Citizen
Date: March 19, 2026 (KST)

Article 1. Purpose
① This protocol defines the criteria for protection, mitigation of responsibility, or conditional immunity for actors who contribute to dismantling responsibility structures through voluntary cooperation and disclosure of information.
② The purposes are as follows:
• Facilitating internal cooperation to dismantle networks
• Breaking responsibility evasion structures
• Accelerating evidence acquisition and asset recovery
• Ensuring fairness and respect for human rights in accountability determination
Article 2. Definition of Cooperative Actor
① A Cooperative Actor refers to an individual or entity that meets the following conditions:
• Has been involved in an aggression-related network
• Voluntarily provides information or evidence
• Contributes to dismantling responsibility structures or identifying higher-level actors
② Cooperative actors may include:
• Direct actors
• Indirect actors
• Supporting and enabling actors
• Asset transfer and management actors
③ This provision does not negate the existence of prior responsibility.
Article 3. Eligibility Criteria
① An actor may be recognized as a cooperative actor if one or more of the following conditions are met:
• Provision of substantial and verifiable information
• Contribution to dismantling network structures
• Identification of higher-level responsible actors or decision-making structures
• Contribution to asset tracing and recovery
② Mere statements or unverified information shall not qualify.
③ Provided information must meet the following standards:
• Objective verifiability
• Direct relevance to the incident
• Capacity to confirm material facts
Article 4. Levels of Cooperation & Legal Effect
① The following measures may be applied according to levels of cooperation:
Level A: Full Cooperation
• Decisive contribution to dismantling core structures
→ Conditional immunity or measures equivalent to immunity may be applied
Level B: Substantial Cooperation
• Identification of key actors or provision of critical information
→ Significant mitigation of responsibility
Level C: Partial Cooperation
• Provision of limited information
→ Limited mitigation
② The level of cooperation shall be assessed based on:
• Significance of information
• Degree of contribution
• Timing (early cooperation)
• Resulting impact
Article 5. Exclusion of Core Responsibility
① The following individuals shall be excluded from full immunity:
• Primary decision-makers
• Key architects or commanders of aggression
• Principal leaders of organized actions
② For such individuals, only the following measures may apply:
• Mitigation of responsibility
• Application of protective measures
Article 6. Verification & Corroboration
① All cooperative information shall undergo:
• Multi-source verification
• Cross-validation with existing evidence
• Independent analysis
② At least two independent verifications shall be required.
③ In cases of false or distorted information:
• Revocation of cooperative status
• Potential imposition of additional responsibility
Article 7. Integration with Evidence Systems
① Cooperative information shall be integrated with:
• Legal Evidence Packages
• Chain of Custody Framework
• Responsibility Dossiers
② All information shall be structured in compliance with evidentiary standards.
③ Cooperative information shall adhere to the following principles:
• Equal integrity standards
• Equal weighting with exculpatory evidence
Article 8. Responsibility Reassessment
① Upon recognition as a cooperative actor:
• Responsibility levels shall be reassessed
• Responsibility ratios shall be adjusted
• Scope of responsibility may be reduced
② These changes shall be reflected in the Responsibility Dossier.
③ All modifications shall be recorded.
Article 9. Protection Measures
① Cooperative actors may be entitled to:
• Identity protection
• Physical security protection
• Anti-retaliation measures
② Enhanced protection shall be applied where:
• There is a threat to life
• Risk of organized retaliation exists
• The actor is linked to high-risk networks
Article 10. Anti-Abuse Safeguards
① The following actions are prohibited:
• False cooperation
• Strategic partial disclosure intended to evade responsibility
• False accusations against other actors
② The following standards shall apply:
• Assessment based on substantive contribution
• Outcome-based evaluation
• Requirement of continued cooperation
③ In cases of abuse:
• Revocation of cooperative status
• Aggravation of responsibility
• Initiation of additional investigation
Article 11. Right to Clarification
① Cooperative actors and related parties shall have the right to:
• Challenge recognition status
• Request adjustment of responsibility levels
• Submit additional information
② Decisions may be revised upon review.
③ All processes shall be recorded.
Article 12. Human Final Authority
① Recognition of cooperative status and determination of immunity shall require human review.
② Human decision-makers shall have the authority to:
• Approve or reject cooperative status
• Determine the level of mitigation
• Decide on conditional immunity
• Approve protection measures
Article 13. Integration with Judicial Submission
① Cooperative status shall be reflected in:
• Multi-Jurisdiction Judicial Submission Protocol
• Materials submitted to courts and investigative authorities
② Submissions may include:
• Details of cooperation
• Evaluation of contribution
• Basis for mitigation of responsibility
Article 14. Transparency & Auditability
① All decisions regarding cooperative actors shall be recorded.
② Records shall include:
• Nature of cooperation
• Verification processes
• Adjustments to responsibility
• Application of protection measures
③ Records shall be used for post hoc audit and legal verification.
Article 15. System Integration & Incentivized Disclosure Mechanism
① This protocol is fully integrated with all analytical, evidentiary, and accountability systems within the Global Collective Defense System.
② Cooperative actor data shall be combined with data generated by:
• Actor Identification & Attribution Framework
• Associated Actor Identification Protocol
③ Cooperative information shall be structured as evidence through:
• Legal Evidence Packaging Protocol
• Chain of Custody & Evidence Integrity Framework
④ Reassessment of responsibility shall be reflected in:
• Responsibility Dossier Generation System
• Responsibility Attribution & Allocation Framework
⑤ Cooperative status and mitigation outcomes shall be reflected in submissions through:
• Multi-Jurisdiction Judicial Submission Protocol
⑥ Cooperative information may also be utilized in:
• Influence-Linked Asset Monitoring Framework
• Extended Network Risk Propagation Protocol
⑦ This protocol functions as the system’s
Incentivized Internal Collapse Mechanism, enabling the dismantling of networks through voluntary disclosure.
