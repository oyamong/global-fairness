2-2. Network Containment & Influence Suppression Framework

Submitted by: Jungyun Kwon, Citizen of the Republic of Korea and Global Citizen
Date: March 19, 2026 (KST)

Article 1. Purpose
① This framework defines a system for containing the spread of network-based influence linked to acts of aggression or structurally threatening conduct.
② The purposes of this framework are as follows:
• Preventing the expansion of influence by high-risk actors
• Disrupting financial, informational, and power networks
• Suppressing cascading instability within the global system
Article 2. Scope of Network
① Within this framework, “network” includes the following:
• Financial networks (fund flows, investment structures)
• Political and diplomatic networks
• Military and security cooperation structures
• Technological and digital infrastructure networks
• Logistics and supply chain structures
② Networks shall be analyzed to include both direct and indirect linkages.
Article 3. Influence Mapping
① The system shall analyze the following structures centered on the actor:
• Financial flow pathways
• Decision-making influence pathways
• Information and public opinion influence structures
• Material and technical support flows
② Influence shall be classified according to the following categories:
• Direct Influence
• Indirect Influence
• Structural Influence
Article 4. Network Containment Strategy
① The system shall contain networks through the following measures:
• Identification and restriction of Core Nodes
• Disruption of connection pathways
• Detection and blocking of circumvention networks
• Reduction of the speed of influence propagation
② Containment strategies shall be applied in stages:
• Level 1: Detection of influence and warning
• Level 2: Restriction of network connections
• Level 3: Blocking of core nodes
• Level 4: Structural separation of networks
③ Detection of circumvention networks shall include the following structures:
• Financial pathways based in tax havens
• Offshore entities and paper companies
• Routes passing through regulatory gray-zone jurisdictions
④ The following measures may be coordinated:
• Recommendations for restricting financial transactions along high-risk pathways
• Issuance of risk alerts to international financial institutions
• Information sharing among cooperating entities regarding circumvention pathways
⑤ Such measures shall function as an international risk management advisory system, and may serve as reference material for national legal determinations and policy decisions.
Article 5. Influence Suppression Mechanism
① The system shall implement influence suppression in the following domains:
• Reduction of financial influence
• Limitation of information and media influence
• Restriction of political and diplomatic influence
• Limitation of access to technology and platforms
② To suppress information and public opinion manipulation, the following mechanisms may be included:
• Detection and disruption of automated bot networks
• Analysis of coordinated mass-account influence operations
• Blocking of dissemination pathways for deepfakes and synthetic content
• Detection of AI-driven information manipulation
• Reduction of the propagation speed of disinformation networks
③ Influence suppression shall be applied primarily to prevent propagation, and excessive control shall be prohibited.
Article 6. Network Risk Propagation Control
① The system shall analyze the propagation of risk within networks in real time.
② Strengthened containment measures shall be applied where the following conditions are met:
• Multi-layered connections to high-risk actors
• Increasing dependency within financial and supply chain structures
• Indicators of expanding political influence
③ The system shall designate priority containment pathways along the routes of risk propagation.
Article 7. Proportionality & Safeguards
① All network containment measures shall be applied in accordance with the principle of proportionality.
② The following standards shall apply:
• Protection of civilian economic activity
• Maintenance of essential supply chains
• Prevention of excessive economic disruption
③ Where excessive containment is detected, the system shall automatically adjust the measures.
Article 8. Humanitarian & Civilian Protection
① The following areas shall be subject to special protection:
• Food and energy supply chains
• Essential medical systems
• Civilian communication infrastructure
② Network containment shall be designed so as not to infringe upon civilian survival rights.
Article 9. Human Final Authority
① All high-risk network containment measures shall be subject to human review.
② Human decision-makers shall have the authority to:
• Adjust the scope of containment
• Protect specific networks
• Apply humanitarian exceptions
• Temporarily defer measures
③ Automated systems shall not independently finalize structural containment measures.
Article 10. Transparency & Adaptive Review
① All network containment measures shall be recorded and traceable.
② The following elements shall be included:
• Targeted network
• Basis for application
• Scope of impact
• Expected effects
③ The system shall continuously learn and improve containment strategies.
④ Where containment measures adversely affect good-faith third parties, the following support may be provided:
• Assistance in connecting to alternative financial and payment networks
• Support for supply chain restructuring
• Assistance in relocation of technology and data infrastructure
• Provision of re-connection pathways through international cooperation
⑤ Such support shall serve the following purposes:
• Protection of good-faith economic actors
• Maintenance of global supply chain stability
• Facilitation of transition toward trust-based network structures
Article 11. System Integration & Framework Linkage
① This framework is structurally integrated with other core frameworks within the Global Collective Defense System.
② This framework shall be applied based on the results and analyses of the following frameworks:
• Aggression Determination Framework
• Actor Identification & Attribution Framework
• Responsibility Attribution & Allocation Framework
• Actor Isolation & Risk Ring-Fencing Protocol
③ Network containment and influence suppression measures under this framework shall be linked to the following subsequent response system:
• Collective Response Framework
④ Application and interpretation across frameworks shall follow these principles:
• Maintenance of temporal consistency
• Consistency in evidence-based assessments
• Proportionality between responsibility level and containment intensity
⑤ This framework does not operate independently, but functions as the system’s risk containment and structural stabilization phase.
