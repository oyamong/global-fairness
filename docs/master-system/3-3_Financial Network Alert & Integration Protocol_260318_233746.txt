Global Collective Defense System
PHASE 1: Operational Detection & Response Infrastructure
3-3. Financial Network Alert & Integration Protocol

Submitted by: Jungyun Kwon, Citizen of the Republic of Korea and Global Citizen
Date: March 19, 2026 (KST)

Article 1. Purpose
① This regulation defines a system for transmitting and integrating financial risk flags into the global financial network.
② The purposes are as follows:
• Sharing risk signals across the financial system
• Establishing structures for real-time response
• Activating market-based risk management
Article 2. Scope of Integration
① This regulation applies to the following institutions and systems:
• Banks and financial institutions
• Securities firms and investment institutions
• Exchanges and payment networks
• Virtual asset platforms
• International financial cooperation networks
② Integration shall be conducted at the level of a global network rather than a single jurisdiction.
③ In the virtual asset domain, integration may include the following structures:
• On-chain data analytics systems and specialized analytical entities
• Address-based risk identification and tracking systems
• Risk information sharing mechanisms among exchanges
④ Such integration shall remain consistent with virtual asset transfer information standards (e.g., the Travel Rule), and may be used for identifying and restricting high-risk addresses and networks.
Article 3. Financial Alert Definition
① A Financial Alert refers to a standardized risk notification message generated on the basis of a risk flag.
② The alert shall include the following information:
• Identification details of actors and networks
• Risk level
• Summary of associated structures
• Recommended response level
• Time of application
Article 4. Alert Transmission Mechanism
① Alerts shall be transmitted through the following methods:
• Real-time API-based transmission
• Standardized messaging protocols
• Secure authenticated channels
② All alerts shall incorporate integrity and anti-tampering mechanisms.
③ Where a false positive alert is identified, the following measures shall be taken:
• Immediate transmission of revocation messages to all integrated networks
• Restoration of prior alert status
• Rapid reassessment of the impact scope
• Recording and analysis of the cause of error
④ Emergency revocation procedures shall follow the principle of Expedited Recovery, and shall be applied consistently across the entire network.
Article 5. Integration Architecture
① Financial institutions may integrate with the system in the following ways:
• Automatic integration (automatic application of risk signals)
• Semi-automatic integration (application following internal review)
• Manual integration (use as reference information)
② The level of integration shall be determined in accordance with each institution’s policies and regulatory environment.
Article 6. Institutional Response Options
① Upon receiving alerts, financial institutions may take the following actions:
• Enhanced transaction monitoring
• Conducting additional due diligence
• Restricting or suspending transactions
• Monitoring asset movements
• Adjusting internal risk ratings
② All actions shall be based on the independent judgment of each institution.
Article 7. Risk Feedback Loop
① Financial institutions may provide feedback to the system, including:
• Changes in actual transaction data
• Observed risk incidents
• Suspicious transaction patterns
• Additional network linkage information
② Feedback shall be used to improve system learning and accuracy.
③ Institutions providing high-quality feedback may receive the following benefits:
• Improved system access conditions or cost reductions
• Assignment of a global risk cooperation rating
• Priority access to trust-based network participation
• Expanded access to joint analysis and shared intelligence
④ Such incentives aim to expand participation and enhance data quality.
Article 8. Interoperability Standards
① The system shall ensure interoperability across diverse financial networks.
② The following standards shall apply:
• Compliance with international data standards
• Harmonization of message structures
• API compatibility
③ Compatibility with existing financial infrastructure shall be maintained.
Article 9. Data Protection & Security
① All data transmission shall comply with the following standards:
• Encryption
• Access control
• Principle of data minimization
② Sensitive information shall be protected in accordance with legal standards and international norms.
Article 10. Safeguards & Stability
① The following principles shall apply to maintain financial system stability:
• Prevention of excessive market disruption
• Avoidance of liquidity crises
• Management of systemic risk
② Where large-scale impact is anticipated, alert application shall be implemented in a phased manner.
Article 11. Right to Clarification
① Entities subject to alerts shall have the right to clarification.
② Financial institutions may adjust their internal decisions based on such clarification.
Article 12. Human Final Authority
① High-risk alerts shall be subject to human review.
② Human decision-makers shall have the authority to:
• Suspend alert transmission
• Limit scope
• Adjust alert levels
• Approve exceptions
Article 13. Transparency & Auditability
① All alert generation and integration processes shall be recorded.
② Records shall include the following:
• Basis for alert generation
• Transmission pathways
• Receiving institutions
• Response outcomes
③ Records shall be used for ex post audit and international review.
Article 14. System Integration & Framework Linkage
① This regulation is structurally integrated with other core frameworks within the Global Collective Defense System.
② Alerts shall be generated and transmitted based on financial risk signals produced by the following frameworks:
• Actor-Based Financial Risk Flagging Framework
• Extended Network Risk Propagation Protocol
• Associated Actor Identification Protocol
③ Financial alerts under this regulation may be applied in conjunction with the following systems:
• Network Containment & Influence Suppression Framework
• Actor Isolation & Risk Ring-Fencing Protocol
④ Financial alerts shall be linked to the following subsequent response framework:
• Collective Response Framework
⑤ Application and interpretation across frameworks shall follow these principles:
• Consistency in evidence-based assessments
• Alignment between risk levels and response measures
• Maintenance of financial system stability and proportionality
⑥ This regulation does not constitute an independent sanctions mechanism, but functions as the system’s financial network-based risk transmission and response activation stage.
