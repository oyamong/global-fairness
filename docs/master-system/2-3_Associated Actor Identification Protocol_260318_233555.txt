2-3. Associated Actor Identification Protocol

Submitted by: Jungyun Kwon, Citizen of the Republic of Korea and Global Citizen
Date: March 19, 2026 (KST)

Article 1. Purpose
① This regulation defines the criteria and procedures for identifying directly and indirectly associated actors (Associated Actors) linked to acts of aggression or structurally threatening conduct.
② The purposes of this regulation are as follows:
• Preventing structures of responsibility evasion
• Identifying indirect support and beneficiary structures
• Preventing network-based risk propagation
Article 2. Definition of Associated Actors
① Associated Actors are defined as actors who:
• Do not directly participate in acts of aggression, but
• Contribute to the execution, maintenance, or benefit of such acts, or
• Are structurally connected to such conduct
② Associated Actors may be classified into the following categories:
• Supporting Actors
• Enabling Actors
• Beneficiary Actors
• Proxy Actors
③ Enabling Actors may include the following specialized service networks:
• Legal advisory and structural design services
• Accounting and auditing services
• Assistance in money laundering or tax structuring
• Public relations and external image management services
• Consulting and lobbying activities aimed at sanctions evasion
Article 3. Categories of Association
① Types of association shall be classified as follows:
• Financial Association
• Operational Association
• Technological Association
• Logistical Association
• Political or Strategic Association
② Where more than one type of association is identified, the degree of association shall be considered strengthened.
Article 4. Identification Criteria
① Identification of Associated Actors shall be based on the following criteria:
• Financial flows and transactional relationships
• Decision-making or command linkages
• Provision of technology or infrastructure
• Repeated patterns of cooperation
• Existence of shared benefit structures
② Identification shall be based on composite pattern analysis, not on a single piece of evidence.
Article 5. Levels of Association
① Associated Actors shall be classified into the following levels:
• Level 1: Direct Support
• Level 2: Indirect Support
• Level 3: Structural Association
• Level 4: Opportunistic Beneficiary
② Measures applied shall be differentiated according to each level.
Article 6. Network-Based Identification
① The system shall analyze networks centered on actors to identify Associated Actors.
② The following elements shall be subject to analysis:
• Transaction networks
• Organizational structures
• Cooperative relationships
• Influence pathways
③ Actors connected to Core Nodes shall be prioritized for analysis.
Article 7. Distinction and Safeguards
① Identification of Associated Actors shall follow these principles:
• Clear distinction from innocent civilians or general users
• Mere contact or incidental linkage shall not be considered association
• Prohibition of excessive or expansive interpretation
② Identification must include an assessment based on contribution to responsibility.
Article 8. Evidence Threshold
① Designation of an Associated Actor must meet the following criteria:
• At least two independent data sources
• Temporal and structural consistency
• Repetition or continuity
② Where there is a possibility of data error or misidentification, re-evaluation shall be conducted.
Article 9. Right to Clarification
① Entities identified as Associated Actors shall have the right to clarification.
② Clarification may include:
• Denial or explanation of association
• Argument for reduction of responsibility scope
• Assertion of technical misidentification
• Request for humanitarian exception
③ Where there is a significant possibility of error, immediate re-evaluation shall be conducted.
④ Self-reporting may be considered where the following conditions are met:
• Voluntary disclosure of association prior to formal identification
• Immediate cessation of the relevant conduct or relationship
• Provision of relevant information or network structure
⑤ Where self-reporting is recognized, the following measures may be applied:
• Deferral of designation
• Reduction in level of association classification
• Application of limited protective measures
⑥ Self-reporting does not imply exemption from responsibility, but is applied for the purpose of mitigating responsibility or facilitating structural disengagement.
Article 10. Human Final Authority
① Final designation of Associated Actors shall be subject to review by human decision-makers.
② Human decision-makers shall have the authority to:
• Revoke or suspend designation
• Narrow the scope
• Exclude protected subjects
• Request additional verification
③ Automated systems shall not independently finalize designation decisions.
Article 11. Transparency & Review
① All processes for identifying Associated Actors shall be recorded.
② The following information shall be included:
• Basis for identification
• Type of association
• Level of association
• Scope of application
③ Periodic review shall allow the following actions:
• Maintain
• Adjust
• Lift
④ Review results may be linked to the following systems:
• Network Containment & Influence Suppression Framework
• Actor Isolation & Risk Ring-Fencing Protocol
⑤ Where association levels change, the following measures may be automatically adjusted:
• Level of financial access restriction
• Adjustments to logistics and supply chain restrictions
• Expansion or reduction of network containment scope
⑥ Such integration shall operate as a Dynamic Blacklist system, allowing real-time reflection of changes in risk levels across the entire system.
Article 12. System Integration & Framework Linkage
① This regulation is structurally integrated with other core frameworks within the Global Collective Defense System.
② This regulation shall be applied based on data and analytical outputs from the following frameworks:
• Aggression Evidence & Monitoring Protocol
• Actor Identification & Attribution Framework
• Responsibility Attribution & Allocation Framework
③ Results of Associated Actor identification shall be directly linked to the following systems:
• Actor Isolation & Risk Ring-Fencing Protocol
• Network Containment & Influence Suppression Framework
④ Application and interpretation across frameworks shall follow these principles:
• Consistency in evidence-based assessments
• Alignment between levels of association and levels of responsibility
• Proportionality between risk level and response intensity
⑤ This regulation does not operate independently, but functions as the system’s foundational stage for identifying relational structures and preventing expansion of systemic risk.
