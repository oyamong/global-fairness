1-3. Aggression Determination Framework

Submitted by: Jungyun Kwon, Citizen of the Republic of Korea and Global Citizen
Date: March 19, 2026 (KST)

Article 1. Purpose
① This regulation establishes the procedures and criteria for fair and rapid determination of aggression.
② The purposes of this system are as follows:
• Objective determination of acts of aggression
• Provision of a basis for collective international response
• Strengthening deterrence against aggression
• Supporting the identification of international legal responsibility
③ This regulation is structurally linked to the following documents:
• Definition of Aggression
• Aggression Determination Authority Framework
• Collective Response Framework
④ This regulation operates as a non-punitive, determination-focused procedural system, and final legal responsibility shall be determined through separate judicial procedures.
⑤ Where issues of continuing harm or temporal continuity arise, this regulation shall maintain consistency with the Temporal Applicability & Continuing Harm Framework.
Article 2. Principles of Determination
① Determinations of aggression shall be conducted in accordance with the following principles:
• Evidence-based approach
• Political neutrality
• Timeliness
• Consistency with international law
② Determinations shall not be influenced by the political interests of any particular state.
③ Determinations shall be conducted by a decision-making structure composed of states not party to the conflict.
④ Determinations shall take into account the principles of proportionality, minimum intervention, and civilian protection.
⑤ The determination process shall ensure repeatability and verifiability.
Article 3. Determination Trigger
① The determination procedure may be initiated under the following circumstances:
• Occurrence of armed conflict
• Allegations of territorial violation
• Attacks on critical national infrastructure
• Large-scale cyber attacks
• Attacks on satellite or communication infrastructure
② Member states or international organizations may request case initiation.
③ In urgent situations, procedures may be initiated through automated detection systems.
④ Where initiated by automated detection systems, the following verification procedures shall be conducted:
• Initial review by technical verification bodies
• Independent analyst or expert validation
• Emergency review by the determination authority
⑤ Aggression determination shall not be made solely on the basis of automated detection results.
⑥ Automated detection results shall be subject to human verification under the Human-in-the-Loop principle.
⑦ Automated systems shall function as support tools only and shall not possess independent decision-making authority.
Article 4. Determination Process
① The determination procedure shall proceed through the following stages:
Case Submission
↓
Evidence Collection
↓
Technical Verification
↓
Actor Identification
↓
Legal Assessment
↓
Determination Announcement
② Each stage shall be recorded to enable international verification.
③ Outputs at each stage shall be structurally linked to the following documents:
• Legal Evidence Packaging Protocol
• Actor Identification & Attribution Framework
• Responsibility Attribution & Allocation Framework
④ Where issues of temporal continuity or continuing harm arise, the legal assessment stage shall be aligned with the Temporal Applicability & Continuing Harm Framework.
Article 5. Evidence Collection
① Determination shall be based on at least two independent sources of evidence.
② Recognized forms of evidence include:
• Satellite imagery
• Military movement records
• Radar and AIS data
• Communication and electronic signal records
• Cyber forensic evidence
• Field images and videos
• International monitoring reports
③ Evidence shall be managed in accordance with the Aggression Evidence & Monitoring Protocol.
④ The following verification processes shall be conducted at the technical verification stage:
• Deepfake detection
• Verification of image and video manipulation
• Data integrity and source verification
• Analysis of disinformation
⑤ All evidence shall comply with the Chain of Custody & Evidence Integrity Framework.
Article 6. Actor Identification
① Actors involved in aggression shall be identified under the following categories:
• Direct perpetrators
• Command and control actors
• Military support actors
• Technical and intelligence support actors
• Proxy actors
② Actor identification shall be conducted in accordance with the Actor Identification & Attribution Framework.
③ State responsibility may be presumed where the following conditions are met:
• Evidence indicates the use of state assets (servers, weapons, funding, etc.)
• The reliability of such evidence is assessed at 60% or higher
④ Such presumption implies:
• Not a final determination of responsibility
• A state of Provisional Attribution
• Subject to further verification and international investigation
⑤ Attribution assessments shall be aligned with:
• State Responsibility Differentiation & Attribution Framework
Article 7. Determination Criteria
① Determination of aggression shall consider the following factors collectively:
• Use of force or coercive measures
• Territorial violation
• Disruption of state functions
• Attacks on survival foundations
• Scale and persistence of the attack
② Criteria shall be interpreted in accordance with the Definition of Aggression.
③ In cases involving gray zone or proxy actors, additional factors shall be considered:
• Level of control
• Provision of support or protection
• Pattern and persistence
• Failure to prevent
④ Where continuing harm or long-term structural violations are at issue, criteria shall be interpreted in conjunction with the Temporal Applicability & Continuing Harm Framework.
Article 8. Determination Timeframe
① The timeframe for determination shall be as follows:
• Case intake → Immediate
• Technical verification → Within 6 hours
• Preliminary determination → Within 24 hours
• Final determination → Within 72 hours
② In urgent situations, immediate response recommendations may be issued.
③ A one-time extension may be granted under the following conditions:
• Cyber attacks requiring advanced technical analysis
• Complex multi-actor involvement
• Additional time required for forensic analysis
④ Extension conditions:
• Maximum extension: up to an additional 72 hours
• Reasons and scope must be recorded and disclosed
⑤ Timeframes shall be applied with consideration for the balance between speed and accuracy.
⑥ Where assessment of continuing harm or temporal continuity is required, additional review time may be allocated in accordance with the Temporal Applicability & Continuing Harm Framework.
Article 9. Emergency Determination Protocol
① In cases of large-scale aggression or urgent response needs, an Emergency Determination Procedure may be activated.
② Activation requires one or more of the following:
• Large-scale military attack
• Destruction of critical national infrastructure
• Mass civilian casualties
• Cyber attacks causing state-level functional paralysis
③ Emergency determination timeline:
• Initial assessment → Within 3 hours
• Emergency technical verification → Within 6 hours
• Preliminary aggression determination → Within 12 hours
④ Emergency determinations shall be treated as Provisional Determinations.
⑤ Such determinations may be subject to subsequent review and adjustment.

Article 11. Determination Outcome
① Determination results shall be categorized as follows:
• Level 1 — Clear Aggression
• Level 2 — Functional Aggression
• Level 3 — Significant Hostile Act
• Level 4 — Military Provocation or Conflict
② Determination outcomes shall be linked to the Collective Response Framework.
③ These levels do not automatically determine the intensity of response and shall be applied in accordance with separate response mechanisms.
Article 12. Judicial Evidence Transmission
① All materials collected during the determination process shall be preserved for judicial use.
② Evidence packages may be transmitted to:
• Domestic judicial authorities
• International Criminal Court (ICC)
• International Court of Justice (ICJ)
• Other competent courts or prosecutorial bodies
③ Evidence shall be maintained in a state ready for immediate submission upon request.
④ Chain of Custody principles shall apply.
⑤ This Article shall be aligned with:
• Legal Evidence Packaging Protocol
• Judicial Execution & Asset Recovery Protocol
Article 13. Transparency and Public Reporting
① Determination results may be disclosed for international verification.
② Disclosed materials may include:
• Satellite observation data
• Military movement records
• Damage reports
• Determination reports
③ Disclosure shall adhere to the following principles:
• Protection of civilian safety
• Protection of military confidentiality
④ The following methods may be applied to protect sensitive information:
• Data anonymization
• Source anonymization
• Removal of technical details
• Reduction of location and time precision
⑤ Disclosure levels shall be categorized as:
• Public Disclosure
• Restricted Technical Disclosure
• Classified Evidence
⑥ Classified materials shall be used only for:
• Internal verification
• Judicial proceedings
⑦ Disclosure shall prioritize civilian protection and prevention of misuse of information.
Article 14. Review Procedure
① Requests for review of determinations may be submitted.
② Requests must be filed within 7 days of the determination.
③ Review shall proceed only under the following condition:
• Submission of new evidence
④ The review process shall follow these principles:
• Independent assessment
• Comparative verification with prior determination
• Transparent record-keeping
