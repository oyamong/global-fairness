PHASE 1: Operational Detection & Response Infrastructure
1-10. Human Final Authority Principle

Date: March 19, 2026 (KST)

Article 1. Principle of Human Final Authority
① The ultimate decision-making authority for all significant determinations within the Global Collective Defense System shall be vested in human decision-makers.
② Automated systems, artificial intelligence-based analytical systems, and data-driven risk detection systems shall function solely as supportive tools for decision-making and shall not possess independent final decision-making authority.
③ The following determinations shall be made exclusively by authorized human decision-makers:
• Final determination of acts of aggression
• Initiation of collective response measures
• Authorization and implementation of economic and financial sanctions
• Actor isolation and risk containment measures
• Final determination of liability and reparations responsibility
• Approval of humanitarian exceptions
④ The structure of final human decision-making shall not be concentrated in a single entity and shall be organized as a distributed system based on multi-jurisdictional and multi-institutional participation.
⑤ No State, institution, or individual shall possess exclusive final decision-making authority.
⑥ No technology, system, authority, or institutional arrangement shall supersede human life, dignity, fundamental rights, or the rule of law.
⑦ Decision-making procedures among multiple jurisdictions and institutions shall be defined in separate procedural regulations and shall be designed to ensure effective and functional decision-making.
Article 2. Human-in-the-Loop Governance
① All automated analytical and enforcement-related systems shall operate under a Human-in-the-Loop governance structure.
② The functions of automated systems shall be limited to the following:
• Risk detection
• Data analysis
• Structuring and organization of evidence
• Analysis of responsibility structures
• Presentation of policy and response options
③ Automated systems shall not independently execute sanctions, isolation measures, military responses, or any form of coercive action.
④ Notwithstanding the foregoing, in situations where a clear and imminent high-risk condition is identified, automated systems may implement temporary protective measures to the minimum extent necessary, provided that human review and authorization are conducted without delay thereafter.
Article 3. Principle of Proportionality
① All enforcement actions, sanctions, isolation measures, and risk containment measures shall be applied in proportion to the severity of the act, the scale of harm, and the level of responsibility.
② The following factors shall be comprehensively considered in such determinations:
• The scale and nature of the act of aggression
• The level of civilian harm
• The degree of direct involvement and responsibility of the actor
• The risk of further harm escalation
③ All measures shall be strictly limited to what is necessary and shall not result in excessive or indiscriminate harm.
Article 4. Humanitarian Protection Filter
① The system shall apply a humanitarian protection filter throughout both automated and human decision-making processes to ensure the protection of civilians and vulnerable populations.
② The following areas shall be recognized as specially protected sectors:
• Food supply
• Essential medical services and pharmaceuticals
• Emergency response systems
• Water and sanitation
• Minimum access to communication
③ Enforcement measures shall be adjusted or suspended where any of the following conditions arise or are reasonably anticipated:
• Collapse of access to food or medical services
• Large-scale violations of civilian survival rights
• Obstruction of humanitarian assistance
• Disproportionate harm to vulnerable populations
Article 5. Distinction Between Actor and Population
① All enforcement measures shall clearly distinguish between actors involved in aggression and the general civilian population.
② Priority enforcement shall be directed toward the following:
• State-responsible actors
• Command and control structures
• Providers of military or material support
• Structures deriving direct benefit from acts of aggression
③ Collective sanctions or discriminatory measures imposed upon the civilian population as a whole shall be prohibited.
Article 6. Right to Explanation and Clarification
① Subjects of enforcement measures shall have the right to provide explanations and clarifications regarding their rights and responsibilities.
② Such submissions may include:
• Rebuttal of factual findings
• Claims for limitation of responsibility
• Concerns regarding civilian harm
• Claims of data errors or misidentification
• Requests for humanitarian exceptions
③ Submission of explanations shall not automatically suspend enforcement; however, where substantial errors or risks of excessive enforcement are identified, a review procedure shall be initiated without delay.
Article 7. Correction and Compensation for Misidentification
① Any misattribution of actors resulting from technical or analytical errors shall be corrected without delay.
② Upon confirmation of such error, the following measures shall be taken:
• Immediate suspension of enforcement measures
• Correction of relevant records
• Initiation of reputation restoration procedures
• Implementation of remedial measures
③ Where significant harm has occurred, appropriate compensation procedures may be considered and applied.
④ Compensation may include:
• Lifting of sanctions
• Restoration of financial access
• Correction of official records
• Provision of official explanations or apologies
⑤ This Article constitutes an essential component for maintaining the reliability and legitimacy of the system.
Article 8. Humanitarian Deferral Authority
① Human decision-makers may defer enforcement measures where humanitarian grounds are recognized.
② Grounds for deferral may include:
• Threats to survival rights
• Risk of collapse of medical or food systems
• Risk of obstruction of humanitarian assistance
• Risk of excessive harm
③ Deferral may be implemented through:
• Postponement of enforcement
• Reduction of scope
• Granting of exceptions
• Phased implementation
④ Deferral shall not extinguish underlying responsibility.
Article 9. Humanitarian Exception Window
① The system may establish procedures for humanitarian exceptions.
② The scope of such exceptions may include:
• Provision of medical supplies
• Supply of food and water
• Civilian evacuation
• Protection of vulnerable populations
③ Such exceptions shall not be used for military or strategic advantage.
Article 10. Ethical Oversight
① Human decision-makers shall take into account the following considerations:
• International law and international human rights standards
• The principle of proportionality
• Protection of civilians
• Humanitarian principles
• Global stability
② Human decision-makers shall retain the authority to independently assess and reject recommendations generated by automated systems.
③ Human judgment shall not be mechanically bound by data outputs.
④ Decisions shall not be used for political purposes, economic gain, or discrimination against specific groups.
⑤ Where repeated bias or abuse is identified, the authority of the decision-maker may be restricted or subject to review.
Article 11. Accountability of Human Decisions
① Human decision-makers shall bear responsibility for their final determinations.
② All decisions shall include the following elements:
• Grounds for the decision
• Evidence relied upon
• Assessment of proportionality
• Humanitarian review
③ All decisions and related records shall be preserved.
④ Such records shall be maintained in a manner that enables independent verification.
⑤ Where a decision is determined to have materially violated clear evidence or objective standards, it may be subject to an independent review process.
⑥ Where repeated or significant bias or abuse of authority is identified, the authority of the decision-maker may be restricted or subject to reassessment, and, where appropriate, a responsibility determination process may be initiated.
⑦ Such procedures shall operate as a system of checks and balances to ensure fairness, independence, and integrity.
Article 12. Periodic Reassessment
① All enforcement measures shall be subject to periodic reassessment.
② The outcome of such reassessment may include:
• Continuation
• Reduction
• Deferral
• Termination
• Expansion of exceptions
③ The following factors shall be considered in reassessment:
• Level of civilian harm
• Humanitarian access conditions
• Effectiveness of enforcement
• Side effects and secondary impacts
④ Where significant civilian harm or humanitarian crises are identified, an automatic review process shall be initiated without delay.
