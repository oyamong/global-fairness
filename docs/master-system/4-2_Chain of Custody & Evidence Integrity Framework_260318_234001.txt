4-2. Chain of Custody & Evidence Integrity Framework

Submitted by: Jungyun Kwon, Citizen of the Republic of Korea and Global Citizen
Date: March 19, 2026 (KST)

Article 1. Purpose
① This framework defines a system to ensure that all collected evidence maintains integrity and continuity from creation to submission.
② The purposes are as follows:
• Preventing evidence tampering and alteration
• Ensuring legal admissibility of evidence
• Securing post hoc verifiability
Article 2. Definition of Chain of Custody
① Chain of Custody refers to a system in which all state changes and access histories of evidence are recorded and managed throughout the following stages:
• Collection
• Storage
• Access
• Analysis
• Transfer
• Submission
② All stages shall be continuously connected without interruption.
Article 3. Definition of Evidence Integrity
① Evidence integrity refers to the following:
• Data remains in its original state
• Any modification is verifiable
• The source is clearly identifiable
② Integrity shall be ensured through both technical and procedural mechanisms.
Article 4. Evidence Registration
① All evidence shall be registered immediately upon collection.
② Registration shall include:
• Unique identification ID
• Time and location of collection
• Collecting entity
• Type of evidence
• Original state information
③ Once registered, evidence becomes subject to tracking.
④ At the time of registration, evidence may include linkages to:
• Events
• Actors
• Affected subjects (States or Protected Entities)
Article 5. Immutable Record System
① All evidence records shall be stored in a tamper-resistant manner.
② The following technologies may be utilized:
• Hash-based verification
• Distributed ledger structures
• Timestamp authentication
③ Any attempt to modify records shall be automatically logged.
④ Distributed storage may include:
• Use of geographically distributed nodes
• Minimization of dependence on any single state or institution
• Maintenance of recoverable distributed architecture
⑤ Cryptographic design may consider:
• Long-term security resilience
• Adaptability to future technological developments
• Gradual upgrade and transition of cryptographic algorithms
Article 6. Access Control
① Access to evidence shall be strictly controlled.
② The following standards shall apply:
• Role-based access control
• Multi-factor authentication
• Logging of all access activities
③ Unauthorized access shall trigger immediate alerts and recording.
Article 7. Evidence Handling Protocol
① Evidence handling shall follow these procedures:
• Recording of access authorization
• Specification of purpose of handling
• Recording of any modifications
• Documentation of handling outcomes
② All handling processes shall be fully traceable.
Article 8. Transfer & Transmission
① The following standards shall apply when evidence is transferred:
• Encrypted transmission
• Sender and receiver logging
• Recording of transmission pathways
• Confirmation of receipt
② Integrity of data during transmission must be verifiable.
Article 9. Verification Mechanism
① Evidence integrity shall be verifiable at any time.
② Verification methods include:
• Hash value comparison
• Cross-checking with original data
• Log consistency verification
③ Any inconsistency shall trigger immediate investigation.
Article 10. Tampering Detection
① The system shall automatically detect the following:
• Attempts to alter data
• Unauthorized access attempts
• Record inconsistencies
• Abnormal access patterns
② Suspected tampering shall trigger immediate alerts.
Article 11. Preservation Duration
① Evidence shall be retained for extended periods in accordance with legal requirements.
② The following standards shall apply:
• Retention for a defined period after case closure
• Compliance with international legal standards
• Consideration of future review needs
③ Data processing procedures may be applied when:
• Legal determination is finalized
• Purpose of retention is no longer valid
• Personal data protection requirements arise
④ In such cases, the following measures may be applied:
• Complete deletion or anonymization
• Irreversible data removal
• Retention of records documenting deletion processes
⑤ These procedures ensure compliance with data minimization and privacy protection principles.
Article 12. Human Oversight
① Critical evidence handling and modification shall require human review.
② Human decision-makers shall have the authority to:
• Approve or restrict access
• Authorize evidence processing
• Initiate investigations into anomalies
Article 13. Audit & Accountability
① All evidence management processes shall be subject to audit.
② Audit scope shall include:
• Access logs
• Modification history
• Processing activities
• Transmission records
③ Accountability tracking must be ensured.
Article 14. System Integration
① This framework shall be integrated with:
• Legal Evidence Packaging Protocol
• Financial Monitoring Systems
• Network Analysis Systems
② Data consistency across systems shall be maintained.
③ This framework supports alignment of evidence with:
• Definition of Aggression frameworks
• Impact structures involving Protected Entities
• Responsibility and asset analysis systems
Article 15. System Integration & Cross-Framework Integrity
① This framework is fully integrated with all data generation, analysis, and response systems within the Global Collective Defense System.
② It ensures integrity and continuity for all data generated by:
• Legal Evidence Packaging Protocol
• Actor Identification & Attribution Framework
• Associated Actor Identification Protocol
③ Financial and asset-related data generated by the following systems shall remain verifiable through this framework:
• Influence-Linked Asset Monitoring Framework
• Financial Network Alert & Integration Protocol
• Extended Network Risk Propagation Protocol
④ All actions and response records generated by the following systems shall be tracked and preserved:
• Conditional Asset Restriction Protocol
• Network Containment & Influence Suppression Framework
• Actor Isolation & Risk Ring-Fencing Protocol
⑤ This framework guarantees the following across all data flows:
• Continuous traceability from creation to submission
• Temporal and logical consistency across datasets
• Complete recording of all modifications and access events
⑥ This framework functions as the system’s
Core Integrity Layer ensuring legal evidentiary reliability across the entire architecture.
