Global Collective Defense System
PHASE 1: Operational Detection & Response Infrastructure
2-1. Actor Isolation & Risk Ring-Fencing Protocol

Submitted by: Jungyun Kwon, Citizen of the Republic of Korea and Global Citizen
Date: March 19, 2026 (KST)

Article 1. Purpose
① This regulation defines isolation measures intended to prevent additional harm and the spread of risk arising from actors and related networks determined to have engaged in aggression or structurally threatening conduct.
② The purpose of this regulation is not punishment, but rather to contain the spread of risk and preserve global stability.
Article 2. Scope of Application
① This regulation applies to the following:
• States, organizations, and individuals determined to be actors of aggression
• Command and control actors directly involved in such conduct
• Actors providing material, technical, or financial support
• Networks deriving structural benefit from acts of aggression
② Indirectly connected actors may also fall within the scope of application where the possibility of risk propagation is established.
Article 3. Principle of Targeted Isolation
① Isolation measures shall be applied on a Targeted Actor-Based basis.
② The following principles shall apply:
• Prohibition of collective disadvantage imposed upon the civilian population as a whole
• Differential application according to the level of responsibility
• Application limited to the minimum necessary scope
③ Broad restrictions imposed on the general population shall not be permitted.
Article 4. Risk Ring-Fencing Mechanism
① The system shall implement risk ring-fencing centered on determined actors in the following domains:
• Financial access and asset flows
• Access to technology and infrastructure
• Information and network influence
• Access to international cooperation and institutional systems
② Risk ring-fencing shall be implemented in the following stages:
• Level 1: Warning and enhanced monitoring
• Level 2: Limited access controls
• Level 3: Isolation of high-risk domains
• Level 4: Structural blocking and network separation
③ Restriction of financial access may include the following measures:
• Restriction of access to traditional financial systems
• Blocking of international payment networks
• Asset freezing and transfer restrictions
④ In order to block circumvention through digital assets and decentralized finance (DeFi), the following measures may be applied:
• Blacklisting of high-risk wallet addresses
• Blockchain analytics-based transaction tracing
• Detection and blocking of mixers and indirect routing paths
• Access restrictions through centralized exchanges (CEXs) and cooperating institutions
• Access restrictions on high-risk smart contracts and DeFi protocols
Article 5. Network-Based Isolation
① Isolation measures shall be applied not only to individual actors, but also on the basis of connected network structures.
② The following elements shall be subject to analysis:
• Financial flows
• Command and control structures
• Technology and logistics networks
• Political and economic influence linkages
③ The system shall identify Core Nodes within the network and prioritize them for restriction.
④ The following circumvention structures shall remain under continuous monitoring:
• Financial structures based in tax havens
• Paper companies and nominee corporations
• Sham contracts and indirect investment structures
• Multi-layered money laundering networks
⑤ Where circumvention linkages are identified, the system shall be authorized to:
• Immediately expand the scope of isolation
• Automatically include newly identified nodes
• Reconstruct and re-block the network at the network level
Article 6. Proportionality & Safeguards
① All isolation measures shall be applied in accordance with the principle of proportionality.
② The following protective standards shall apply:
• Prohibition of infringement on civilian survival rights
• Maintenance of essential infrastructure
• Guarantee of humanitarian access
③ Where the risk of excessive isolation is detected, the system may automatically adjust or suspend the relevant measures.
Article 7. Humanitarian Exception
① The following areas shall be exceptionally protected from isolation measures:
• Food and water supply
• Essential medical care and medicines
• Emergency rescue and evacuation
• Minimum communications necessary for civilian survival
② Such exceptions shall not be diverted for military or political advantage.
Article 8. Human Final Authority
① All significant isolation measures shall be subject to human review in accordance with the Human Final Authority Principle.
② Human decision-makers shall have the authority to:
• Reduce the scope of isolation
• Defer the timing of application
• Approve humanitarian exceptions
• Exclude specific subjects
③ Automated systems shall not independently finalize isolation measures.
Article 9. Transparency & Review
① All isolation measures shall be recorded and shall include the following information:
• Subject of application
• Scope of application
• Basis for application
• Proportionality assessment
② Periodic review shall be conducted, and the following actions may be taken:
• Maintain
• Mitigate
• Lift
• Redesign
③ The minimum conditions for lifting isolation measures may include the following:
• Cessation of aggression or attack conduct
• Withdrawal of, or confirmation of absence of, further attack plans
• Cooperation in freezing or tracing related assets
• Cooperation with international investigation or verification procedures
• Implementation of concrete measures to prevent further spread of harm
④ Fulfillment of the above conditions shall be assessed through technical verification and multi-source data analysis.
⑤ Where such conditions are met, isolation measures may be gradually relaxed.
Article 10. Non-Punitive Nature
① These isolation measures do not replace criminal punishment or sanctions.
② This regulation shall be regarded as a preventive measure aimed at risk management and preventing risk propagation.
Article 11. System Integration & Framework Linkage
① This regulation is structurally linked with other core frameworks within the Global Collective Defense System.
② This regulation shall be applied on the basis of the results and analyses of the following frameworks:
• Aggression Determination Framework
• Actor Identification & Attribution Framework
• Responsibility Attribution & Allocation Framework
③ Isolation and risk ring-fencing measures under this regulation shall be linked to the following subsequent response framework:
• Collective Response Framework
④ Application and interpretation across these frameworks shall follow the following principles:
• Maintenance of temporal consistency
• Maintenance of consistency in evidence-based assessment
• Maintenance of proportionality between the level of responsibility and the level of isolation
⑤ This regulation does not operate independently, but functions as part of the overall system’s risk containment and response-preparedness stage.
