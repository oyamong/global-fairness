3-5. Conditional Asset Restriction Protocol

Submitted by: Jungyun Kwon, Citizen of the Republic of Korea and Global Citizen
Date: March 19, 2026 (KST)

Article 1. Purpose
① This regulation defines the criteria and procedures for applying Conditional Asset Restrictions based on the results of influence-linked asset monitoring.
② The purposes are as follows:
• Restricting the movement of high-risk assets
• Preventing risk propagation
• Maintaining financial system stability
Article 2. Definition of Conditional Restriction
① Conditional Asset Restriction refers to measures of limited control applied to specific assets or asset networks based on defined conditions, rather than full blocking.
② It has the following characteristics:
• Gradual application
• Reversible (subject to release) structure
• Proportionality-based adjustment
Article 3. Scope of Application
① This regulation applies to:
• Assets of high-risk actors
• Assets within associated networks
• Influence-linked asset structures
• Assets subject to risk propagation
② Application may be conducted at the level of asset networks rather than individual assets.
Article 4. Trigger Conditions
① Restrictions shall be triggered where one or more of the following conditions are met:
• Financial Risk Flag at High Risk level or above
• Confirmation of network-based risk propagation
• Attempted concealment or movement of assets
• Identification of influence-linked asset structures
② Determinations shall be based on automated system analysis, but final confirmation shall require Human Final Authority review.
Article 5. Types of Restriction
① Restrictions may be applied in the following forms:
• Transaction Limitation
• Transfer Delay
• High-Risk Transaction Blocking
• Conditional Approval Requirements
• Access Restriction
② Restrictions shall be applied in a phased manner.
③ Where attempts to circumvent restrictions are detected, the following measures may be applied:
• Immediate risk reassessment upon detection of nominee accounts or indirect structures
• Blocking of virtual asset-based circumvention pathways
• Escalation of restriction levels across the entire associated network
• Consideration of highest-level application (Level 4) in cases of significant evasion
Article 6. Restriction Levels
① Restriction levels shall be classified as follows:
• Level 1: Monitoring Restriction
• Level 2: Conditional Restriction
• Level 3: Partial Restriction
• Level 4: Strategic Restriction
② Levels shall be adjusted according to risk levels and impact.
Article 7. Network-Based Restriction
① Restrictions may be applied not only to individual assets but to entire connected asset networks.
② The following criteria shall apply:
• Financial flow connections
• Joint investment structures
• Influence-based linkages
• Repeated transactional relationships
③ The scope of restriction shall be adjusted in accordance with the principle of proportionality.
Article 8. Conditional Release Mechanism
① Restricted assets may be released upon fulfillment of the following conditions:
• Acceptance of clarification
• Verification of risk resolution
• Separation from high-risk networks
• Completion of legal or judicial procedures
② Release shall be conducted in stages.
③ Assets subject to potential dispute may be managed as follows:
• Custodial arrangements through trusted institutions
• Temporary segregation and neutral management
• Restoration and return upon resolution of risk
④ These mechanisms serve the following purposes:
• Secure preservation of assets
• Ensuring trust among stakeholders
• Preventing unnecessary economic loss
⑤ In the case of virtual assets, automated release mechanisms may include:
• Smart contract-based release upon fulfillment of conditions
• On-chain data-based condition verification
• Predefined release triggers
Article 9. Safeguards & Proportionality
① Restrictions shall adhere to the following principles:
• Prohibition of excessive measures
• Protection of legitimate economic activity
• Protection of essential assets
• Maintenance of global supply chain stability
② Humanitarian exceptions may be applied where necessary.
Article 10. Right to Clarification
① Subjects of restriction shall have the right to clarification.
② Clarification may include:
• Denial of asset control
• Explanation of transaction legitimacy
• Denial of network association
• Assertion of technical error
③ Restrictions may be adjusted or lifted based on clarification outcomes.
④ Where data errors or misidentification are identified during clarification, the following procedures may be applied:
• Immediate reassessment of restriction measures
• Temporary relaxation or provisional lifting of restrictions
• Post-verification and reporting procedures
⑤ These procedures shall operate as a rapid response mechanism to minimize unjust harm.
Article 11. Human Final Authority
① Restrictions at Level 3 or above shall require human review.
② Human decision-makers shall have the authority to:
• Suspend restrictions
• Reduce scope
• Exclude specific assets
• Approve emergency release
Article 12. Transparency & Auditability
① All restriction measures shall be recorded.
② Records shall include:
• Basis for restriction
• Scope of application
• Duration
• Conditions for release
③ Records shall be used for ex post audit and as evidence in legal proceedings.
Article 13. System Integration & Framework Linkage
① This regulation is structurally integrated with other core frameworks within the Global Collective Defense System.
② Restrictions shall be applied based on asset and risk information identified through:
• Influence-Linked Asset Monitoring Framework
• Actor-Based Financial Risk Flagging Framework
• Extended Network Risk Propagation Protocol
③ Information on asset restrictions may be transmitted to the financial system through:
• Financial Network Alert & Integration Protocol
④ Restriction measures may be applied in conjunction with:
• Network Containment & Influence Suppression Framework
• Actor Isolation & Risk Ring-Fencing Protocol
⑤ The intensity and scope of restrictions shall remain aligned with:
• Responsibility Attribution & Allocation Framework
⑥ This regulation does not constitute an independent sanctions mechanism, but functions as the system’s stage for asset-based risk control and prevention of risk propagation.
