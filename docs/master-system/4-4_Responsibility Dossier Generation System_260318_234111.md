4-4. Responsibility Dossier Generation System

Submitted by: Jungyun Kwon, Citizen of the Republic of Korea and Global Citizen
Date: March 19, 2026 (KST)

Article 1. Purpose
① This system defines a framework for generating Responsibility Dossiers that structurally consolidate accountability at the individual actor level in relation to acts of aggression and associated networks.
② The purposes are as follows:
• Preventing responsibility evasion structures
• Clarifying accountability at the actor level
• Structuring information for judicial utilization
Article 2. Definition of Responsibility Dossier
① A Responsibility Dossier refers to a structured file that integrates the following information for a specific actor:
• Identity and identification data
• Records of actions
• Network linkage structures
• Asset and influence structures
• Responsibility levels and contribution
② The dossier is not a simple record, but an analytical document supporting legal accountability determination.
Article 3. Scope of Subjects
① Dossiers shall be generated for the following subjects:
• Aggressor actors
• Associated actors
• Supporting and enabling actors
• Beneficiary actors
• Core nodes within networks
② Individuals, organizations, and legal entities are all included.
③ This scope may be extended to include:
• States
• Actors connected to Protected Entities
• Actors impacting populations or communities
Article 4. Dossier Structure
① Each Responsibility Dossier shall be structured as follows:
1. Identity Profile
• Identification of individuals or entities
• Associated accounts and identifiers
• Scope of activities
2. Actor Role Classification
• Direct actors
• Indirect actors
• Supporting actors
• Beneficiaries
3. Action Timeline
• Key event timestamps
• Patterns of continuous activity
• Linkages to incidents
4. Network Mapping
• Connected actors
• Influence pathways
• Core network nodes
5. Asset & Financial Linkages
• Asset structures
• Financial flows
• Investment and benefit structures
6. Responsibility Attribution
• Types of responsibility
• Allocation ratios
• Contribution analysis
7. Legal Qualification
• Applicable legal elements
• Relevant legal categories
• Potential legal liability
Article 5. Generation Criteria
① A dossier shall be generated when the following conditions are met:
• Actor identification is completed
• Network linkage is confirmed
• Minimum threshold of responsibility contribution is established
② Generation shall be based on automated analysis, but subject to Human Final Authority review.
Article 6. Dynamic Update Mechanism
① A dossier is not static.
② It shall be updated in the following circumstances:
• Acquisition of new evidence
• Changes in network structures
• Asset movements
• Alterations in responsibility structures
③ All updates shall be recorded.
Article 7. Evidence Integration
① The dossier shall be directly linked to:
• Legal Evidence Packages
• Chain of Custody records
• Financial risk data
• Network analysis outputs
② All information shall be connected in a verifiable manner.
③ Evidence integration shall maintain consistency with:
• Definition of Aggression frameworks
• Impact structures involving Protected Entities
• Events and scope of impact
Article 8. Multi-Actor Correlation
① Individual dossiers shall be interconnected.
② The system shall enable analysis of:
• Co-perpetration structures
• Organizational hierarchies
• Influence systems
③ Each actor’s position within the overall network shall be mapped.
④ Structural analysis may include:
• Command hierarchies and decision-making lines
• Functional role segmentation
• Identification of asset and power concentration points
Article 9. Responsibility Levels
① Responsibility shall be classified into the following levels:
• Level 1: Peripheral
• Level 2: Supporting
• Level 3: Core Involvement
• Level 4: Primary Responsibility
② Judicial implications vary according to the level.
③ The following factors shall be considered in determining responsibility levels:
• Intent
• Continuity and repetition
• Scope of influence
• Contribution to harm
④ Harm contribution may include impact on:
• States
• Protected Entities
• Populations and communities
Article 10. Safeguards & Fairness
① The following principles shall apply:
• Prohibition of excessive attribution of responsibility
• Protection of innocent actors
• Maintenance of proportionality
• Data-driven determination
② Responsibility shall not be assigned based solely on association.
③ Determination of responsibility shall include:
• Consideration of exculpatory evidence
• Analysis of mitigating factors
• Distinction between intentional and incidental association
Article 11. Right to Clarification
① Actors shall have the right to clarification.
② Clarification may include:
• Denial of role
• Claims for reduced responsibility
• Denial of network association
• Identification of data errors
③ The dossier shall be modified based on clarification outcomes.
④ Clarification results may lead to:
• Downgrading of responsibility level
• Removal of specific entries
• Reassessment of network linkages
• Change of dossier status
Article 12. Human Final Authority
① Finalization of dossiers shall require human review.
② Human decision-makers shall have the authority to:
• Suspend dossier generation
• Adjust responsibility levels
• Exclude specific elements
• Request additional investigation
Article 13. Usage & Submission
① Dossiers may be used for:
• Investigative support
• Prosecutorial materials
• Judicial evidence
• International legal submission
② Dossiers shall be maintained in a state of immediate submission readiness.
③ In multi-jurisdictional submissions, the following principles may apply:
• Structural consistency across jurisdictions
• Adjustment of scope per receiving authority
• Format adaptation to legal requirements
Article 14. Transparency & Auditability
① All dossier generation and modification processes shall be recorded.
② Records shall include:
• Basis for generation
• Modification history
• Review records
③ Records shall be used for post hoc audit and legal verification.
Article 15. System Integration & Accountability Mapping
① This system is fully integrated with all analytical and evidence-generation frameworks within the Global Collective Defense System.
② Responsibility Dossiers shall be generated based on actor and association data from:
• Actor Identification & Attribution Framework
• Associated Actor Identification Protocol
③ Responsibility analysis shall remain aligned with:
• Responsibility Attribution & Allocation Framework
④ Asset and financial data generated by the following systems shall be integrated into dossiers:
• Influence-Linked Asset Monitoring Framework
• Financial Network Alert & Integration Protocol
⑤ Evidence and integrity data generated by the following frameworks shall be incorporated:
• Legal Evidence Packaging Protocol
• Chain of Custody & Evidence Integrity Framework
⑥ Responsibility Dossiers generated by this system may be submitted to judicial authorities through:
• Multi-Jurisdiction Judicial Submission Protocol
⑦ This system functions as the system’s
Core Accountability Layer, finalizing actor-level responsibility structures in a form suitable for judicial use.
