PHASE 1: Operational Detection & Response Infrastructure
1-4. Aggression Determination Authority Framework

Submitted by: Jungyun Kwon, Citizen of the Republic of Korea and Global Citizen
Date: March 19, 2026 (KST)

Article 1. Purpose
① This document defines the structure and authority of the international body responsible for verifying and determining acts of aggression.
② The purposes of this authority are as follows:
• Objective verification of aggression-related incidents
• Ensuring determination procedures independent from political influence
• Providing reliable determinations for collective international response
• Establishing an evidentiary system for international judicial processes
③ This authority is structurally linked to the following documents:
• Definition of Aggression
• Aggression Determination Framework
• Collective Response Framework
④ This authority operates as a non-punitive, verification-focused institution, and final legal responsibility shall be determined through judicial procedures.
Article 2. Institutional Status
① This authority shall operate as an Independent Aggression Verification Authority.
② It shall function as an evidence-based verification body, not a political institution.
③ Its functions include:
• Verification of aggression-related evidence
• Conducting technical analysis
• Identification of actors
• Preparation of aggression determination reports
④ The authority shall not be subject to control by any specific state or bloc.
Article 3. Institutional Structure
① The authority shall consist of the following divisions:
• Technical Verification Division
• Evidence & Data Analysis Division
• Actor Attribution Division
• Legal Evidence Division
• Transparency & Reporting Division
② The roles of each division are as follows:
• Technical Verification Division
• Satellite data analysis
• Military movement analysis
• Cyber attack technical analysis
• Electronic signal analysis
• Evidence & Data Analysis Division
• Case data collection
• Image and video verification
• Cross-data validation
• Analysis of international monitoring data
• Actor Attribution Division
• Identification of perpetrators
• State responsibility analysis
• Proxy actor analysis
• Command structure analysis
• Legal Evidence Division
• Structuring legal evidence
• Preparation of evidence packages for international judicial use
• Evidence preservation management
• Transparency & Reporting Division
• Preparation of determination reports
• Management of public disclosure materials
• Coordination with international organizations
③ Each division shall maintain operational independence, while data shall be managed in an integrated manner.
Article 4. Neutrality Safeguards
① The authority shall maintain strict political neutrality.
② The following principles shall apply:
• Exclusion of states party to the conflict
• Exclusion of states from the same continental region as the conflict parties
• Guarantee of independence in the determination process
③ Institutional composition shall follow these criteria:
• Regional balance
• Military expertise
• Expertise in international law
④ These criteria shall be continuously applied throughout all determination processes.
Article 5. Real-Time Monitoring Authority
① The authority may operate a continuous monitoring system for detecting aggression.
② The monitoring system may include:
• Satellite surveillance networks
• Maritime and aviation movement monitoring
• Cyber attack detection systems
• Global communications data analysis
③ The system may cooperate with the following sources:
• State-provided monitoring data
• Open-source intelligence (OSINT)
• Private satellite operators
• Commercial aviation and maritime data providers
• Independent research and technical analysis institutions
④ The system shall function as a real-time monitoring system, not solely as a post-incident investigation mechanism.
⑤ The system shall operate within limits that do not unduly infringe upon civilian infrastructure and individual rights.
Article 6. Evidence Authority
① The authority may access the following data for determination purposes:
• Satellite observation data
• Maritime and aviation movement records
• Cyber attack logs
• Communications and electronic signal data
② Member states may cooperate in providing relevant data where necessary.
③ The authority may utilize the following sources to ensure objectivity and multi-layer verification:
• State-provided data
• International organization data
• Open-source data
• Private technical and satellite data
• Reports from independent analytical institutions
④ All evidence shall comply with:
• Aggression Evidence & Monitoring Protocol
• Chain of Custody & Evidence Integrity Framework
Article 7. Aggression Determination Council
① An Aggression Determination Council shall be established.
② The Council shall perform the following functions:
• Review of technical analysis results
• Verification of evidentiary reliability
• Determination of aggression
③ The Council shall consist of 9 or 11 independent members.
④ Composition principles include:
• Exclusion of nationals from states party to the conflict
• Exclusion of nationals from the same continental region as conflict parties
• Regional balance representation
• Expertise in military affairs and international law
⑤ Decision-making procedures:
• Ordinary cases: simple majority
• Major cases: two-thirds majority
⑥ Members shall exercise independent judgment and shall not receive external instructions.
Article 8. Emergency Determination Panel
① An Emergency Determination Panel may be convened in urgent situations.
② The panel shall consist of:
• Five members
• Including the head of technical verification
③ The panel may issue a preliminary determination within 24 hours.
④ Emergency determinations shall be subject to subsequent verification.
Article 9. Judicial Evidence Transmission
① Evidence collected during determination procedures shall be preserved for judicial use.
② Evidence packages may be transmitted to:
• Domestic judicial authorities
• International Criminal Court (ICC)
• International Court of Justice (ICJ)
• Other competent courts or prosecutorial authorities
③ Evidence shall be maintained in a state ready for immediate submission upon request.
④ Chain of Custody principles shall apply.
⑤ This Article shall be aligned with:
• Legal Evidence Packaging Protocol
• Judicial Execution & Asset Recovery Protocol
Article 10. Transparency and Reporting
① A certain level of disclosure may be made to ensure credibility.
② Disclosable materials include:
• Summary of determination reports
• Satellite imagery
• Attack pathway analysis
③ Disclosure shall adhere to:
• Protection of civilian safety
• Protection of military confidentiality
④ Disclosure shall be conducted in consideration of preventing misuse of information and maintaining international stability.
Article 11. Operational Independence and Funding
① The authority shall operate independently from political influence of any specific state.
② Funding may be secured through:
• Member state contributions proportional to GDP
• Joint international funds
• Confiscated assets of aggressor states
③ Suspension of contributions by any single state shall not halt operations.
④ Funding structures shall preserve institutional independence.
Article 12. Relationship with Collective Defense System
① Determinations shall be linked with:
• Collective Response Framework
• Regional Defense Architecture Framework
• Regional Collective Defense Treaty
• Responsibility Attribution & Allocation Framework
• State Responsibility Differentiation & Attribution Framework
• Temporal Applicability & Continuing Harm Framework
② Determination outcomes may serve as triggering evidence for collective response mechanisms.
③ The authority shall not execute responses directly and is limited to providing determinations.
Article 13. Human Oversight of Analytical Systems
① The authority may utilize AI-based analytical systems.
② Such systems may perform:
• Satellite pattern analysis
• Detection of military movement patterns
• Cyber anomaly detection
• Global communications data analysis
③ AI outputs shall be used only as decision-support tools.
④ Final authority for determination rests with human members.
⑤ AI systems shall not independently determine aggression.
⑥ This principle follows the Human-in-the-Loop Principle.
⑦ It is aligned with the Human Final Authority Principle.
Article 14. Information Sharing Safeguards
① Data provided by member states shall be managed under the principle of national security protection.
② Protection measures include:
• Data anonymization
• Source anonymization
• Removal of technical details
• Reduction of temporal and geographic precision
③ Data-providing states retain the right to:
• Request limitation of disclosure scope
• Request classification levels
• Request source confidentiality
④ Provided data shall not be used for political or military misuse.
⑤ This Article shall be aligned with the Aggression Evidence & Monitoring Protocol.
Article 15. Data Protection and Confidentiality
① Data shall be classified as:
• Public Information
• Restricted Analytical Data
• Classified Intelligence Evidence
② Classified data shall be used only for:
• Aggression determination
• International judicial proceedings
• Limited verification
③ Access shall be restricted under strict security protocols.
④ Data protection shall follow:
• Minimum disclosure
• Protection of information assets
• Compliance with international security standards
Article 16. Institutional Inviolability and Protection
① The following components shall be considered protected international infrastructure:
• Servers and databases
• Evidence preservation systems
• Secure communication networks
• Physical operational facilities
② Attacks or interference against these elements may be considered:
• Serious obstruction of determination procedures
• Structural threats to international verification and judicial cooperation systems
• A significant factor in aggression assessment
③ Such acts shall be further evaluated under:
• Definition of Aggression
• Aggression Determination Framework
④ Emergency review procedures may be initiated where necessary.
⑤ This Article shall be interpreted as a non-military protection standard for institutional functionality.
Article 17. Protection of Independent Members and Experts
① Members and experts shall be protected from retaliation.
② Protection procedures may be triggered in the following situations:
• Physical threats
• Political retaliation
• Arbitrary legal actions
• Threats against family members or associates
③ The authority may facilitate:
• Linkage to international protection mechanisms
• Secure working or relocation support
• Legal assistance
• Requests for protection from international organizations or member states
④ Where home states fail to provide protection, additional protection may be requested through international cooperation.
⑤ Such protection may be integrated with international cooperation and humanitarian protection mechanisms.
