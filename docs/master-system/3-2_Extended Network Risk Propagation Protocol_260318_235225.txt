Global Collective Defense System
PHASE 1: Operational Detection & Response Infrastructure
3-2. Extended Network Risk Propagation Protocol

Submitted by: Jungyun Kwon, Citizen of the Republic of Korea and Global Citizen
Date: March 19, 2026 (KST)

Article 1. Purpose
① This regulation defines the criteria and mechanisms by which financial risk flags extend beyond specific actors to the broader connected network.
② The purposes are as follows:
• Preventing distributed network structures designed to evade responsibility
• Identifying indirect risk propagation structures
• Enhancing the accuracy of market-based risk responses
Article 2. Definition of Risk Propagation
① Risk propagation refers to the process by which financial risk assigned to a specific actor is extended to connected actors or networks.
② Such propagation is based not on direct diffusion, but on structural and relational linkages.
Article 3. Scope of Propagation
① Risk propagation applies to the following:
• Actors in direct transactional relationships
• Actors belonging to the same network or organization
• Actors sharing common benefit structures
• Actors connected through influence or control structures
② The scope of propagation may be expanded in stages.
Article 4. Propagation Criteria
① Risk propagation shall be assessed based on the following criteria:
• Connectivity of financial flows
• Repeated transactional relationships
• Joint investment or shared profit structures
• Decision-making or control linkages
• Technological or infrastructural dependency
② Mere contact or one-time transactions shall be excluded from propagation.
③ The following forms of malicious linkage attempts may be excluded from propagation assessment:
• Intentionally generated small-scale or abnormal transactions
• Non-recurring connections based on isolated events
• Formal transactions lacking substantive influence
④ The system shall filter malicious linkages based on the following criteria:
• Analysis of anomalous transaction patterns
• Detection of temporal and structural inconsistencies
• Imbalance between network influence and transaction scale
• Assessment of intentional risk contamination attempts
Article 5. Levels of Propagation
① Risk propagation shall be classified into the following levels:
• Level 1: Direct Link Propagation
• Level 2: Secondary Network Propagation
• Level 3: Structural Network Propagation
• Level 4: Systemic Influence Propagation
② As levels increase, the scope of application expands, but remains constrained by the principle of proportionality.
Article 6. Propagation Intensity
① The intensity of propagated risk shall be adjusted based on the following factors:
• Strength of connection
• Transaction scale
• Degree of dependency
• Level of influence
② Even within the same network, identical risk levels shall not be uniformly applied to all actors.
Article 7. Containment Threshold
① The system shall apply thresholds to prevent excessive propagation of risk.
② Propagation shall be limited where the following conditions are present:
• Risk of excessive impact on the broader civilian economy
• Risk of disruption to global supply chains
• Impact on humanitarian essential systems
③ Where necessary, the scope of propagation shall be automatically reduced.
Article 8. Network Mapping & Visualization
① The system shall structurally analyze networks among actors to enable risk propagation.
② The following elements shall be included:
• Core Nodes
• Strength of connections
• Influence pathways
• Cluster structures
③ High-risk nodes shall be prioritized as propagation targets.
④ The following non-traditional financial structures shall also be subject to analysis:
• Over-the-counter (OTC) networks
• Informal and non-institutional financial flows
• Collateral-based informal financing structures
• Anonymous or bearer-asset-based transaction structures
⑤ Such structures may be detected and integrated through dedicated analytical systems.
Article 9. Dynamic Propagation
① Risk propagation shall operate as a dynamic structure that evolves over time.
② Propagation shall be adjusted in response to the following:
• Emergence of new transactions
• Changes in network structures
• Movement of assets
• Reallocation of influence
Article 10. Safeguards & Fairness
① The following principles shall apply in the propagation process:
• Prohibition of excessive expansion
• Protection of innocent actors
• Maintenance of proportionality
• Data-driven decision-making
② Propagation shall be condition-based rather than automatic.
Article 11. Right to Clarification
① Actors subject to propagation shall have the right to clarification.
② Clarification may include:
• Denial of association
• Assertion of minimal influence
• Explanation of legitimacy of transactions
• Identification of data errors
③ Based on clarification, the scope of propagation may be adjusted.
④ Where the following conditions are met, risk sanitization may be applied:
• Proven disconnection from high-risk networks
• Establishment of transparency in assets and transaction structures
• Cessation of repeated transactions
• Verification of independent operational structure
⑤ Upon fulfillment of these conditions, the following measures may be applied:
• Gradual release from propagated risk
• Downgrading of risk levels
• Reassessment of network linkages
⑥ Risk sanitization shall follow the principle of Expedited Adjustment, while maintaining the integrity of verification procedures.
Article 12. Human Oversight
① High-level propagation (Level 3 and above) shall be subject to human review.
② Human decision-makers shall have the authority to:
• Halt propagation
• Reduce scope
• Exclude specific actors
• Request additional verification
Article 13. Transparency & Traceability
① All propagation processes shall be recorded.
② Records shall include the following:
• Propagation pathways
• Applied criteria
• Scope of impact
• Adjustment history
③ Records shall be used for ex post audit and responsibility determination.
Article 14. System Integration & Framework Linkage
① This regulation is structurally integrated with other core frameworks within the Global Collective Defense System.
② This regulation performs propagation mechanisms based on financial risk signals generated by:
• Actor-Based Financial Risk Flagging Framework
• Associated Actor Identification Protocol
③ Risk propagation under this regulation may be applied in conjunction with the following systems:
• Network Containment & Influence Suppression Framework
• Actor Isolation & Risk Ring-Fencing Protocol
④ The intensity and scope of propagation shall remain aligned with the:
• Responsibility Attribution & Allocation Framework
⑤ Application and interpretation across frameworks shall follow these principles:
• Consistency in evidence-based assessments
• Alignment between responsibility levels and propagation intensity
• Maintenance of system stability and proportionality
⑥ This regulation does not operate independently, but functions as the system’s stage for financial risk propagation and identification of network-based risk structures.
