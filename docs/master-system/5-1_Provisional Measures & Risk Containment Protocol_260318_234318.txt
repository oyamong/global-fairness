5-1. Provisional Measures & Risk Containment Protocol

Submitted by: Jungyun Kwon, Citizen of the Republic of Korea and Global Citizen
Date: March 19, 2026 (KST)

Article 1. Purpose
① This protocol defines a system of provisional measures aimed at preventing the spread of risk and preserving evidence and assets prior to the final determination of responsibility when aggression or equivalent high-risk conduct occurs.
② The purposes are as follows:
• Preventing delays in initial response
• Blocking asset flight and concealment
• Preventing destruction of evidence
• Minimizing the spread of harm
③ This protocol may include the following:
• Preservation of assets and evidence for future reparations enforcement
• Securing the possibility of reparations prior to the determination of individual and state responsibility
Article 2. Fundamental Principle
① This protocol follows the following principles:
• Principle of Non-Punitive Measures
• Preservation of the presumption of innocence
• Principles of proportionality and minimal impairment
• Principle of Human Final Authority
② These measures do not include:
• Criminal punishment
• Findings of guilt
• Final determination of responsibility
③ This protocol constitutes provisional measures for the purposes of risk containment and preservation.
④ These measures may subsequently be linked to the following procedures:
• Responsibility attribution
• Calculation and distribution of reparations
• Asset recovery execution
Article 3. Activation Trigger
① This protocol may be activated where one or more of the following conditions are met:
• Possible aggression or equivalent violation of international law
• Detection of high-risk network activities
• Attempted movement or concealment of assets
• Risk of evidence destruction
② Activation may occur even without a finalized determination of responsibility.
③ Measures activated under this protocol may serve as the foundation for subsequent reparations and recovery procedures.
Article 4. Scope of Application
① This protocol applies to the following:
• Actors related to aggression
• Associated networks
• Relevant assets and financial flows
② States may be subject to this protocol under the following conditions:
• Clear control or command
• Clear acquiescence or omission
• Confirmed failure to prevent
③ Application to states shall be determined on a conditional basis.
④ These measures are intended to protect the following:
• Victims
• Protected Entities
• States and communities
Article 5. Actor Immediate Measures
① The following measures may be applied immediately to identified actors:
• Restriction on asset movements
• Restriction on access to financial networks
• Blocking of high-risk transactions
• Enhanced monitoring of activities
② Such measures may be applied without prior notice.
③ These measures may serve as an initial basis for later asset recovery and reparations enforcement.
Article 6. Conditional State Measures
① Measures against states shall be applied according to the following criteria:
1. High Control / Direct Involvement
→ Immediate control measures may be applied
2. Unclear or Partial Involvement
→ Monitoring and limited measures may be applied
3. No Involvement Confirmed
→ No measures shall be applied
② State measures may include:
• Restriction of specific assets and financial flows
• Monitoring of network-based activities
• Limited control over high-risk areas
③ State measures serve the following purposes:
• Securing evidence for future responsibility determinations
• Preparing for and managing potential reparations responsibility
Article 7. Asset Preservation Measures
① The following measures may be undertaken:
• Freezing or restricting the movement of assets
• Monitoring high-risk accounts and wallets
• Applying conditional transaction approval systems
② Assets shall be managed for preservation purposes, and ownership shall not be transferred arbitrarily.
③ Preserved assets may later be used for the following purposes:
• Asset recovery
• Victim reparations
• Supplementary state reparations
Article 8. Evidence Preservation Measures
① The following measures shall be undertaken:
• Protection of original data
• Reinforced recording of access and modification
• Preservation of evidentiary integrity
② All evidence shall be managed in accordance with Chain of Custody standards.
③ Preserved evidence may later be used for:
• Responsibility determination
• Calculation of reparations
• Judicial proceedings
Article 9. Network Containment Measures
① The following measures may be applied:
• Blocking of high-risk network linkages
• Restriction of influence propagation pathways
• Automatic identification and expansion of associated actors
② Expanded control across entire networks may be applied.
③ Such measures may be used not only to prevent the spread of harm but also to support later analysis of reparative responsibility structures.
Article 10. Transition to Formal Adjudication
① This stage shall transition to the next stage where the following conditions are met:
• Sufficient evidence is secured
• Responsibility structures become identifiable
• Judicial authorities request or intervene
② Subsequent procedures shall then proceed to:
• Responsibility Attribution
• Judicial Submission
• Asset Recovery
③ After transition, the following may additionally be undertaken:
• Reparations calculation
• Finalization of reparations distribution structures
Article 11. Temporal Limitation
① These measures are provisional in nature.
② They shall be lifted or adjusted where the following conditions are met:
• Risk is resolved
• Absence of responsibility is confirmed
• Judicial adjudication is completed
③ Where measures are maintained over a long period, periodic review is mandatory.
④ Long-maintained measures may be used as reference material for reparations and responsibility determinations.
Article 12. Safeguards & Fairness
① The following principles apply:
• Prohibition of excessive restrictions
• Protection of innocent parties
• Maintenance of proportionality
② The following shall be ensured:
• Consideration of exculpatory evidence
• Review of possible false positives
• Minimization of the scope of restrictions
③ These measures may serve as foundational material for maintaining fairness in reparations and responsibility structures.
Article 13. Human Final Authority
① Major measures shall be subject to human review.
② Human decision-makers shall have the authority to:
• Approve or terminate measures
• Adjust the scope of application
• Authorize emergency release
③ Human decisions may later be reflected in responsibility determinations and reparations structures.
Article 14. Transparency & Auditability
① All measures and procedures shall be recorded.
② Records shall include the following:
• Basis for activation
• Subjects of application
• Content of measures
• Records of modification and release
③ Such records shall be used for ex post audit and legal verification.
④ Records shall also be linked to the following:
• Responsibility determination
• Asset recovery
• Reparations distribution
Article 15. System Integration & Pre-Enforcement Coordination
① This protocol is fully integrated with the risk detection, asset tracking, evidence preservation, and responsibility determination systems within the Global Collective Defense System.
② Activation of this protocol shall be based on risk signals and actor identification information generated by:
• Financial Risk Flag Framework
• Actor Identification & Attribution Framework
• Associated Actor Identification Protocol
③ Asset and network containment measures under this protocol may be maintained and expanded in coordination with:
• Influence-Linked Asset Monitoring Framework
• Financial Network Alert & Integration Protocol
④ Evidence preservation measures under this protocol shall remain consistent with:
• Chain of Custody & Evidence Integrity Framework
• Legal Evidence Packaging Protocol
⑤ Assets and evidence preserved under this protocol shall be linked to the following subsequent stages:
• Responsibility Attribution & Allocation Framework
• Responsibility Dossier Generation System
⑥ This protocol supports transition to the following procedures:
• Multi-Jurisdiction Judicial Submission Protocol
• Judicial Execution & Asset Recovery Protocol
⑦ This protocol is also directly linked to the following reparations structure:
• State Responsibility & Reparations Framework
⑧ This protocol functions within the overall system as the Pre-Enforcement Containment Layer, serving to block the spread of risk and preserve evidence and assets prior to the final determination of responsibility.
