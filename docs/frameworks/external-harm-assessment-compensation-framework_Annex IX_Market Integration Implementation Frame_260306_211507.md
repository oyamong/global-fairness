Annex IX.
Market Integration Implementation Framework
for External Harm in International Conflicts

Submitted by: Jungyun Kwon, Citizen of the Republic of Korea and Global Citizen
Date: March 6, 2026 (KST)

Article 1
Purpose
This Framework establishes the implementation structure through which the results of responsibility assessments and compensation implementation status relating to external harm affecting third States arising from international conflicts may be linked with international financial and market systems in order to promote recovery and encourage the fulfillment of responsibility.

This Framework shall operate in conjunction with the following documents:
1. Responsibility Scoring Framework
2. Damage Assessment Standard for External Harm in International Conflicts
3. Liability Allocation Calculation Regulation
4. Financial Mechanism Regulation for Compensation of External Harm

Article 2
Guiding Principles
The market integration mechanism shall operate in accordance with the following principles:
1. Consistency with international financial systems
2. Provision of market-based information
3. Encouragement of responsibility implementation
4. Maintenance of political neutrality
5. Ensuring accessibility of information for investors and financial institutions

This Framework is not intended to establish direct market regulation or sanctions, but rather to support information-based market responses.

Article 3
Market-Linked Information
The following information relating to responsibility assessments and compensation implementation may be utilized as market-linked information:
1. Total External Damage (TED)
2. Responsibility shares by State
3. Liability amounts
4. Status of compensation implementation
5. Outstanding unpaid amounts
6. Identification of retaliatory conduct, where applicable
Such information may be provided as reference material for participants in international financial markets.

Article 4
Global External Harm Indicators
Based on external harm and responsibility assessment data, the following indicators may be developed:
1. External Harm Exposure Index
2. Conflict Responsibility Indicator
3. Compensation Compliance Indicator

These indicators may be used as analytical reference tools by international financial institutions, investors and policy institutions.

Article 5
Integration into Investment and Financial Risk Assessment
Market-linked information may be used as reference indicators in the following areas:
1. Sovereign risk analysis
2. Investment risk assessment
3. Financial system stability analysis
4. Supply-chain risk evaluation

The status of responsibility implementation may be considered a reference factor in long-term sovereign risk assessment.

Article 6
Linkage with Financial Cooperation Mechanisms
Market-linked information may be referenced in financial cooperation structures involving institutions such as:
• International Monetary Fund (IMF)
• World Bank
• United Nations Development Programme (UNDP)
These institutions may take such information into account in relevant data analysis and policy cooperation processes.

Article 7
Insurance and Guarantee Market Integration
Market-linked information may serve as reference material within financial risk management structures including:
1. Investment risk insurance
2. Export credit guarantees
3. Political risk insurance
4. Reinsurance markets

The status of responsibility implementation may be considered as a reference element in long-term insurance risk assessments.

Article 8
Supply Chain and Corporate Risk Assessment
External harm responsibility information may also serve as reference material for global corporate and supply-chain analysis.

This information may be considered in the following areas:
1. Supply-chain stability analysis
2. Corporate risk management
3. Global logistics assessment
4. Energy and food system stability evaluation

Article 9
Principles of Market Information Disclosure
Disclosure of market-linked information shall be conducted in accordance with the following principles:
1. Objective data-based reporting
2. Maintenance of political neutrality
3. Protection of affected States
4. Protection of sensitive information

The scope of disclosure may be determined through independent verification procedures and review by relevant institutions.

Article 10
Encouragement of Responsibility Implementation
The publication of responsibility assessments and compensation implementation status may encourage responsibility implementation through the following mechanisms:
1. Reflection in financial market risk assessments
2. Provision of reference material for investment decisions
3. Consideration in international cooperation programme participation
4. Influence on global reputation assessments

This mechanism may generate market-based incentives for compliance without requiring direct sanctions.

Article 11
International Cooperation
The market integration mechanism may support international cooperation in the following areas:
1. Global financial stability analysis
2. Supply-chain risk evaluation
3. Research on energy and food system stability
4. Analysis of conflict-related global impacts

Article 12
Reporting
Market-linked information shall be included in the Annual Global External Harm Report.

Such reporting may include:
1. Total external damage
2. Responsibility shares
3. Compensation implementation status
4. Market-linked indicators
5. Risk analysis findings

Article 13
Methodological Development
The market integration mechanism may be continuously improved through:
1. International financial data analysis
2. Feedback from market participants
3. Policy research
4. Technological development
