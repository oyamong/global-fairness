Annex VIII.
Financial Mechanism Regulation
for Compensation of External Harm in International Conflicts

Submitted by: Jungyun Kwon, Citizen of the Republic of Korea and Global Citizen
Date: March 6, 2026 (KST)

Article 1
Purpose
This Regulation establishes the structure, principles, operational modalities and settlement procedures of a financial mechanism intended to facilitate fair and practical compensation and recovery support for external harm affecting third States arising from international conflicts.

This Regulation shall be applied in conjunction with the following documents:
1. Responsibility Scoring Framework
2. Damage Assessment Standard for External Harm in International Conflicts
3. Liability Allocation Calculation Regulation
4. State Damage Submission Template

Article 2
Guiding Principles
The compensation financial mechanism shall operate in accordance with the following principles:
1. Protection of affected third States
2. Effectiveness of recovery and support measures
3. Fair burden-sharing based on responsibility shares
4. Parallel operation of direct compensation and indirect settlement
5. Consistency with international financial systems
6. Political neutrality and transparency

Article 3
Definitions
For the purposes of this Regulation:
1. Total External Damage (TED) means the total amount of external harm assessed under the relevant Damage Assessment Standard.
2. Responsibility Share (RShare) means the relative share of responsibility assigned to each actor under the Responsibility Scoring Framework and the Liability Allocation Calculation Regulation.
3. Direct Compensation Track means the financial pathway through which a responsible actor or related State fulfills compensation through direct payment, agreement, offset, or compensation arrangement.
4. Indirect Settlement Track means the financial pathway through which settlement is pursued through international financial, insurance, guarantee, development finance or cooperative mechanisms where direct implementation is delayed, refused or not undertaken.
5. Compensation Financial Mechanism means the overall structure for compensation, support, settlement and recovery operating under this Regulation.
6. Set-off between States means the settlement of liability amounts through existing creditor-debtor relationships between responsible States, affected States, third States, or through payment flows in multilateral financial or cooperation structures.

Article 4
Scope of Application
This Regulation may apply to the following forms of external harm arising from international conflicts:
1. Economic harm
2. Supply-chain harm
3. Financial system harm
4. Environmental harm
5. Energy and food system harm
6. Humanitarian harm

Article 5
Structure of the Compensation Financial Mechanism
The compensation financial mechanism shall operate through the following two tracks in parallel:
1. Direct Compensation Track
2. Indirect Settlement Track

Each track may operate independently and, where appropriate, may be applied simultaneously.
The compensation financial mechanism may also operate in conjunction with set-off settlement structures between States.

Article 6
Direct Compensation Track
The Direct Compensation Track may operate through one or more of the following means:
1. Direct payment by the responsible State
2. Direct compensation agreements between the responsible State and the affected State
3. Payment arrangements facilitated through the good offices or mediation of international organizations
4. Settlement through inter-State set-off arrangements
5. Voluntary implementation programmes by the responsible State

The Direct Compensation Track may, where feasible, be considered as a primary avenue of compensation.

Article 6-2
Multilateral Settlement Reference Network
Where multiple States are involved in negotiations concerning compensation for external harm, such discussions may be informed by a Multilateral Settlement Reference Network.

The Network may serve the following purposes:
1. Enhancing structural understanding of creditor-debtor relationships among multiple States
2. Examining the potential for inter-State set-off arrangements
3. Improving settlement efficiency in compensation discussions

The Network shall not constitute an independent financial institution or clearing house and may function as a complementary analytical and reference framework within existing international financial cooperation structures.

Article 7
Indirect Settlement Track
The Indirect Settlement Track may operate through one or more of the following mechanisms:
1. Linkages with international financial cooperation programmes
2. Settlement adjustments through multilateral development finance structures
3. Cost offsets through international guarantee, insurance or reinsurance arrangements
4. Linkages with recovery and reconstruction support programmes
5. Adjustment of risk premiums in the event of non-implementation
6. Advance support followed by settlement through international cooperative financial arrangements
The Indirect Settlement Track may be activated in parallel where the responsible State refuses, delays or avoids direct implementation.

Article 8
Principle of Parallel Operation
The Direct Compensation Track and the Indirect Settlement Track shall operate in accordance with the following principles:
1. Preparations for indirect settlement shall not be suspended merely because direct compensation procedures have begun
2. Where only partial implementation occurs, indirect settlement may proceed with respect to the remaining amount
3. In cases of urgent recovery needs, indirect settlement may be implemented first and subsequently offset
4. Where necessary for the protection of affected States, both tracks may operate simultaneously

Article 9
Basic Calculation of Compensation Amounts
The basic liability amount for each actor shall be determined in accordance with the Liability Allocation Calculation Regulation.
Liabilityₐ = TED × RShareₐ

Where:
• Liabilityₐ = liability amount of actor a
• TED = Total External Damage
• RShareₐ = responsibility share of actor a

Article 10
Retaliatory Conduct and Additional Weighting
Where retaliatory conduct is identified in relation to the submission, verification, assessment or compensation process concerning external harm, the liability amount of the relevant actor may be recalculated on the basis of a responsibility score adjusted by a retaliation-related weighting factor.

Retaliatory conduct may include:
1. Economic retaliation
2. Diplomatic pressure or intimidation
3. Cyber operations or information disruption
4. Military threats or coercive measures
5. Interference with international data submission or assessment procedures
In such cases, priority use of the Indirect Settlement Track may be considered.

The existence of retaliatory conduct may be assessed on the basis of:
• submissions by affected States
• international data sources
• independent verification procedures

Article 11
Principle of Advance Support
Where the recovery needs of affected States are urgent, the compensation financial mechanism may support advance disbursement prior to final recovery from the responsible State or related actor through one or more of the following means:
1. Linkages with international cooperation support programmes
2. Priority allocation of recovery support resources
3. Temporary support through multilateral financial mechanisms
4. Emergency support for humanitarian harm
Amounts advanced under this Article may subsequently be linked to settlement and recovery procedures involving the responsible State or related actors.

Article 12
Means of Indirect Settlement
Indirect settlement may be considered through the following instruments:
1. Adjustments within multilateral development finance programmes
2. Adjustments through international guarantee and risk-sharing structures
3. Adjustments relating to export credit or investment guarantee arrangements
4. Adjustments in international insurance and reinsurance premiums
5. Cost offsets within international financial cooperation programmes
6. Priority-setting within international cooperation mechanisms

The means provided under this Article shall not presuppose direct compulsory seizure and shall operate through lawful adjustments within international financial, insurance and cooperation structures.

Article 13
Clearing Account and Accounting Management
The compensation financial mechanism may establish a Clearing Account or comparable accounting structure in order to record and manage the flows of direct compensation and indirect settlement.

The Clearing Account may perform the following functions:
1. Recording liability amounts
2. Recording payments and offsets
3. Recording advance disbursements and recoveries
4. Recording disbursement status by affected State
5. Recording non-implementation and outstanding balances
The Clearing Account shall be subject to independent accounting review.

Article 14
Priority of Disbursement to Affected States
Disbursement under the compensation financial mechanism may take into account the following factors in determining priority:
1. Absolute scale of harm
2. Limited recovery capacity of the affected State
3. Harm affecting essential survival sectors, including food, energy and health
4. Severity of humanitarian distress
5. Cumulative harm arising from protracted conflict

Article 15
Incentives for Direct Implementation
The following elements may be utilized in order to encourage direct implementation by responsible States or related actors:
1. Reflection of implementation status in international reports
2. Consideration of implementation status in participation in international cooperation programmes
3. Reference to implementation status in financial and investment risk analysis
4. Consideration of implementation status as a factor of credibility in recovery and support discussions

This Article is intended to encourage direct compensation while supporting transition to indirect settlement in cases of non-implementation.

Article 16
Linkage to Individual Responsibility
Where the following circumstances are identified in the decision-making process of a conflict, the compensation financial mechanism may be linked to procedures reviewing individual responsibility:
1. Conflict decisions taken for personal political gain
2. Pursuit of economic or financial private interests
3. Authorization of attacks prohibited under international law
4. Approval of attacks despite known risks to civilians

In such cases:
1. The primary responsibility of the State shall remain intact; and
2. Secondary recovery from relevant decision-makers or individuals may be considered.

Article 17
Set-off between States
Where compensation obligations arise in relation to external harm from international conflicts, the relevant liability amounts may be offset through existing creditor-debtor relationships between States.

Set-off may be considered where:
1. The responsible State has outstanding debt obligations to another State
2. The responsible State is expected to receive payments through international cooperation programmes
3. Financial transactions exist between the responsible State and the affected State
4. Set-off is feasible within multilateral financial cooperation structures

The amount of set-off may apply to all or part of the liability amount determined under the Liability Allocation Calculation Regulation.

Article 18
Multilateral Set-off Structures
In examining the possibility of set-off, the Multilateral Settlement Reference Network may be used to analyse:
1. Payment adjustments within multilateral development finance programmes
2. Offsets involving inter-State development cooperation funds
3. Settlement adjustments within international financial cooperation programmes
4. Payment adjustments within multilateral financial cooperation structures
Such analysis may serve as reference material during compensation discussions among States.

Article 19
Set-off Procedures
Set-off procedures may proceed through the following stages:
1. Determination of liability amounts
2. Identification of creditor-debtor relationships
3. Calculation of amounts eligible for set-off
4. Notification of the relevant States
5. Registration of settlement records

Results of set-off shall be recorded in the Clearing Account.
Set-off settlement may be applied regardless of whether direct implementation by the responsible State has occurred.

Article 20
Linkage to Recovery Procedures
Where a State or related actor fails to implement compensation obligations, recovery may be considered in conjunction with the following procedures:
1. Domestic judicial procedures
2. International legal procedures
3. Asset freezing or other lawful financial recovery procedures
4. Set-off procedures involving international financial, insurance or guarantee structures
5. Settlement procedures linked to multilateral cooperation programmes

Article 21
Transparency of Financial Operations
The operation of the compensation financial mechanism shall observe the following principles:
1. Transparent accounting management
2. Independent verification and audit
3. Preservation of payment and settlement records
4. Prevention of conflicts of interest
5. Periodic reporting to the extent possible

However, information requiring confidentiality for the protection of affected States or for the protection of sensitive financial information may be treated on a restricted basis.

Article 22
Linkage to Data and Supporting Evidence
All amounts, ratios and settlement records used in the operation of the compensation financial mechanism may be linked to the following materials:
1. Data submitted by affected States
2. Responsibility scoring results
3. Damage assessment results
4. Findings of the Independent Verification Panel
5. Objective market, financial and insurance-related data

Article 23
Reporting
The operational results of the compensation financial mechanism shall be included in the Annual Global External Harm Report.

Reported information may include:
1. Total external damage
2. Responsibility shares and liability amounts
3. Status of direct compensation implementation
4. Progress of indirect settlement
5. Outstanding amounts and unpaid balances
6. Recovery support status by affected State

Article 24
Methodological Development
The structure and methodology of the compensation financial mechanism may be continuously improved through:
1. International cooperative research
2. Operational experience
3. Development of new financial and insurance structures
4. Independent expert review
5. Advances in technical data analysis
