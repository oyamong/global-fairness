Annex X.
Independent Verification and Audit Regulation
for External Harm Assessment and Compensation Mechanisms in International Conflicts

Submitted by: Jungyun Kwon, Citizen of the Republic of Korea and Global Citizen
Date: March 6, 2026 (KST)

Article 1
Purpose
This Regulation establishes an independent verification and audit framework to ensure the reliability and accuracy of the assessment of external harm affecting third States arising from international conflicts, the calculation of responsibility, the settlement of compensation, and the functioning of the market integration mechanism.

This Regulation shall be applied in conjunction with the following documents:
1. Responsibility Scoring Framework
2. Damage Assessment Standard for External Harm in International Conflicts
3. Liability Allocation Calculation Regulation
4. Financial Mechanism Regulation for Compensation of External Harm
5. Market Integration Implementation Framework

Article 2
Guiding Principles
Independent verification and auditing shall be conducted in accordance with the following principles:
1. Political independence
2. Objective data-based verification
3. Transparency
4. Protection of affected States
5. Prevention of conflicts of interest

Verification procedures shall be conducted independently of any specific State or political interest.

Article 3
Independent Verification Structure
An Independent Verification Panel may be established to verify the external harm assessment and compensation mechanisms.

The Independent Verification Panel may consist of experts from the following fields:
1. Economic analysis
2. International finance
3. Supply-chain analysis
4. Environmental impact assessment
5. Data science
6. International law

In forming the Panel, consideration may be given to regional diversity and balance of expertise.

Article 4
Scope of Verification
Independent verification and auditing may cover the following elements:
1. Damage assessment data
2. Responsibility score calculations
3. Responsibility share calculations
4. Compensation amount calculations
5. Compensation implementation and settlement records
6. Market-linked information

Article 5
Data Verification Methods
Verification of data may utilize the following methods:
1. Cross-verification across multiple datasets
2. Comparison with international market data
3. Satellite data analysis
4. Supply-chain and logistics data analysis
5. Review of research conducted by independent institutions
Where necessary, third-party data sources may be utilized.

Article 6
Verification of Submitted Data
Data submitted by affected States or relevant institutions may be verified through the following procedures:
1. Formal review of submission format
2. Consistency analysis of submitted data
3. Comparison with international datasets
4. Expert analytical review
Security measures for the protection of affected States shall be maintained throughout the verification process.

Article 7
Field Investigations
Where necessary, the Independent Verification Panel may conduct field investigations or collect additional data.

Field investigations may be conducted for the following purposes:
1. Verification of environmental damage
2. Assessment of supply-chain disruption impacts
3. Verification of humanitarian harm
4. Analysis of industrial and economic damage

Field investigations shall be conducted with the cooperation of the relevant State.

Article 8
Scope of Financial Audit
Auditing relating to the compensation financial mechanism may include the following areas:
1. Operation of the Clearing Account
2. Payment and recovery records
3. Set-off settlement records
4. Advance disbursement and recovery procedures
5. Transparency of financial operations

Article 9
Audit Procedures
Audits may be conducted through the following procedures:
1. Review of accounting records
2. Analysis of financial flows
3. Verification of settlement records
4. Confirmation of consistency between payments and recoveries
Where appropriate, external auditing institutions may participate in the audit process.

Article 10
Prevention of Conflicts of Interest
Experts or institutions participating in verification and auditing may be restricted from participation where the following circumstances exist:
1. Direct financial relationships with parties to the conflict
2. Political interests related to the conflict under examination
3. Potential conflicts of interest with the subject of verification

Potential conflicts of interest may be identified through prior disclosure procedures.

Article 11
Protection of Affected States
The following protective measures shall be maintained during verification and auditing processes:
1. Protection of the anonymity of submitting institutions where applicable
2. Confidential handling of sensitive information
3. Maintenance of data security

Verification procedures shall take into consideration the need to prevent risks to the safety or diplomatic position of affected States.

Article 12
Reporting of Verification Results
Results of verification and auditing may be reported in the following forms:
1. Verification reports
2. Audit reports
3. Data reliability assessments

These reports may be included in the Annual Global External Harm Report.

Article 13
Error Correction Procedures
Where errors or inconsistencies are identified during the verification process, the following procedures may be applied:
1. Request for additional data
2. Re-examination of analytical assessments
3. Revision of responsibility calculations

Where necessary, relevant reports may be updated accordingly.

Article 14
Continuous Verification
External harm assessments and compensation mechanisms may be subject to continuous verification.

Verification may be conducted in the following circumstances:
1. During the preparation of the annual report
2. When major conflicts arise
3. When large-scale external harm occurs

Article 15
Institutional Cooperation
During verification and auditing processes, cooperation may occur with the following institutions:
• United Nations Development Programme (UNDP)
• World Bank
• International Monetary Fund (IMF)
Where appropriate, cooperation with independent research institutions and academic bodies may also occur.

Article 16
Methodological Development
Verification and auditing methodologies may be continuously improved through:
1. Advances in data analysis technologies
2. International cooperative research
3. Accumulated verification experience
4. Expert review
