Annex XI.
Annual Reporting Framework
for External Harm in International Conflicts

Submitted by: Jungyun Kwon, Citizen of the Republic of Korea and Global Citizen
Date: March 6, 2026 (KST)

Article 1
Purpose
This Regulation establishes an annual reporting framework for the systematic analysis and presentation of information concerning the scale of external harm affecting third States arising from international conflicts, responsibility assessments, compensation implementation status, and related market impacts.

The annual reporting framework aims to:
1. Systematically document the scale of external harm
2. Track recovery and compensation implementation
3. Analyze impacts on the global economy and supply chains
4. Support international cooperation and policy research

Article 2
Scope of Application
The annual reporting framework shall operate in conjunction with the following documents:
1. Responsibility Scoring Framework
2. Damage Assessment Standard for External Harm in International Conflicts
3. Liability Allocation Calculation Regulation
4. Financial Mechanism Regulation for Compensation of External Harm
5. Market Integration Implementation Framework
6. Independent Verification and Audit Regulation
Annual reporting shall synthesize analytical results derived from these frameworks.

Article 3
Title of the Annual Report
The annual report may be published under one of the following titles:
Global External Harm Assessment Report
or
Annual Report on External Harm in International Conflicts
The title shall be designed to maintain political neutrality and an analytical orientation.

Article 4
Reporting Period
The annual report shall be prepared with reference to the following periods:
1. International conflicts occurring during the reporting year
2. Ongoing impacts from previous conflicts
3. Progress in compensation and recovery implementation
4. Where necessary, long-term impacts from past conflicts

Article 5
Contents of the Report
The annual report may include the following elements:
1. Global analysis of external harm
2. Analysis of harm by conflict type
3. Supply chain and market impact analysis
4. Responsibility share analysis
5. Compensation implementation status
6. Recovery progress for affected States

The report shall maintain an analytical structure and shall not be intended as criticism of specific States.

Article 6
Harm Analysis
The report may include analysis of the following categories of harm:
1. Economic harm
2. Supply-chain harm
3. Financial system harm
4. Environmental harm
5. Energy and food system harm
6. Humanitarian harm

Harm analysis shall be conducted in accordance with the Damage Assessment Standard.

Article 7
Responsibility Analysis
The report may include responsibility-related information calculated under the Responsibility Scoring Framework, including:
1. Distribution of responsibility scores
2. Responsibility share analysis
3. Estimated liability ranges

Where appropriate, responsibility analysis may be presented in summary form.

Article 8
Compensation Implementation Analysis
The report may analyze compensation implementation under the Financial Mechanism Regulation, including:
1. Status of direct compensation implementation
2. Progress of indirect settlement arrangements
3. Scale of advance support provided
4. Outstanding liability amounts

Article 9
Market Impact Analysis
The report may include analysis of market impacts related to international conflicts, including:
1. Energy market impacts
2. Food market impacts
3. Global supply-chain disruptions
4. Financial market impacts

Such analysis may serve as reference material for participants in international financial markets.

Article 10
Data-Based Analysis
The report may utilize the following data sources for analytical purposes:
1. International economic datasets
2. Supply-chain and logistics data
3. Financial market data
4. Environmental impact data
5. Satellite observation data

All analysis shall be conducted using objective indicator-based methodologies.

Article 11
Protection of Affected States
The following safeguards shall be maintained during the reporting process:
1. Protection of data submitted by affected States
2. Confidential handling of sensitive information
3. Preservation of the anonymity of submitting institutions where applicable

When reports are published, due consideration shall be given to preventing diplomatic or economic disadvantages to affected States.

Article 12
Special Reports
In addition to the annual report, special reports may be issued in the following circumstances:
1. Large-scale external harm events
2. Major global supply-chain disruptions
3. Environmental or humanitarian crises

Special reports may provide in-depth analysis of specific conflicts or incidents.

Article 13
Publication of Reports
Annual reports may be published through the following formats:
1. Public summary reports
2. Policy analysis reports
3. Research-oriented datasets
Sensitive information may be subject to restricted disclosure procedures.

Article 14
Policy Research Use
The results of the annual report may be used in the following areas:
1. International policy research
2. Supply-chain resilience studies
3. Environmental impact assessments
4. Economic stability research

The report may serve as a foundational reference for international cooperation and policy analysis.

Article 15
International Cooperation
The preparation of the annual report may involve cooperation with institutions including:
1. United Nations
2. International Monetary Fund (IMF)
3. World Bank

Where appropriate, cooperation with research institutions and academic organizations may also occur.

Article 16
Improvement of the Reporting Framework
The annual reporting framework may be continuously improved through:
1. Utilization of new data sources
2. Improvements in analytical methodologies
3. International cooperative research
4. Technological developments
