Annex VII.
Liability Allocation Calculation Regulation
for External Harm in International Conflicts

Submitted by: Jungyun Kwon, Citizen of the Republic of Korea and Global Citizen
Date: March 6, 2026 (KST)

Article 1
Purpose
This Regulation establishes the procedures and methodology for calculating the allocation of responsibility for external harm affecting third States arising from international conflicts.

This Regulation shall be applied in conjunction with the Responsibility Scoring Framework and the Damage Assessment Standard for External Harm in International Conflicts.

Article 2
Guiding Principles
Liability allocation calculations shall be conducted in accordance with the following principles:
1. Objective data-based assessment
2. Ensuring international comparability
3. Transparent calculation methodologies
4. Protection of affected third States
5. Maintenance of political neutrality

Article 3
Structure of Liability Allocation
The allocation of responsibility for external harm shall be calculated through the following stages:
1. Determination of total external damage
2. Calculation of responsibility scores
3. Determination of responsibility shares
4. Allocation of damage amounts

Article 4
Total External Damage
Total external damage shall be calculated in accordance with the Damage Assessment Standard, including the following components:
1. Economic harm
2. Supply-chain harm
3. Financial system harm
4. Environmental harm
5. Energy and food system harm
6. Humanitarian harm

Total external damage shall be expressed as:
Total External Damage (TED)

Article 5
Application of Responsibility Scores
Responsibility scores for each actor shall be calculated in accordance with the Responsibility Scoring Framework.
Each actor’s responsibility score shall be defined as:
RSₐ = IESₐ + OISₐ + CESₐ + CHISₐ + EHISₐ − DADJₐ

Where:
• IESₐ = Initial Escalation Score
• OISₐ = Operational Impact Score
• CESₐ = Conflict Expansion Score
• CHISₐ = Civilian Harm Impact Score
• EHISₐ = External Harm Impact Score
• DADJₐ = Defensive Action Adjustment

The lawful exercise of self-defence under international law shall not be considered an adverse responsibility factor.

However, responsibility calculations may include harm resulting from defensive actions where:
• damage to third States occurs
• civilian harm increases
• the principle of proportionality is violated
In such cases, the resulting harm may be reflected in the External Harm Impact Score or Civilian Harm Impact Score.

Article 6
Total Responsibility Score
The total responsibility score of all actors shall be calculated as:
TRS = Σ RSₐ

Where:
• TRS = Total Responsibility Score
• RSₐ = Responsibility score of actor a

Article 7
Calculation of Responsibility Shares
Each actor’s responsibility share shall be calculated as:
RShareₐ = RSₐ / TRS

Where:
• RShareₐ = Responsibility share of actor a

Article 8
Calculation of Liability Amount
The external harm liability assigned to each actor shall be calculated as:
Liabilityₐ = TED × RShareₐ

Where:
• Liabilityₐ = Liability amount of actor a
• TED = Total External Damage

Article 9
Allocation of Responsibility in Multilateral Conflicts
Where multiple actors participate in a conflict, the following factors may be considered:
1. Level of military participation
2. Participation in economic measures
3. Operational contribution
4. Contribution to external harm

Article 9-2
Conflicts Involving Proxy Forces
Where conflict actions are conducted by a proxy force or non-state actor, the responsibility score of such actors may be partially reflected in the responsibility calculation of supporting States.

The following factors may be considered:
1. Scale of support
2. Duration of support
3. Operational influence
4. Degree of strategic control

The responsibility share of the supporting State may be calculated as:
RShare_support = RS_support / TRS
This provision is intended to reflect indirect contributions by States supporting proxy actors.

Article 9-3
Responsibility Structure for Proxy Actors
Where conflict actions are conducted by proxy forces or non-state actors, liability allocation calculations may take into account the responsibility structure established under Article 4-3 of the Responsibility Scoring Framework.
In such cases, compensation responsibility for external harm may be primarily attributed to the State that supported or materially enabled the relevant actions.

Article 10
Application of Retaliation Penalty
Where retaliatory actions are identified against States participating in external harm data submission or assessment processes, additional weighting may be applied to the responsibility score or responsibility share of the actor concerned.

Retaliatory actions may include:
1. Economic sanctions or trade restrictions
2. Diplomatic pressure or coercion
3. Cyber operations or information disruption
4. Military threats or coercive measures
5. Interference with international data submission processes

Penalty Calculation
Where retaliatory actions are confirmed, the adjusted responsibility score may be calculated as:
RSₐ_adjusted = RSₐ × (1 + RPF)

Where:
• RSₐ = Original responsibility score
• RPF = Retaliation Penalty Factor
• RPF range: 0.05 – 0.30

Retaliatory conduct may be assessed based on:
• submissions by affected States
• international data sources
• findings of the Independent Verification Panel

Article 11
De Minimis Contribution Threshold
Actors whose responsibility share is below 5% may be classified as having a de minimis contribution for analytical purposes.
Such actors may be excluded from detailed liability allocation calculations, but may still be included as reference information in analytical reports.
This classification shall not be interpreted as denying the existence of responsibility.

Article 12
Compensation Mechanisms for Affected States
Compensation for affected States may be provided through one or more of the following mechanisms:
1. International damage compensation funds
2. Direct compensation agreements
3. International financial assistance programs
4. Recovery and reconstruction support programs

Compensation mechanisms may operate through international cooperation mechanisms, multilateral financial structures, or international compensation funds.

Article 13
Linkage to Decision-Maker Responsibility
Where the following circumstances are identified in the decision-making process of the conflict, liability allocation results may be linked to procedures reviewing individual responsibility:
1. Conflict decisions taken for personal political gain
2. Pursuit of economic or financial private benefit
3. Authorization of attacks in violation of international law
4. Approval of operations despite awareness of civilian harm risks

Such circumstances may be reviewed in conjunction with Article 16 of the Responsibility Scoring Framework.

Article 13-2
Multilateral Settlement Linkage
Where multiple States are involved, liability allocation results may be reviewed together with multilateral settlement reference network analysis.
This analysis may serve the following purposes:
• improving settlement efficiency in compensation negotiations
• identifying potential offsets between States
• examining integration with multilateral financial cooperation mechanisms
Such analysis may be used as reference material during compensation negotiations.

Article 14
Data Verification
Data used for liability allocation calculations may be verified through the following procedures:
1. Cross-data analysis
2. Independent expert review
3. International data comparison

Article 15
Reporting
Liability allocation calculation results shall be included in the Annual Global External Harm Report.

Article 16
Methodological Development
The liability allocation methodology may be continuously improved through:
1. Use of new data sources
2. International cooperative research
3. Technological advancements
