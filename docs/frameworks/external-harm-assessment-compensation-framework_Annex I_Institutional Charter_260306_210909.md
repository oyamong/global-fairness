Annex I.
Charter of the International External Harm Assessment Mechanism for Global Conflicts
(Global Conflict External Harm Task Force – GCEHTF)

Submitted by: Jungyun Kwon, Citizen of the Republic of Korea and Global Citizen
Date: March 6, 2026 (KST)

Preamble
This Charter is adopted for the purpose of establishing an international cooperative framework to objectively assess and document external harm affecting third States arising from international conflicts.

This Charter is based on the following principles:
• Protection of human life and preservation of human dignity
• Promotion of international cooperation and peace
• Objective assessment of external harm arising from conflicts
• Protection of third States affected by conflicts
The international community recognizes that conflicts may occur within the international system. At the same time, it acknowledges the need to develop a cooperative international mechanism to ensure that external harm inflicted upon third States as a consequence of actions undertaken by parties to a conflict is not disregarded.

Accordingly, this Charter establishes the institutional framework and operational principles of a cooperative mechanism for the assessment of external harm arising from international conflicts.

Article 1
Establishment
Pursuant to a resolution of the United Nations General Assembly, an international cooperative analytical mechanism designated as the Global Conflict External Harm Task Force (GCEHTF) is hereby established.
The Task Force shall function as a technical and cooperative mechanism for assessing external harm affecting third States arising from international conflicts.

Article 2
Purpose
The purposes of the Task Force are:
1. To assess harm affecting third States arising from international conflicts
2. To analyze economic, environmental and supply-chain impacts
3. To assess systemic impacts on global economic and institutional systems
4. To provide data-based analytical inputs for policy consideration
5. To promote international cooperation in addressing conflict-related external harm

Article 3
Scope of Application
This Charter applies to the assessment of external harm arising from conflict-related actions including, but not limited to:
1. Traditional armed conflict
2. Cyber operations affecting digital or economic systems
3. Economic blockades or coercive economic restrictions
4. Interference with international financial systems
5. Restrictions affecting energy supply
6. Disruption of critical supply chains

Article 4
Participating Institutions
The Task Force shall cooperate with relevant international organizations and specialized institutions including:
• United Nations Development Programme (UNDP)
• United Nations Conference on Trade and Development (UNCTAD)
• United Nations Environment Programme (UNEP)
• United Nations Office for the Coordination of Humanitarian Affairs (OCHA)
• International Monetary Fund (IMF)
• Food and Agriculture Organization of the United Nations (FAO)
• World Bank Group
Additional international organizations, research institutions or expert bodies may participate where appropriate.

Article 5
Institutional Independence
The Task Force shall operate as a technical analytical mechanism independent from political influence.
The existing mandates, authorities and institutional independence of participating organizations shall be fully respected.

Article 6
Core Functions
The Task Force shall perform the following functions:
1. Collection of data concerning external harm arising from conflicts
2. Analysis of the scale and characteristics of such harm
3. Assessment of impacts on global markets and economic systems
4. Analytical evaluation of the relative contribution of conflict activities to external harm
5. Preparation and publication of international analytical reports

Article 7
Data Submission
Member States may submit data relating to external harm arising from conflicts.
Submitted data may include information relating to:
1. Economic impacts
2. Supply-chain disruptions
3. Environmental damage
4. Financial system impacts
Data submissions shall not require the attribution of legal responsibility to any specific State.

Article 8
Data Security and Confidentiality
Sensitive information submitted by Member States shall be protected in accordance with the following principles:
1. Restricted access
2. Anonymization where appropriate
3. Removal of sensitive information from publicly released reports

Article 9
Verification
The Task Force may establish an Independent Verification Panel.
The Panel may perform the following functions:
1. Verification of submitted data
2. Comparative analysis with international market and economic datasets
3. Independent analytical review

Article 10
Assessment Model
The external harm assessment model may take into consideration factors including:
1. Initial escalation actions within a conflict
2. Defensive or responsive actions
3. Conflict expansion affecting external systems
4. Participation of multiple actors

Article 11
Reporting
The Task Force shall prepare an Annual Global External Harm Report and submit it to the United Nations General Assembly.

Article 12
Cooperation
The Task Force may cooperate with:
• International organizations
• Research institutions
• Financial institutions
• Other relevant expert bodies

Article 13
Financing
Operational financing of the Task Force may be derived from:
1. International cooperation funds
2. Existing international institutional cooperation programmes
3. Voluntary contributions
Where external harm arising from conflicts is identified, Conflict External Harm Contributions may be considered based on assessment results.

Such contributions may, subject to future international agreement, support:
• Recovery and reconstruction efforts
• Operation of the external harm assessment framework

Article 14
Future Development
This cooperative framework may evolve into a standing international institutional arrangement through future international agreement.

Article 15
Principle of Voluntary Participation
Participation in and utilization of this framework shall be based on the principle of voluntary participation.
No State or institution shall be under any obligation to participate.

Each participating entity retains the freedom to determine the scope and manner of its engagement with the mechanism.

Article 16
Entry into Operation
This Charter shall enter into operation following the adoption of the relevant resolution of the United Nations General Assembly once the following conditions are met:
1. At least five participating international organizations formally confirm their cooperation; and
2. At least fifteen Member States express their intention to participate in data cooperation.
Upon fulfillment of these conditions, the Task Force shall commence its operations.
