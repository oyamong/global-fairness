I. Foundational Resolution
United Nations General Assembly Resolution
Framework for the Assessment, Responsibility Analysis and Compensation of External Harm in International Conflicts

This resolution establishes an international analytical and cooperation framework to assess external harm affecting third States arising from international conflicts and to support recovery, responsibility analysis and international cooperation.

The following annexes constitute the operational and technical framework supporting this resolution.

II. Institutional and Operational Framework
Annex I
Charter of the International External Harm Assessment Mechanism for Global Conflicts
Establishes the institutional mandate, governance principles and organizational structure for the Global Conflict External Harm Task Force (GCEHTF).

Annex II
Operational Regulations of the Global Conflict External Harm Task Force
Defines operational procedures, governance mechanisms, organizational structure and coordination among participating institutions.

III. Analytical and Assessment Framework
Annex III
Responsibility Scoring Framework for External Harm in International Conflicts
Provides the methodology for calculating responsibility scores and responsibility shares among conflict actors.

Annex IV
Damage Assessment Standard for External Harm in International Conflicts
Establishes standardized methodologies for the quantitative assessment of external harm affecting third States.

IV. Data and Reporting Infrastructure
Annex V
Data Standard Protocol for External Harm in International Conflicts
Defines data structures, submission formats and interoperability standards for international harm assessment data.

Annex VI
State Damage Submission Template for External Harm in International Conflicts
Provides the standardized submission format through which States may report external harm resulting from international conflicts.

V. Responsibility Allocation and Compensation Framework
Annex VII
Liability Allocation Calculation Regulation for External Harm in International Conflicts
Defines the methodology for translating responsibility scores into liability shares and financial responsibility allocations.

Annex VIII
Financial Mechanism Regulation for Compensation of External Harm in International Conflicts
Establishes the financial mechanisms through which compensation and recovery support may be implemented.

VI. Market and Financial System Integration
Annex IX
Market Integration Implementation Framework for External Harm in International Conflicts
Provides mechanisms through which responsibility and compensation information may be integrated into global financial and market analysis systems.

VII. Verification and Accountability Mechanism
Annex X
Independent Verification and Audit Regulation for External Harm Assessment and Compensation Mechanisms
Establishes independent verification and audit procedures to ensure reliability, neutrality and transparency of assessments and compensation processes.

VIII. Global Reporting and Monitoring
Annex XI
Annual Reporting Framework for External Harm in International Conflicts
Establishes the annual global reporting system summarizing harm assessment, responsibility analysis, compensation implementation and market impacts.

IX. Institutional Output
The framework may produce the following recurring outputs:
• Global External Harm Assessment Report
• Special External Harm Reports
• Global External Harm Indicators
These outputs support international cooperation, policy research and risk analysis relating to international conflicts.

X. Institutional Implementation Structure
The framework may operate through the following structure:
Global Conflict External Harm Task Force (GCEHTF)
Supported by:
• Independent Verification Panel
• Data Coordination Unit
• Technical Analysis Panels
Participating institutions may include:
• United Nations
• International Monetary Fund (IMF)
• World Bank
• United Nations Development Programme (UNDP)
• Other relevant international organizations and research institutions.

XI. Nature of the Framework
This framework is intended to function as:
• an analytical cooperation mechanism
• a data-based assessment system
• a support mechanism for recovery and compensation discussions
It does not create new binding legal obligations for States unless adopted through further international agreement.

XII. Future Development
The framework may evolve through future international cooperation into:
• a permanent international assessment body
• a global conflict external harm monitoring system
• a coordinated compensation and recovery cooperation mechanism.
