Annex V.
Data Standard Protocol
for External Harm in International Conflicts

Submitted by: Jungyun Kwon, Citizen of the Republic of Korea and Global Citizen
Date: March 6, 2026 (KST)

Article 1
Purpose
The purpose of this Protocol is to establish common data standards governing the collection, submission, exchange and verification of data among Member States and international organizations for the assessment of external harm affecting third States arising from international conflicts.

The objectives of this Protocol are:
1. To ensure international comparability of harm assessment data
2. To improve data quality and reliability
3. To support automated analytical and evaluation models
4. To establish a transparent and consistent data submission framework

Article 2
Guiding Principles
The data standard shall operate in accordance with the following principles:
1. Application of internationally recognized data formats
2. Ensuring interoperability
3. Guaranteeing data integrity
4. Compliance with security and personal data protection requirements

Article 3
Data Categories
This Protocol may include the following categories of data:
1. Economic data
2. Supply-chain data
3. Financial system data
4. Energy and food system data
5. Environmental damage data
6. Humanitarian impact data

Article 4
Data Structure
Data submissions may follow a standardized structure including the following core elements:

Mandatory data fields may include:
1. State identification information
2. Data reporting period
3. Harm category
4. Scale or magnitude of harm
5. Data source information

Article 5
Data Formats
Data submissions may be provided in the following formats:
1. JSON
2. XML
3. CSV
Where appropriate, API-based data transmission may be utilized.

Article 6
Data Identification Systems
The following standardized coding systems may be used for data identification:
1. ISO country codes
2. International currency codes
3. International commodity classification codes
4. International trade classification systems

Article 7
Data Submission Frequency
Data submissions may follow the following reporting cycles:
1. Monthly reporting
2. Quarterly reporting
3. Annual reporting
In emergency situations, special reporting procedures may be requested.

Article 8
Data Verification Procedures
Submitted data may be verified through the following procedures:
1. Automated data validation systems
2. Cross-dataset comparative analysis
3. Independent expert review

Article 9
Data Security
The following security principles shall apply during data transmission and storage:
1. Encrypted data transmission
2. Access control management
3. Data integrity verification

Article 10
Data Quality Management
To ensure data quality, the following procedures may be applied:
1. Detection of data inconsistencies or errors
2. Data correction procedures
3. Confirmation by the submitting institution

Article 11
Data Disclosure Principles
Data may be made available under the following disclosure levels:
1. Public data
2. Restricted-access data
3. Confidential data
Sensitive information may be disclosed only after appropriate anonymization or aggregation procedures.

Article 12
Updates to Data Standards
This data standard may be updated through:
1. Technological developments
2. International cooperative research
3. Analytical findings derived from data use

Article 13
Data Governance and Security
Data collected within this framework may be managed in accordance with the following principles:
1. Protection of submitted data
2. Confidential treatment of sensitive information
3. Restricted data access
4. Compliance with international data protection standards
5. Prevention of misuse of submitted data
Where appropriate, data may be anonymized or aggregated prior to analysis or public release.

Article 14
System Integration
This Protocol may be interoperable with the following international data systems:
1. Global economic data systems
2. International trade data systems
3. Environmental data platforms
4. Humanitarian assistance data systems
