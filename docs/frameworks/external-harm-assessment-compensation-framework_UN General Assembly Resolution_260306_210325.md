United Nations General Assembly
Framework for the Protection and Assessment of External Harm to Third States Arising from International Conflicts
(Citizen Proposal)

Submitted by: Jungyun Kwon, Citizen of the Republic of Korea and Global Citizen
Date: March 6, 2026 (KST)

The General Assembly,

Recalling the purposes and principles of the Charter of the United Nations, in particular the maintenance of international peace and security and the promotion of international cooperation,

Recognizing that contemporary conflicts increasingly produce effects that extend beyond the territories of the parties to the conflict and generate significant economic, environmental, supply chain and systemic disruptions at the global level,

Concerned that States and populations not directly involved in conflicts may nonetheless bear substantial external costs, including economic shocks, trade disruptions, food insecurity and financial market instability,

Noting with concern that developing countries and economically vulnerable regions often experience disproportionate impacts from such external shocks,

Recognizing the reality that armed conflicts and other forms of confrontation may occur within the international system, while emphasizing the need for objective and reasonable mechanisms to assess the external harm caused to third States,

Reaffirming that the protection of human life and the preservation of human dignity require the safeguarding of minimum conditions necessary for sustainable livelihoods,

Recognizing that modern conflicts increasingly take forms beyond traditional military confrontation, including cyber operations, economic disruption, interference with financial systems, and restrictions affecting energy and supply chains, which may also produce substantial external harm,

Emphasizing that preventing, minimizing and objectively assessing such external harm can contribute to international stability, fairness and resilience,

Recognizing that existing international organizations and specialized agencies possess expertise and data capacities that may support the assessment of such impacts,

Adopts the following framework:

Operative Clauses
1.
Affirms the need to establish an international cooperative framework for assessing and documenting external harm to third States arising from international conflicts.

This framework shall not replace existing international cooperation mechanisms nor create new binding legal obligations, but may function as a supporting analytical tool to facilitate a more systematic understanding and analysis of external harm arising from international conflicts.

The framework shall operate in a complementary manner with existing international institutions and cooperation mechanisms.

2.
Recognizes the importance of developing an objective and data-driven assessment mechanism to analyze economic, environmental and systemic impacts on States not party to a conflict.

3.
Decides to establish an international cooperative analytical mechanism under the designation:
Global Conflict External Harm Task Force (GCEHTF).

4.
Invites the participation and cooperation of relevant international organizations and specialized agencies, including but not limited to:
• United Nations Development Programme (UNDP)
• United Nations Conference on Trade and Development (UNCTAD)
• United Nations Environment Programme (UNEP)
• United Nations Office for the Coordination of Humanitarian Affairs (OCHA)
• International Monetary Fund (IMF)
• Food and Agriculture Organization (FAO)
• World Bank Group
and other relevant institutions and expert bodies.

5.
Affirms that the Task Force shall operate in a technical and data-driven manner, respecting the mandates and institutional independence of participating organizations and remaining insulated from political influence.

6.
Requests that the primary functions of the Task Force include:
a. The collection and analysis of data on economic, environmental and supply-chain impacts affecting third States arising from conflicts;

b. The development of objective methodologies for evaluating the scale of external harm;

c. Analytical assessment of the relative contribution of conflict-related actions to such external harm;

d. The preparation of periodic analytical reports for the international community.

7.
Requests the Task Force to develop standardized methodologies and reporting frameworks for the submission and evaluation of external harm data.

8.
Encourages Member States to cooperate voluntarily by submitting relevant data regarding economic and environmental impacts arising from conflicts.

9.
Affirms that reports submitted by Member States shall focus on harm assessment and supporting data and shall not require the attribution of legal responsibility to any specific State.

10.
Requests the Task Force to develop analytical models to examine the relationship between conflict activities and external harm.

11.
Such analytical models may take into consideration factors including:
a. Initial escalation actions within a conflict;

b. Defensive or responsive actions consistent with international humanitarian law;

c. Conflict expansion affecting external systems;

d. Participation of multiple actors.

12.
Adjustments related to defensive or responsive actions may be considered only where the following conditions are satisfied:
a. Compliance with international humanitarian law;
b. Observance of civilian protection principles;
c. Adherence to the principle of proportionality.

13.
Requests that the Task Force submit an annual global analytical report on external harm arising from international conflicts to the General Assembly.

14.
Decides that the Task Force may establish an Independent Verification Panel composed of independent experts.

15.
The Independent Verification Panel may be mandated to:
a. Conduct cross-verification of submitted data;
b. Compare submissions with international market and economic datasets;
c. Utilize third-party data sources or field verification where appropriate.

16.
Recognizes that this framework may operate in alignment with ongoing international cooperation initiatives, including:
a. The Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs);
b. Global cooperation on climate and environmental protection;
c. International efforts to strengthen supply-chain resilience;
d. Conflict impact assessment and recovery assistance programs.

This provision aims to ensure that external harm assessment and potential compensation mechanisms operate in a complementary manner with existing international cooperation frameworks.

17.
Requests the Secretary-General to examine the feasibility of establishing a
Global Conflict External Harm Compensation Fund
based on analytical findings produced by the Task Force.

18.
Decides to review the possibility of developing this framework into a standing international institutional arrangement in the future.

19.
For the purposes of this framework, conflict-related actions may include:
a. Traditional armed conflict;
b. Cyber operations affecting digital infrastructure;
c. Large-scale economic disruption or coercive economic restrictions;
d. Interference with international financial systems;
e. Artificial restrictions on energy supply or flows of critical resources.

20.
Affirms that sensitive information submitted by Member States shall be protected through appropriate confidentiality procedures, including restricted access, anonymization measures, and exclusion of sensitive information from public reports where necessary.

21.
Encourages that Member States providing reliable and timely data may be taken into consideration in future international recovery cooperation and assistance programmes.
