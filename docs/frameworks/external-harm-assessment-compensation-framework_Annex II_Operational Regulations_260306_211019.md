Annex II.
Operational Regulations of the Global Conflict External Harm Task Force
(GCEHTF)

Submitted by: Jungyun Kwon, Citizen of the Republic of Korea and Global Citizen
Date: March 6, 2026 (KST)

Article 1
Purpose
The purpose of these Operational Regulations is to establish the operational procedures and functional arrangements of the Global Conflict External Harm Task Force (GCEHTF), which has been established to assess external harm affecting third States arising from international conflicts.

These Regulations aim to ensure the effective functioning of an international cooperative assessment mechanism while preserving the institutional independence and technical expertise of the Task Force.

Article 2
Scope of Activities
These Regulations shall apply to the following activities:
1. Collection of external harm data
2. Assessment of the scale of external harm
3. Analysis of impacts on global markets and systems
4. Evaluation of relative contributions to external harm
5. Preparation of international analytical reports

Article 3
Organizational Structure
The Task Force shall operate through the following organizational structure:
1. Steering Committee
The Steering Committee shall determine the strategic direction and major operational policies of the Task Force.

It may include:
• Representatives of participating international organizations
• Independent experts
• Technical advisory members

2. Technical Analysis Panels
Technical Analysis Panels shall conduct sector-specific assessments of external harm.
Panels may include expertise in the following areas:
• Economic impact analysis
• Supply-chain analysis
• Environmental impact assessment
• Financial system impact analysis
• Food and energy impact analysis

3. Data Coordination Unit
The Data Coordination Unit shall perform the following functions:
• Data collection
• Data management
• Analytical support for data processing

Article 4
Institutional Cooperation
The Task Force shall cooperate with relevant international organizations including:
• United Nations Development Programme (UNDP)
• United Nations Conference on Trade and Development (UNCTAD)
• United Nations Environment Programme (UNEP)
• United Nations Office for the Coordination of Humanitarian Affairs (OCHA)
• International Monetary Fund (IMF)
• Food and Agriculture Organization of the United Nations (FAO)
• World Bank Group
Additional international organizations, research institutions or expert bodies may participate where appropriate.

Article 5
Principle of Independence
The Task Force shall function as a technical analytical mechanism independent from political influence.
The existing mandates, authorities and institutional independence of participating organizations shall be fully respected.

Article 6
Decision-Making
Decisions of the Steering Committee shall, to the extent possible, be made by consensus.
Where consensus cannot be achieved, decisions may be adopted by a simple majority of participating institutions.
For analytical outcomes that may have significant implications for global economic or financial systems, consultation with relevant international financial and development institutions is encouraged.

Article 7
Data Submission
Member States and cooperating institutions may submit data concerning external harm arising from international conflicts.

Submitted data may include information related to:
1. Economic damage
2. Supply-chain impacts
3. Environmental harm
4. Financial system impacts
5. Food and energy impacts

Article 8
Data Protection
Sensitive data shall be protected in accordance with the following principles:
1. Restricted access
2. Anonymization where appropriate
3. Secure storage

Article 9
Verification Procedures
The Task Force may establish an Independent Verification Panel in order to ensure the reliability of submitted data.
Verification procedures may include:
1. Cross-verification of submitted data
2. Comparison with international market and economic datasets
3. Independent analytical review

Article 10
Assessment Methodology
The Task Force shall develop analytical models for assessing external harm, taking into consideration factors such as:
1. Initial escalation actions within a conflict
2. Conflict expansion
3. Impacts on global markets and systems
4. Participation of multiple actors

Article 11
Reporting
The Task Force shall publish the following reports:
1. Annual Global External Harm Report
2. Special analytical reports
3. Emergency impact assessment reports
Where feasible, reports may include:
• Analysis of the scale of harm
• Assessment of external systemic impacts
• Mitigation recommendations

Article 12
Cooperation
The Task Force may cooperate with:
• International organizations
• Research institutions
• Financial institutions
• Data and analytical organizations

Article 13
Transparency
To the extent possible, the Task Force shall publish assessment results in order to enhance international understanding of the external impacts of conflicts.
Public outputs may include:
1. Official technical reports
2. Policy summary reports
3. Plain Language Summaries
Sensitive information shall remain protected.

Article 14
Financing
Operational financing of the Task Force may be derived from:
1. International cooperation funds
2. Voluntary contributions
3. Conflict External Harm Contributions

Article 15
Amendment of Regulations
These Regulations may be amended by consensus of the Steering Committee.

Article 16
Entry into Force
These Operational Regulations shall enter into force upon the establishment of the Task Force.
