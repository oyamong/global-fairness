Annex VI.
State Damage Submission Template
for External Harm in International Conflicts

Submitted by: Jungyun Kwon, Citizen of the Republic of Korea and Global Citizen
Date: March 6, 2026 (KST)

Article 1
Purpose
This Template serves as the official submission document for reporting external harm affecting third States arising from international conflicts in a standardized format.

The objectives of this template are:
1. To ensure international comparability of damage data
2. To collect data necessary for objective harm assessment
3. To support responsibility scoring and harm allocation analysis
4. To ensure protection of affected States and data security

Article 2
Submitting State Information
1. Required Fields
The following information may be included in the submission:
• Name of the State
• ISO country code
• Reporting institution
• Reporting department or responsible unit
• Official communication channel

2. State-Level Responsibility Principle
Submissions shall be made under the principle of State-level responsibility.
The names, personal contact details, or individual email addresses of specific officials shall not be included in the submission template.

3. Official Communication
All official correspondence regarding submitted materials shall be conducted through the official communication channel of the reporting institution.

Article 3
Conflict Information
The following information may be provided regarding the conflict:
• Name or designation of the conflict
• Geographic location of the conflict
• Date of conflict outbreak
• Date when external impacts began
• Reporting period

Article 4
Selection of Harm Categories
The submitting State may select the relevant harm categories:
☐ Economic harm
☐ Supply-chain harm
☐ Financial system harm
☐ Environmental harm
☐ Energy and food harm
☐ Humanitarian harm

Article 5
Economic Harm Reporting
Fields may include:
• Estimated scale of harm
• Data sources
• Reduction in GDP
• Decline in trade
• Decline in industrial production
• Decline in investment

Article 6
Supply-Chain Harm Reporting
Fields may include:
• Description of harm
• Data sources
• Logistics delays
• Increased transportation costs
• Disruptions in critical component supply
• Production interruptions

Article 7
Financial System Harm Reporting
Fields may include:
• Description of harm
• Data sources
• Financial market volatility
• Capital outflows
• Currency fluctuations
• Financial system instability

Article 8
Energy and Food Harm Reporting
Fields may include:
• Description of harm
• Data sources
• Increase in energy prices
• Disruptions in energy supply
• Increase in food prices
• Reduction in food supply

Article 9
Environmental Harm Reporting
Fields may include:
• Description of harm
• Data sources
• Ecosystem damage
• Spread of pollution
• Loss of natural resources
• Environmental restoration costs

Article 10
Humanitarian Harm Reporting
Fields may include:
• Estimated scale of harm
• Data sources
• Civilian casualties
• Impact on medical systems
• Population displacement
• Refugee movements

Article 11
Damage Period
The following information may be provided:
• Date when damage began
• Duration of impact
• Estimated recovery period

Article 12
Data Sources
The submitting State may indicate the primary sources of the submitted data:
☐ National statistical institutions
☐ Central bank data
☐ Government ministries or agencies
☐ International organization datasets
☐ Private research institutions
☐ Other relevant sources

Article 13
Additional Explanatory Notes
The submitting State may provide additional explanatory information relating to damage assessment.
This section allows free-text descriptions where necessary.

Article 14
Supporting Documentation
The following supporting materials may be attached:
☐ Economic statistical reports
☐ Supply-chain analysis reports
☐ Environmental impact assessments
☐ Satellite imagery data
☐ Humanitarian impact reports
☐ Other relevant documentation

Article 15
Data Security and Anonymous Submission
Affected States may submit data using one of the following submission modes:
1. Standard Submission(State identification information included)
2. Restricted Disclosure Submission(State identification information accessible only to authorized verification bodies)
3. Anonymous Submission
(State identification information excluded from public reports)

Data submitted anonymously shall be used solely for aggregated analytical purposes and shall not disclose the identity of the submitting State in publicly released reports.

Article 16
Protected Data Submission Channels
Damage data may be submitted through protected channels including:
1. Encrypted data submission portals
2. Secure international institutional data channels
3. Submission through the Independent Verification Panel
Submitted data shall be accessible only to authorized verification bodies.

Article 17
Principle of Non-Retaliation
Submission of damage data shall be considered an act of international cooperation.
No State shall undertake retaliatory measures against a submitting State on the basis of its participation in data submission.

Such prohibited retaliatory actions may include:
1. Economic retaliation
2. Diplomatic pressure
3. Political sanctions

Where retaliatory actions are identified in relation to damage data submission, such actions may be considered an aggravating factor in responsibility scoring procedures.
Responsibility scores or responsibility shares may be adjusted accordingly under the Responsibility Scoring Framework.

Article 18
Confirmation of Data Reliability
The submitted data shall be confirmed by an official institution of the submitting State to the best of its knowledge and available information.

Data reliability confirmation shall be conducted under a State-level certification process.
Fields may include:
• Submitting State
• Reporting institution
• Reporting period
• Submission date
Personal identifying information shall not be included in public reports.
Where necessary, verification of data may be conducted by authorized independent verification bodies under restricted access procedures.
